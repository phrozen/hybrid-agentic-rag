## Race Categories

### Noble Species

The Noble Species are characterized by their civilization-building capabilities and tendency toward organized societies.

- **[Humans](humans.md)**: The most adaptable and ambitious race, found across all environments
- **[Elves](elves.md)**: Long-lived masters of magic with High, Wood, and Dark subtypes
- **[Dwarves](dwarves.md)**: Stout craftsmen with Mountain, Hill, and Deep variants

### Wild Species

Wild Species maintain strong cultural traditions while adapting to various environments.

- **[Orcs](orcs.md)**: Physically imposing warriors with proud martial traditions
- **[Halflings](halflings.md)**: Small-statured but resilient folk with Lightfoot and Stout variants
- **[Gnomes](gnomes.md)**: Inventive intellectuals with Rock, Forest, and Deep subtypes

### Exotic Species

Exotic Species possess extraordinary heritage that sets them apart from standard humanoid races.

- **[Dragonborn](dragonborn.md)**: Humanoids with draconic ancestry, scales, and breath weapons
- **[Tieflings](tieflings.md)**: Humans with fiendish blood, marked by horns and unusual features
- **[Aasimar](aasimar.md)**: Humans with celestial heritage, characterized by radiant features

### Beast Species

Beast Species combine humanoid intelligence with animalistic characteristics.

- **[Lizardfolk](lizardfolk.md)**: Reptilian humanoids with natural armor and powerful instincts
- **[Catfolk](catfolk.md)**: Feline humanoids with grace, agility, and climbing abilities
- **[Minotaurs](minotaurs.md)**: Bull-headed humanoids with natural strength and directional sense

### Element-Touched

Element-Touched races manifest direct connections to elemental forces.

- **[Genasi](genasi.md)**: Humanoids influenced by elemental planes (Fire, Water, Earth, Air)

### Shapeshifters

Shapeshifters possess the ability to alter their physical form.

- **[Changelings](changelings.md)**: Natural shapeshifters able to assume multiple humanoid forms
- **[Lycanthropes](lycanthropes.md)**: Humans cursed or blessed with animal transformation (Werewolves, Werebears, Weretigers)

## Inter-Race Relations

### Generally Positive Relations
- Humans maintain diplomatic and trade relationships with most races
- Halflings and gnomes enjoy particularly friendly relations
- Dwarves and humans have strong trading partnerships
- Elves maintain formal but stable relations with most civilized races

### Complex Relations
- Elves and dwarves: Respect mixed with historical tension over resource utilization
- Aasimar and tieflings: Working to overcome traditional prejudices
- Orcs and elves: Historical territorial disputes, though trade relationships developing
- Changelings: Valued for diplomacy but sometimes viewed with suspicion

### Regional Variations
- Relations vary significantly by geographic region and local history
- Urban centers generally more accepting of diverse races
- Frontier regions may have different dynamics based on common threats
- Historical conflicts continue to influence modern diplomatic relations

## Biological Notes

### Hybridization

Some races can produce viable offspring with other species:

- **Humans**: Most compatible, can produce half-elves, half-orcs, and other mixed heritage
- **Elves**: Can produce half-elves with humans
- **Other races**: Generally maintain genetic stability with limited hybridization

### Lifespan Categories

- **Short-lived** (60-100 years): Humans, lizardfolk, orcs, minotaurs
- **Medium-lived** (100-200 years): Halflings, catfolk, changelings, tieflings, aasimar
- **Long-lived** (200-400 years): Dwarves, gnomes, dragonborn, genasi
- **Ancient** (500+ years): Elves

## Geographic Distribution

### Most Widespread
- **Humans**: Found on all continents in diverse environments
- **Halflings**: Integrated into most multi-species settlements
- **Tieflings**: Urban centers across multiple regions

### Regionally Concentrated
- **Elves**: Western Reaches, ancient forests
- **Dwarves**: Mountain ranges, underground holds
- **Dragonborn**: Southern Expanse
- **Lizardfolk**: Wetlands, coastal regions
- **Minotaurs**: Maritime regions, mountain strongholds

### Dispersed Populations
- **Aasimar**: Networks rather than concentrated settlements
- **Changelings**: Multiple identities across different communities
- **Gnomes**: Specialized communities in varied environments
- **Genasi**: Locations of strong elemental power
