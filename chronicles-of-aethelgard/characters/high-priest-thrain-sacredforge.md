# High Priest Thrain Sacredforge

## Overview

High Priest Thrain Sacredforge leads the **Order of the Sacred Anvil** from **Stonehold**'s **Temple of the Sacred Anvil**, serving as **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s spiritual advisor and the foremost authority on dwarven religious matters. Sacredforge maintains **Moradin**'s traditions while supporting **King Thorin**'s modernization efforts, balancing traditionalist dwarves with surface trade expansion. He oversees the **Ironhand Guild**'s runemagic blessings and represents dwarven faith in **Order of the Silver Dawn** interfaith dialogues.

## Background and Origins

Born in **Stonehold** in 298 AS to the Sacredforge clan, Thrain showed early aptitude for both metallurgy and divine magic. His family has served **Moradin** for twelve generations, with each firstborn son entering the priesthood.

Thrain trained at the **Temple of the Sacred Anvil** from age 30, mastering dwarven theology, runemagic blessing rituals, and temple administration. He was ordained at 80 years and rose through the priesthood ranks through diplomatic skill and spiritual devotion.

## Role and Significance

As High Priest, Thrain serves three critical functions:

### Spiritual Leader

Thrain interprets **Moradin**'s will for the dwarven people, conducting major religious ceremonies, blessing new mines and forges, and ordaining priests. His annual "Forge Blessing" ritual sanctifies **Stonehold**'s **Royal Armory** output for the coming year.

### Royal Advisor

**King Thorin Stoneforge** consults Thrain on matters of faith, tradition, and moral governance. Thrain supported the king's decision to expand surface trade, arguing that **Moradin** blesses dwarven craftsmanship being shared with worthy allies.

### Interfaith Diplomat

Thrain represents dwarven religion in the **Order of the Silver Dawn**, building relationships with **Aasimar** clerics, **Elf** druids, and **Human** priests. His ecumenical approach has reduced religious tensions across Aethelgard.

## Relationships and Allies

### King Thorin Stoneforge

Thrain and **King Thorin** have collaborated for 150 years, developing deep mutual respect. The king relies on Thrain to maintain traditionalist support while pursuing modernization. Thrain views the king as **Moradin**'s chosen instrument for dwarven advancement.

### High Priestess Celestia

Thrain maintains close friendship with **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s spiritual advisor, an **Elf** priestess of **Corellon**. Their interfaith dialogues have produced joint statements on creation, craftsmanship, and divine purpose.

### Ironhand Guild Masters

Thrain oversees the **Ironhand Guild**'s religious activities, blessing runemagic inscriptions and ensuring craftsmen maintain spiritual discipline. Guild members view his blessings as essential for creating masterwork items.

### Archbishop Radiant Dawn

**Aasimar** leader of the **Order of the Silver Dawn** collaborates with Thrain on humanitarian projects. Their partnership demonstrates dwarven-celestial cooperation to skeptical traditionalists.

## Abilities and Traits

Thrain possesses distinctive capabilities:

### Divine Magic

Thrain channels **Moradin**'s power for healing, protection, and crafting blessings. His "Sacred Forge" ritual temporarily enchants weapons with holy power against evil foes.

### Runemagic Blessing

Only Thrain can perform the highest-level runemagic blessings, inscribing divine power into **Ironhand Guild** creations. His blessed items are legendary for durability and potency.

### Diplomatic Wisdom

Thrain navigates complex theological and political disputes with patience and insight. His "Middle Path" philosophy finds compromises satisfying both traditionalists and reformers.

### Spiritual Perception

Thrain discerns divine will through meditation and omens. His prophecies, though cryptic, have guided **Stonehold** through crises.

## Notable Deeds

- **Blessed the Reconstruction** (451 AS): Sanctified **Stonehold**'s contribution to post-war rebuilding
- **Negotiated Temple Accords** (448 AS): Secured dwarven religious rights in **Silverhaven** and **Thaldor**
- **Consecrated Ironhand Guild** (420 AS): Elevated craftsmen guild to sacred status
- **Mediated Succession Crisis** (405 AS): Prevented civil war over **King Thorin**'s heir
- **Published "Moradin's Wisdom"** (390 AS): Theological text on faith and craftsmanship
- **Blessed Silverhaven Gates** (385 AS): Collaborated with **Grimjaw Ironhand** on magical wards

## Mentions In Lore

Thrain's influence extends across Aethelgard:

- The **Sacred Anvil** in **Stonehold**'s temple bears his personal runes
- **Order of the Silver Dawn** includes his teachings in interfaith curriculum
- **Ironhand Guild** members swear oaths before his portrait
- **King Thorin** consults him before major decisions
- **Archbishop Radiant Dawn** cites Thrain's ecumenical work as model for cooperation

Thrain is expected to serve as High Priest for another century, continuing his work bridging tradition and progress.
