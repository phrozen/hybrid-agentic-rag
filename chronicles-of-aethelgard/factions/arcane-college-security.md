# The Arcane College Security Force

## Overview

The Arcane College Security Force is **Thaldor**'s elite magical law enforcement organization, responsible for protecting the college, investigating magical crimes, and apprehending rogue mages. The Force employs battle-mages, diviners, and magical creatures in their duties, maintaining a reputation for effectiveness and discretion. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** commands the Force directly, and they cooperate with **Queen Elara**'s guards and **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s warriors on cross-jurisdictional cases.

## Founding and History

The Arcane College Security Force was established in the turbulent years following the **War of Shattered Skies**, when the College leadership recognized that their institution's growing influence necessitated a dedicated enforcement arm capable of handling magical threats that conventional city watches were wholly unequipped to address. Prior to the Force's founding, magical crimes in **Thaldor** were investigated by ad hoc committees of faculty volunteers whose academic duties left them ill-prepared for the dangers of confronting rogue practitioners.

The Force's creation was not without controversy. Many within the **Council of Archmages** feared that an armed magical force under College control would be perceived as a threat to civil authority. **Queen Elara**'s government expressed similar concerns, worrying that the College might use such a force to operate outside the law. The compromise that established the Force placed it under the joint authority of the Archchancellor and the crown, with an oversight commission that includes representatives from **Stonehold**, **Silvermoon**, and the **Southern Expanse**.

Over the decades since its founding, the Force has evolved from a small campus police unit into a sophisticated intelligence and law enforcement organization with jurisdiction over magical crimes throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**. Their purview expanded significantly during the **Plague of Shadows**, when conventional law enforcement collapsed in several cities and the Force became the only effective response to necromantic threats. Queen Elara subsequently formalized their expanded role through the Magical Crimes Act, which remains the legal foundation for their operations.

## Organization and Structure

The Force is organized into four divisions, each specializing in a different aspect of magical law enforcement. The Patrol Division maintains a visible presence around the Arcane College campus and in key areas of **Thaldor**, responding to immediate magical incidents and providing a deterrent against casual magical crime. The Investigation Division handles complex cases requiring extended research and surveillance, employing diviners and forensic magical specialists to reconstruct crime scenes and identify perpetrators.

The Tactical Division comprises the Force's combat-ready battle-mages, trained for direct confrontation with dangerous magical practitioners. These operatives undergo rigorous physical and magical conditioning, and their distinctive midnight-blue uniforms mark them as the most formidable law enforcement agents in the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The Intelligence Division operates discreetly, maintaining networks of informants throughout Aethelgard and monitoring magical black markets for restricted materials and knowledge.

Each division is commanded by a Deputy Commander holding the rank of Senior Archmage, and all four report directly to the Force Commander, currently Senior Archmage Kellen Shadowend, who in turn answers to **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**. This chain of command ensures rapid response to emergencies while preserving civilian oversight through the Archchancellor's accountability to the **Council of Archmages**.

## Headquarters and Bases

The Force's primary headquarters occupies a heavily warded complex adjacent to the Arcane College in **Thaldor**, connected to the main campus by a secure corridor lined with detection enchantments. The complex includes holding cells specifically designed for magical prisoners, complete with abjuration wards that suppress spellcasting within their walls, and interrogation rooms equipped with truth-detection enchantments that operate under strict judicial oversight.

Field offices operate in **Silverhaven**, **Stonehold**, **Crystal Bay**, and **Drakkenheim**, each staffed by a small team of investigators and equipped with teleportation circles linking them to the main headquarters. These offices coordinate with local law enforcement and serve as staging points for operations requiring cross-jurisdictional cooperation. The Force also maintains a secure training facility in a remote valley within the **Stormpeak Mountains**, where tactical trainees can practice combat magic without risk to civilians.

The most sensitive installations are the Force's containment facilities, located in undisclosed locations protected by layered wards and misdirection enchantments. These facilities house the most dangerous magical criminals and artefacts, including several entities too powerful for conventional imprisonment and requiring constant magical suppression by teams of abjuration specialists.

## Goals and Philosophy

The Force's mission statement encompasses three core objectives: protecting the Arcane College and its community from magical threats, investigating and prosecuting magical crimes throughout Aethelgard, and preventing the proliferation of dangerous magical knowledge and materials. These objectives reflect a philosophy that views magical power as both a tremendous asset and a grave responsibility requiring active stewardship.

The Force operates under the principle of proportional response, matching the level of magical force employed against a threat with equivalent but not excessive countermeasures. This doctrine was developed in response to early incidents where overzealous operatives caused significant collateral damage while pursuing minor offenders. Training now emphasizes containment and de-escalation whenever possible, with lethal force authorized only when innocent lives are in immediate danger.

The Force maintains that magical talent does not confer the right to operate outside the law, a position that places them in frequent conflict with rogue practitioners who view their abilities as natural entitlements. This philosophical stance extends to their regulation of magical research: the Force monitors experiments involving **necromancy**, **elemental magic** summoning, and other high-risk disciplines, ensuring that practitioners follow established safety protocols and obtain proper authorization.

## Membership and Recruitment

The Force recruits exclusively from graduates of the Arcane College who have demonstrated both magical competence and moral character, with additional candidates drawn from the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s martial branch and other law enforcement agencies with magical capabilities. New recruits undergo an eighteen-month training program covering magical combat, investigation techniques, legal procedures, and ethical constraints.

Current Force strength stands at approximately three hundred active operatives, with the Tactical Division comprising the largest contingent at one hundred twenty battle-mages. The Investigation Division maintains eighty specialists, the Patrol Division fields sixty officers, and the Intelligence Division operates with forty analysts and field agents. An additional hundred support staff handle logistics, research, and administrative functions.

Promotion within the Force is based on demonstrated performance, with advancement through the ranks requiring successful completion of increasingly challenging assignments and evaluation by a review board of senior operatives and independent **Council of Archmages** observers. This meritocratic approach has produced a highly professional organization, though critics note that the Force's demographics skew heavily toward human and elven operatives, with dwarven, halfling, and other species significantly underrepresented.

## Allies and Enemies

The Force's most important collaboration is with **Queen Elara**'s crown guard, with which it shares intelligence, coordinates jurisdiction, and conducts joint operations under the Magical Crimes Act framework. The partnership with **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s military operates similarly, with the Force providing magical expertise during dwarf-related operations while receiving dwarven military support when confronting threats that exceed purely magical response capabilities. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s **Forest Wardens** provide wilderness intelligence that has proven invaluable for tracking fugitives who flee into the **Whispering Woods**.

The Force's primary adversaries are organized magical criminal networks, particularly the **Shadowfen Collective**, which runs sophisticated smuggling operations in restricted magical materials and has attempted to infiltrate the College on multiple occasions. Individual rogue mages who reject licensing and oversight constitute a persistent threat, with the Force currently tracking an estimated forty unlicensed practitioners across Aethelgard. **Necromancy** cults operating in secret pose the most dangerous category of threat, as their activities often escalate rapidly from illegal research to active threats against civilian populations.

## Notable Members

Force Commander Kellen Shadowend, a veteran of the **Pirate Wars** and the **Plague of Shadows** response, has led the Force for fifteen years with a reputation for both tactical brilliance and unwavering ethical standards. Deputy Commander Vera Stormward commands the Tactical Division and pioneered the proportional response doctrine that now defines Force engagement protocols. Chief Investigator Aldric Truthseeker leads the Investigation Division and has solved more magical crimes than any other operative in Force history, earning him both respect and numerous enemies among the criminal underworld.

The Intelligence Division operates under Deputy Commander Mira Whisperwind, an elven diviner whose information networks extend throughout Aethelgard's magical communities. Her identity is closely guarded, and she rarely appears in public, communicating with her subordinates through magically encrypted channels. Patrol Division Commander Boram Steadfast, a dwarven abjurer and one of the few non-human senior officers, has made increasing species diversity within the Force a personal priority.

## Notable Operations

Operation Shattered Staff in 430 AS represented the Force's largest single action, a coordinated raid across four cities that dismantled a necromantic ring responsible for the disappearances of over thirty civilians. The operation required simultaneous tactical strikes in **Thaldor**, **Silverhaven**, **Crystal Bay**, and a remote compound in the **Western Reaches**, demonstrating the Force's ability to conduct large-scale multi-jurisdictional operations. Twenty-three suspects were apprehended and nineteen victims were rescued alive, making it the most successful anti-necromancy operation in Aethelgard's history.

The ongoing Codex Vigil operation monitors the black market trade in restricted magical texts and materials, with Intelligence Division agents embedded in trading networks throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**. This operation has resulted in the seizure of hundreds of prohibited items and the identification of several previously unknown smuggling routes used by the **Shadowfen Collective** and other criminal organizations.

The Force's continuous campus security deployment around the Arcane College represents their most visible day-to-day operation. Patrol Division officers maintain a twenty-four-hour presence at all College entry points, monitor the campus ward network, and respond to magical incidents ranging from student mishaps in practice chambers to genuine security threats. This constant vigilance has prevented multiple assassination attempts on **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** and other senior College officials over the years.
