# The Chandlers' Guild

## Overview

The Chandlers' Guild produces candles, soaps, and lighting products from tallow, beeswax, and magical luminescent materials. Guild members supply **Silverhaven**'s streets, **Stonehold**'s deep halls, and **Crystal Bay**'s lighthouse. The Guild trains chandlers and maintains quality standards for lighting products.

## Founding and History

The Chandlers' Guild was founded in 342 AS, emerging from the practical necessity of illuminating Aethelgard's growing cities, underground spaces, and maritime hazards. Before organized chandlery, inconsistent candle and soap quality created fire hazards and hygiene problems across the continent.

### Founding Period (342-375 AS)

**Urban Lighting Crisis**: As **Silverhaven** expanded, unregulated candle makers produced dangerous products that caused fires and failed prematurely. Human chandler **Brightwick Chandler** organized the first quality standards after a devastating fire killed 30 people in a poorly lit tenement.

**Underground Demand**: **Stonehold** required reliable, long-burning illumination for deep dwarven halls. **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s predecessor sponsored the guild's dwarven chapter, recognizing that proper lighting saved mining lives. The deep-hall candles the guild developed remain standard issue.

**Maritime Safety**: **Crystal Bay**'s lighthouse required consistent, bright-burning candles visible for miles. Early guild chandlers developed specialized maritime tallow blends that burned brighter and longer than any previous formulation. **Mayor Ironscale**'s predecessor mandated guild candles for the lighthouse.

### Modern Era (375-450 AS)

**Magical Innovation**: Collaboration with the **Arcane College of Thaldor** produced luminescent candles that burn with magical light rather than flame. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provided enchantment techniques, while guild chandlers developed stable formulations. These candles now illuminate **Stonehold**'s deepest tunnels safely.

**Soap Production Expansion**: The guild expanded from pure lighting into soap production, recognizing that chandlery byproducts made excellent soap bases. **Queen Elara** mandated guild-quality soap for **Silverhaven**'s public health, drastically improving urban hygiene.

**Industrial Scale**: Population growth across **Eastern Kingdoms**, dwarven holds, and coastal cities required massive production increases. The guild built large-scale manufactories in **Silverhaven**, **Stonehold**, and **Crystal Bay**, employing hundreds of workers.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guild Master**: Supreme authority, currently the human master chandler **Brightwick Lightbourne**, whose family has led the guild for three generations. Guild Master Lightbourne coordinates with **Queen Elara** on urban lighting contracts and **King Thorin Stoneforge** on underground illumination.

**Master Chandlers**: Senior members qualified to develop new formulations and oversee production. Masters specialize in candle-making, soap production, or magical luminescence.

**Journeyman Chandlers**: Qualified chandlers authorized to produce standard guild products. Journeymen operate workshops and retail shops across Aethelgard.

**Apprentice Chandlers**: Students learning chandlery arts. Apprentices train for 4-6 years, mastering tallow rendering, wax formulation, wick construction, and soap chemistry.

### Production Facilities

**Silverhaven Manufactory**: Largest production facility, supplying **Eastern Kingdoms** with candles and soap. **Queen Elara**'s street lighting contract occupies half the manufactory's capacity. The facility also produces the guild's premium beeswax religious candles.

**Stonehold Deep-Hall Works**: Specialized underground facility producing long-burning tunnel candles and magical luminescent products. **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds this facility directly, as proper illumination saves dwarven mining lives.

**Crystal Bay Lighthouse Workshop**: Produces maritime candles and signal lights for **Crystal Bay**'s lighthouse and **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s fleet. These specialized products must withstand salt air and burn consistently for days.

## Headquarters and Bases

The guild headquarters stands in **Silverhaven**'s artisan district, adjacent to the main manufactory. The building features the longest continuously burning guild candle—a beeswax masterpiece that has burned for 78 years. Regional production facilities operate in **Stonehold** (underground products), **Crystal Bay** (maritime products), and **Thaldor** (magical luminescent products near the **Arcane College**).

## Goals and Philosophy

### Illumination for All

**Affordable Lighting**: The guild believes everyone deserves reliable, safe illumination. **Queen Elara** subsidizes street lighting for poor districts, and the guild offers candles at cost for public institutions. **Stonehold**'s deep-hall candles are distributed to miners free of charge.

**Safety Standards**: Every guild candle must meet burn-time, soot-production, and flame-stability standards. Substandard products are destroyed, not sold at discount. **King Thorin Stoneforge** enforces these standards in dwarven territories with legal penalties.

**Innovation**: The guild constantly improves formulations—longer burning, brighter light, less soot. Collaboration with the **Arcane College** produced magical luminescent candles that produce no flame, eliminating fire hazard entirely.

### Hygiene and Health

**Soap Quality**: The guild's soap production improves public health across Aethelgard. **Queen Elara** credits guild soap with reducing disease in **Silverhaven**'s crowded districts. The guild produces medicinal soaps infused with **Poppy Greenfields**' herbal additives.

**Accessible Products**: Like lighting, the guild believes hygiene products should be affordable. **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** workers receive discounted soap, and **Porters' and Teamsters' Guild** members use guild soap at way stations.

**Medical Collaboration**: The guild works with the **Healers' Guild** on antiseptic soap formulations for hospitals and clinics. **Shaman Vessska** contributed **Lizardfolk** herbal knowledge to medicinal soap recipes.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Patience and Precision**: Chandlery requires careful measurement, temperature control, and quality testing. The guild values steady hands and meticulous attention to detail.

**Physical Tolerance**: Chandlers work with hot tallow, strong fragrances, and chemical additives. Candidates must tolerate these conditions without adverse reactions.

**Chemical Aptitude**: Basic understanding of materials and their interactions helps candidates master formulations faster. **Gnomes** and **dwarves** often excel in this area.

### Training Programs

**Foundation Training**: Two years learning basic candle-making—tallow rendering, wax formulation, wick construction, and molding techniques. Apprentices produce simple dipped candles under journeyman supervision.

**Soap Chemistry**: Two years learning soap production—alkali reactions, fat saponification, fragrance addition, and medicinal formulations. **Poppy Greenfields** assists with herbal additive instruction.

**Specialization**: Journeyman candidates choose candle-making, soap production, or magical luminescence. The **Arcane College of Thaldor** provides enchantment training for magical luminescence specialists.

### Membership Demographics

**Humans** (45%): The majority of chandlers, producing standard candles and soaps for **Eastern Kingdoms** markets. Human chandlers dominate urban production facilities.

**Dwarves** (25%): Underground and industrial chandlery specialists. Dwarves developed deep-hall candles and mining-safety formulations. **King Thorin Stoneforge** sponsors dwarven chandlers.

**Gnomes** (15%): Chemical specialists producing precise formulations and magical luminescent products. **Gnomish** chandlers collaborate with the **Arcane College** on innovation.

**Other Species** (15%): **Elves** producing premium beeswax candles, **halflings** with herbal soap additives, and **Catfolk** with fragrant specialty products for **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s markets.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**Queen Elara**: Primary patron for urban lighting contracts and public health mandates. **Queen Elara** requires guild candles for **Silverhaven** street lighting and guild soap for public hygiene.

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Dwarven patron funding underground illumination. **Stonehold**'s deep-hall candles save mining lives, and **King Thorin Stoneforge** considers them essential infrastructure.

**Arcane College of Thaldor**: Research partner on magical luminescent products. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provides enchantment techniques; the guild provides stable chemical formulations.

**Poppy Greenfields**: **Halfling** agricultural innovator providing herbal additives for medicinal soaps. **Poppy Greenfields** and the guild collaborate on herbal candle fragrances.

### Cautious Partners

**Admiral Hornbreaker**: Naval partner requiring specialized maritime candles that withstand salt air and rough seas. **Admiral Hornbreaker** demands higher specifications than standard products, requiring custom formulations.

**Healers' Guild**: Medical partner on antiseptic soaps. The **Healers' Guild** provides medical requirements; the chandlers provide manufacturing. Occasional disagreements arise over formulation specifics.

**Brewers' Guild**: Competing for tallow and fat byproducts. Both guilds need animal fats—brewers for some specialty products, chandlers for nearly everything. **Queen Elara** mediates resource disputes.

### Ideological Opponents

**Unlicensed Chandlers**: Producers ignoring quality standards and selling dangerous candles. **Queen Elara** prosecutes fire hazards; the guild reports violations.

**Shadowfen Alchemists**: **Malakor Shadowdancer**'s agents seek magical luminescent formulas for shadow operations. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** purchase requests and guards enchantment formulas carefully.

## Notable Members

Human master chandler whose family has led the guild for three generations. **Lightbourne** pioneered magical luminescent candles through collaboration with the **Arcane College**, transforming underground illumination across **Stonehold**.

**Gnome** luminescence specialist developing the next generation of magical lighting products. Master Glowstone's collaboration with the **Arcane College** produced candles that adjust brightness based on ambient light.

### Journeyman Tallowwick

Human chandler managing the **Crystal Bay Lighthouse Workshop**. Journeyman Tallowwick's maritime candles burn reliably for 72 hours in salt spray, making **Crystal Bay**'s lighthouse one of Aethelgard's most reliable.

**Queen Elara** contracts the guild to illuminate **Silverhaven**'s streets every night. The program uses 15,000 candles nightly, requiring constant production and distribution. The guild employs dedicated street-lighting crews who replace candles each evening.

### Stonehold Deep-Hall Illumination (Ongoing)

**King Thorin Stoneforge** funds the production and distribution of deep-hall candles and magical luminescent products throughout **Stonehold**'s mines and tunnels. The program has reduced mining accidents by 40% since its inception.

### Crystal Bay Lighthouse Supply (Ongoing)

**Mayor Ironscale** contracts the guild to supply **Crystal Bay**'s lighthouse. The lighthouse requires specialized maritime candles that burn for 72+ hours in salt spray. **Admiral Hornbreaker** also uses guild signal lights for naval operations.

### Medicinal Soap Program (435-450 AS)

Collaboration with the **Healers' Guild** and **Poppy Greenfields** to produce antiseptic soaps for hospitals, clinics, and public health. **Shaman Vessska** contributed **Lizardfolk** herbal knowledge. **Queen Elara** mandated guild soap in all **Silverhaven** public facilities, significantly reducing disease transmission.
