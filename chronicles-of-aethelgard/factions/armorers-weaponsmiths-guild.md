# The Armorers' and Weaponsmiths' Guild

## Overview

The Armorers' and Weaponsmiths' Guild produces weapons and armor across Aethelgard, with **Stonehold**'s **Royal Armory**, **Silverhaven**'s military suppliers, and **Drakkenheim**'s **Dragonborn** craftsmen all represented. The Guild maintains quality standards, trains smiths, and supplies military forces under **Queen Elara**, **King Thorin Stoneforge**, and **Lady Zara Goldscale**.

## Founding and History

The Armorers' and Weaponsmiths' Guild traces its origins to the aftermath of the **War of Shattered Skies**, when the urgent need for standardized military equipment became undeniable. Founded in 322 AS, the guild emerged from wartime forge cooperatives that had proven the value of coordinated production and quality enforcement.

### Founding Period (322-360 AS)

**Wartime Origins**: During the **War of Shattered Skies**, individual smiths produced weapons and armor of wildly varying quality. Military leaders, including early **General Ironfist** predecessors, demanded standardization. The surviving forge masters formed the guild to prevent future equipment failures.

**Royal Endorsement**: **King Durin Stoneforge** granted the first dwarven charter, establishing the **Stonehold Royal Armory** as the guild's founding chapter. **King Aldric the Unifier** followed with the human charter, and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s ancestors provided the **Dragonborn** enrollment.

**Cross-Species Standards**: Early guild masters developed the Unified Arms Standard, specifying metal compositions, forging techniques, and quality testing procedures. Dwarven **Grimjaw Ironhand** contributed runic reinforcement methods, while **Dragonborn** smiths shared heat-treatment secrets.

### Modern Era (360-450 AS)

**Military Expansion**: **Queen Elara**'s reign required expanded military production. The guild tripled **Silverhaven**'s weapons output and established new forges in frontier garrisons. **General Ironfist** relies on guild-standard equipment for all **Eastern Kingdoms** forces.

**Dragonborn Integration**: **Lady Zara Goldscale** formally integrated **Drakkenheim**'s smiths into the guild, sharing ancient **Dragonborn** metallurgical techniques. Dragon-forged blades now carry the guild mark alongside traditional dwarven and human products.

**Naval Contracts**: **Admiral Hornbreaker** commissioned guild-produced naval weapons, including harpoon launchers and reinforced hull plating. **Crystal Bay**'s ship armament depends entirely on guild supply.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guild Master**: Supreme authority over all guild operations, currently the dwarven master smith **Thorvald Hammerfall**. Guild Master Hammerfall trained under **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s successors and maintains dwarven quality traditions while honoring cross-species commitments. **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Queen Elara** both recognize his authority.

**Forge Masters**: Senior smiths who oversee regional forges. Each forge master manages production, quality testing, and apprentice training in their territory. The council of forge masters meets quarterly in **Stonehold**.

**Journeyman Smiths**: Qualified smiths authorized to produce standard guild weapons and armor. Journeymen work in guild forges or licensed independent shops across Aethelgard.

**Apprentice Smiths**: Students in the guild's rigorous training programs. Apprenticeships last 8-12 years depending on specialization—longer for runic reinforcement, shorter for basic armor production.

### Regional Forges

**Stonehold Royal Armory**: The guild's founding forge and primary production facility. **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds this forge directly, and **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** blesses all weapons produced here. The armory produces Aethelgard's finest plate armor and dwarven war axes.

**Silverhaven Military Forge**: **Queen Elara**'s primary weapons supplier. This forge produces standardized military equipment for the **Eastern Kingdoms** armed forces. **General Ironfist** inspects quarterly to verify quality standards.

**Drakkenheim Dragon Forge**: **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** smiths practice ancient draconic metallurgy here. Dragon-forged blades, renowned for retaining edge and resisting heat, are produced exclusively at this forge.

**Crystal Bay Naval Forge**: Specializing in naval armaments, this forge produces harpoon launchers, marine armor, and ship-mounted weapons for **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s fleet.

## Headquarters and Bases

The guild headquarters occupies the **Stonehold Royal Armory**, a massive underground forge complex with training halls, design studios, and the legendary **Anvil of the Founders** where the guild charter was signed. Regional forge halls operate in **Silverhaven**, **Crystal Bay**, and **Drakkenheim**. Each location features species-specific forging environments—dwarven magma forges, **Dragonborn** dragonfire furnaces, and human conventional forges.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Quality Above All

**The Guild Mark**: Every weapon and armor piece bearing the guild mark has passed rigorous quality testing. Substandard work is destroyed, not sold at discount. **King Thorin Stoneforge** enforces this standard with legal penalties for forgery of the guild mark.

**Material Standards**: The guild specifies exact metal compositions for all products. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s runic steel formula remains the standard for premium weapons, while **Dragonborn** heat-treatment processes produce unmatched blade edges.

**Lifetime Guarantee**: Guild products carry a lifetime guarantee against manufacturing defects. Smiths who produce defective items must repair or replace them at personal cost.

### Military Readiness

**Strategic Reserves**: The guild maintains weapons and armor reserves in **Stonehold**, **Silverhaven**, and **Crystal Bay**. **General Ironfist** coordinates reserve levels based on threat assessments.

**Rapid Production**: In emergencies, the guild activates dormant forges and recalls retired smiths. **Queen Elara**'s emergency production orders can triple output within 30 days.

**Innovation**: The guild collaborates with the **Ironhand Guild** on runic weapon research and with **Gnomish Engineers** on siege weapon design. **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s naval harpoon launchers resulted from this collaboration.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Physical Strength**: Smithing demands endurance and power. Candidates must demonstrate capacity for long hours at the forge. Dwarves and **Dragonborn** naturally excel, but determined humans and halflings also qualify.

**Temperament**: The guild values patience and precision. Rushed or careless smiths produce dangerous defects. **King Thorin Stoneforge** personally interviews dwarven candidates.

**Sponsorship**: Existing members must vouch for candidates. The guild investigates sponsors if apprentices fail training.

### Training Programs

**Foundation Years**: Four years of basic metallurgy, forging techniques, and material identification. Apprentices work communal forges under journeyman supervision.

**Specialization Tracks**: After foundations, apprentices choose among armor smithing, weapon smithing, runic reinforcement, or **Dragonborn** techniques. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s runic methods require the longest specialization.

**Masterwork Examination**: Candidates produce a masterwork weapon or armor piece evaluated by three forge masters. **King Thorin Stoneforge** sometimes attends **Stonehold** examinations.

### Membership Demographics

**Dwarves** (40%): Traditional masters of metalsmithing. Dwarven guild members staff the **Royal Armory** and produce Aethelgard's finest plate armor.

**Humans** (30%): Military production specialists. Human smiths fill **Silverhaven**'s forges and produce standardized equipment for **Queen Elara**'s forces.

**Dragonborn** (15%): Heat-treatment and draconic metallurgy specialists. **Lady Zara Goldscale** ensures **Dragonborn** techniques are preserved and shared within the guild.

**Other Species** (15%): **Gnomes** specializing in precision work, **half-orcs** in heavy weapons, and **Catfolk** in flexible armor designs.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Primary royal patron. The dwarven king funds the **Royal Armory** and enforces guild standards in dwarven territories. The guild provides **Stonehold**'s military equipment exclusively.

**Queen Elara**: Human royal patron. **Queen Elara** commissions guild production for **Eastern Kingdoms** military forces. **General Ironfist** coordinates supply contracts.

**Lady Zara Goldscale**: **Dragonborn** patron who integrated **Drakkenheim**'s smiths. **Lady Zara Goldscale** shares ancient draconic metallurgical secrets with the guild.

**Ironhand Guild**: Close collaboration on runic weapon reinforcement. **Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge** and Guild Master Hammerfall coordinate production of enchanted military equipment.

### Cautious Partners

**Admiral Hornbreaker**: Naval weapons contracts require specialized production. **Admiral Hornbreaker** demands rust resistance and saltwater durability standards that challenge guild traditions.

**Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**: **Catfolk** trader distributes guild products to civilian markets. Commercial pressures sometimes conflict with military production priorities.

**Gnomish Engineers Guild**: Collaborative on siege weapons, competitive on precision mechanical components. Joint projects include **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s harpoon launchers.

### Ideological Opponents

**Rogue Smiths**: Independent blacksmiths producing unmarked weapons of questionable quality. **Queen Elara** prosecutes weapons fraud; **King Thorin Stoneforge** bans substandard arms from dwarven territories.

**Shadowfen Collectors**: Agents of **Malakor Shadowdancer** seek guild weapons for shadow operations. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** orders and reports suspicious purchase attempts.

**War Profiteers**: Merchants stockpiling guild weapons for resale at inflated prices during conflicts. **Queen Elara** authorizes the guild to restrict sales to verified buyers during emergencies.

## Notable Members

### Guild Master Thorvald Hammerfall

Dwarven master smith leading the guild since 430 AS. Thorvald trained under **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s direct successors and maintains the **Royal Armory**'s legendary quality. He oversaw the integration of **Dragonborn** smiths and modernized production standards.

**Achievements**:
- Integrated **Dragonborn** metallurgical techniques into guild standards
- Tripled **Silverhaven**'s weapons production capacity
- Designed **General Ironfist**'s personal enchanted plate armor
- Maintained **Stonehold Royal Armory**'s flawless quality record
- Negotiated naval weapons contracts with **Admiral Hornbreaker**

### Forge Master Zarask Ironscale

**Dragonborn** smith leading the **Drakkenheim Dragon Forge**. Zarask brought **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s ancient draconic formulas to the guild, producing blades that hold edges three times longer than conventional steel.

### Journeyman Elara Swiftforge

Human smith managing the **Silverhaven Military Forge**. Elara streamlined production processes, enabling the forge to meet **General Ironfist**'s emergency supply requirements during border skirmishes with **Northern Wastes** raiders.

## Notable Operations

### Royal Armory Expansion (435-440 AS)

**King Thorin Stoneforge** commissioned a complete modernization of the **Royal Armory**. Guild Master Hammerfall oversaw construction of new magma forges, expanded training halls, and updated safety systems. The upgraded facility doubled production capacity while maintaining quality.

### Dragonborn Integration (438-442 AS)

**Lady Zara Goldscale** and Guild Master Hammerfall negotiated the formal integration of **Drakkenheim**'s smiths into the guild. The process required adapting **Dragonborn** traditional techniques to guild quality standards while preserving draconic metallurgical secrets. The resulting hybrid methods produce some of Aethelgard's finest blades.

### Crystal Bay Naval Armament (440-448 AS)

**Admiral Hornbreaker** commissioned full naval armament for **Crystal Bay**'s expanded fleet. The guild produced harpoon launchers, marine armor, and ship-mounted weapons designed to withstand saltwater corrosion. Collaboration with the **Gnomish Engineers Guild** produced innovative naval siege weapons.

### Emergency War Production (448-450 AS)

Following **Northern Wastes** incursions, **Queen Elara** activated emergency production orders. The guild recalled retired smiths, activated dormant forges, and delivered 10,000 weapons and 5,000 armor sets to **General Ironfist** within 60 days. The operation demonstrated the guild's strategic reserve system.
