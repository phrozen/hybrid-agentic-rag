# The Jewelers' Consortium

## Overview

The Jewelers' Consortium represents gem cutters, jewelers, and enchanters working with precious stones from **Stormpeak Mountains**, **Southern Expanse**, and **Shattered Isles**. The Consortium sets quality standards, certifies enchanted jewelry, and trains apprentices in **Stonehold**, **Crystal Bay**, and **Silverhaven**. **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Lady Zara Goldscale** both patronize Consortium members.

## Founding and History

The Jewelers' Consortium was founded in 338 AS to regulate the gem and jewelry trade that had become plagued by fraud, enchanted forgeries, and unsafe working conditions. The discovery of rich gem deposits in the **Stormpeak Mountains** and **Shattered Isles** created a boom that required organization.

### Founding Period (338-370 AS)

**Fraud Prevention**: The consortium was founded partly in response to widespread gem fraud—fake stones, mislabeled enchantments, and deceptive pricing. Dwarven gem cutter **Ruby Ironvein** and human enchanter **Gemstone Brightlapse** organized honest jewelers to establish certification standards.

**Royal Certification**: **King Thorin Stoneforge** immediately endorsed the consortium, recognizing that dwarven gem quality certification would protect **Stonehold**'s reputation. **Crystal Bay**'s trade leaders followed, creating the tri-city certification system still used today.

**Enchantment Standards**: The consortium developed the first standards for enchanted jewelry, requiring certification of magical properties. The **Arcane College of Thaldor** provided enchantment verification, and **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**'s predecessor approved the magical certification system.

### Modern Era (370-450 AS)

**Dragonborn Integration**: **Lady Zara Goldscale** brought **Dragonborn** gem knowledge and **Southern Expanse** stones into the consortium. **Dragonborn** traditional lapidary techniques, especially heat-based cutting methods, transformed the consortium's capabilities.

**Shattered Isles Gems**: Rare gems from the **Shattered Isles** became available through careful exploration. These planar-touched stones possess unique magical properties highly valued by enchanters. The consortium maintains strict controls on **Shattered Isles** gem distribution.

**Fashion Influence**: The consortium's annual jewelry exhibitions set fashion trends across Aethelgard. **Queen Elara** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** both wear consortium-certified pieces at state functions, driving demand for certified jewelry.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Consortium Director**: Supreme authority, currently the dwarven master jeweler **Ruby Ironvein II**, granddaughter of the founding director. Director Ironvein maintains **Stonehold** certification standards and coordinates with **King Thorin Stoneforge** on gem trade regulation.

**Master Jewelers**: Senior members qualified to cut the rarest gems, create masterwork jewelry, and certify enchanted pieces. Masters specialize in cutting, setting, or enchanting.

**Journeyman Jewelers**: Qualified members authorized for standard jewelry production and basic enchantment. Journeymen operate shops in major cities across Aethelgard.

**Apprentice Jewelers**: Students learning cutting, setting, and enchantment. Apprenticeships last 8-12 years for enchantment specialists, reflecting the skill required.

### Certification System

**Stonehold Certification**: Gems and jewelry certified in **Stonehold** bear the dwarven quality mark, the consortium's most prestigious designation. **King Thorin Stoneforge** personally guarantees **Stonehold**-certified pieces.

**Crystal Bay Certification**: **Crystal Bay** certification focuses on trade stones and commercially enchanted pieces. **Mayor Ironscale** endorses **Crystal Bay** certification for sea trade.

**Silverhaven Certification**: **Silverhaven** certification covers fashion jewelry and **Queen Elara**'s court pieces. The human certification emphasizes setting design and wearability.

## Headquarters and Bases

The consortium headquarters occupies a secure building in **Stonehold**'s gem district, featuring cutting workshops, enchantment laboratories, and the legendary **Vault of Certified Stones** where master examples of every certified gem type are stored. Regional offices in **Crystal Bay** and **Silverhaven** each maintain certification workshops and display galleries. **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds the headquarters' security.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Quality and Certification

**Absolute Standards**: Every gem and piece of jewelry bearing the consortium's mark must meet exacting quality standards. Fraudulent certification is punished by permanent expulsion. **King Thorin Stoneforge** prosecutes counterfeit marks under dwarven law.

**Enchantment Verification**: The **Arcane College of Thaldor** verifies all enchanted jewelry certifications. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provides magical analysis, ensuring enchantments match claimed properties.

**Provenance Tracking**: Every certified stone can be traced from mine to finished piece. The consortium tracks gem origins to prevent conflict stone trading and ensure ethical sourcing.

### Artistic Excellence

**Species Techniques**: The consortium celebrates diverse jewelry techniques—dwarven precision cutting, **Dragonborn** heat working, elven living-metal settings, and human design innovation. **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** methods complement dwarven traditions.

**Innovation**: The consortium encourages new techniques and designs while maintaining quality standards. Annual exhibitions showcase innovations that often become next season's trends across Aethelgard.

**Cross-Species Collaboration**: Master jewelers learn each other's techniques. **Dragonborn** heat cutting, dwarven precision faceting, and elven organic setting combine in pieces that no single species could create alone.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Aptitude for Precision**: Jewelry requires exceptional hand-eye coordination, patience, and attention to detail. **Gnomes** and dwarves often excel in these assessments.

**Magical Sensitivity**: Enchantment specialists must demonstrate magical aptitude. The **Arcane College** tests candidates' ability to sense and channel magical energy.

**Character Evaluation**: The consortium investigates candidates' backgrounds thoroughly. Fraud, theft, and ethical violations disqualify applicants permanently.

### Training Programs

**Cutting Foundation**: Three years learning gem identification, cutting techniques, and precision tool use. **Stonehold** masters teach dwarven cutting methods; **Dragonborn** masters teach heat-based techniques.

**Setting and Design**: Two to three years learning metalworking, gem setting, and jewelry design. Human and elven design traditions are taught alongside dwarven structural techniques.

**Enchantment Specialization**: Three to five years learning jewelry enchantment under **Arcane College** supervision. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provides theoretical instruction; consortium masters teach practical application.

### Membership Demographics

**Dwarves** (35%): Master gem cutters and miners. Dwarven precision cutting remains the gold standard in Aethelgard. **King Thorin Stoneforge** proudly sponsors dwarven consortium members.

**Dragonborn** (20%): Heat-based cutting specialists and **Southern Expanse** gem experts. **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s people bring unique stones and techniques to the consortium.

**Humans** (25%): Design innovators and commercial jewelers. Human members dominate **Silverhaven**'s fashion jewelry market and **Queen Elara**'s court commissions.

**Elves** (10%): Artistic specialists creating organic, flowing designs. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** restricts some elven setting techniques to approved members.

**Other Species** (10%): **Gnomes** specializing in miniature work, **Catfolk** with decorative sensibilities, and **halflings** with nature-inspired designs.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Primary patron and certification guarantor. **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds **Stonehold** certification and prosecutes gem fraud under dwarven law. Dwarven gems carry the consortium's most prestigious mark.

**Lady Zara Goldscale**: **Dragonborn** patron bringing **Southern Expanse** gems and traditional techniques. **Lady Zara Goldscale** funds **Dragonborn** training programs and sources rare stones from **Drakkenheim** territories.

**Arcane College of Thaldor**: Enchantment verification partner. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provides magical certification services. The consortium cannot certify enchanted jewelry without **Arcane College** verification.

**Queen Elara**: Royal patron commissioning court jewelry and endorsing **Silverhaven** certification. **Queen Elara** wears consortium-certified pieces at state functions, driving fashion demand.

### Cautious Partners

**Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**: **Catfolk** trader distributing consortium jewelry to commercial markets. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s distribution network expands reach but introduces commercial pressures.

**Ironhand Guild**: Collaborative on enchanted jewelry but competitive for some royal commissions. **Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge** and Director Ironvein maintain professional respect.

**Glassblowers' Guild**: Minor overlap in decorative crystal work. Both guilds source similar raw materials, creating occasional procurement competition.

### Ideological Opponents

**Gem Fraudsters**: Criminals selling fake or misrepresented stones and enchantments. The consortium investigates fraud vigorously and refers cases to royal authorities. **King Thorin Stoneforge** prosecutes gem fraud harshly.

**Shadowfen Smugglers**: **Malakor Shadowdancer**'s network seeks **Shattered Isles** gems for shadow enchantments. The consortium refuses all questionable gem sales and tracks **Shattered Isles** stones scrupulously.

**Conflict Stone Traders**: Unethical merchants selling gems from conflict zones or stolen sources. The consortium's provenance tracking system prevents conflict stones from receiving certification.

## Notable Members

### Director Ruby Ironvein II

Dwarven master jeweler and granddaughter of founding director **Ruby Ironvein**. Director Ironvein combines dwarven precision cutting with **Dragonborn** heat techniques, creating pieces that earn both **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s highest praise.

**Achievements**:
- Integrated **Dragonborn** gem-cutting techniques into consortium training
- Established the **Shattered Isles** gem provenance tracking system
- Created **Queen Elara**'s coronation necklace, the finest piece in **Eastern Kingdoms**
- Negotiated **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** training program funding
- Maintained **Stonehold** certification as Aethelgard's most prestigious jewelry mark

### Master Heatforge

**Dragonborn** lapidary specialist who introduced **Southern Expanse** gems and heat-based cutting to the consortium. Master Heatforge's pieces combine **Dragonborn** fire techniques with dwarven precision.

### Journeyman Starspark

Human designer managing the **Silverhaven** certification office. Journeyman Starspark's fashion-forward designs earn **Queen Elara**'s regular commissions and set Aethelgard's jewelry trends.

## Notable Operations

### Annual Jewelry Exhibition (Annual)

The consortium's flagship event showcasing the year's finest pieces. **Queen Elara**, **King Thorin Stoneforge**, and **Lady Zara Goldscale** all attend. Winning entries become next season's fashion must-haves, driving commercial demand for consortium-certified work.

### Shattered Isles Gem Program (430-450 AS)

The consortium carefully acquires and certifies rare **Shattered Isles** gems possessing unique magical properties. Provenance tracking prevents these dangerous stones from reaching unauthorized hands. **Arcane College** mages verify enchantments; **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds security.

### Queen Elara's Coronation Jewelry (440 AS)

Director Ironvein personally designed and crafted **Queen Elara**'s coronation necklace, featuring a **Stormpeak Mountains** diamond set in dwarven platinum with **Dragonborn** fire-cutting. The piece remains the most celebrated jewelry in **Eastern Kingdoms**.

### Certification Expansion (435-445 AS)

The consortium expanded certification from **Stonehold** alone to include **Crystal Bay** and **Silverhaven** offices. **Mayor Ironscale** and **Queen Elara** now endorse regional certifications, expanding the consortium's reach while maintaining quality standards.
