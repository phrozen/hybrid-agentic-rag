# The Porters' and Teamsters' Guild

## Overview

The Porters' and Teamsters' Guild handles cargo transport across Aethelgard, managing docks, warehouses, and overland freight. The Guild coordinates with the **Messengers' Guild**, **Trade Federation of Crystal Bay**, and **Thunder Pass** customs. Guild members ensure goods move efficiently from producers to markets.

## Founding and History

The Porters' and Teamsters' Guild was founded in 340 AS when Aethelgard's growing trade volume required organized cargo handling and transport. Random laborers and cart operators could no longer handle the continent's commerce efficiently or safely.

### Founding Period (340-370 AS)

**Trade Boom**: As **Silverhaven**, **Crystal Bay**, and **Stonehold** grew, cargo volume outpaced available transport. Merchants lost goods to theft, damage, and delays. Human teamster **Bjorn Haulstead** organized the first union of porters and drivers to establish standards.

**Crystal Bay Partnership**: **Mayor Ironscale**'s predecessor recognized that **Crystal Bay**'s port needed organized dock workers. The guild's dock operations became the model for all Aethelgard's ports. **Trade Federation of Crystal Bay** coordination followed.

**Thunder Pass Customs**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s predecessor authorized the guild to manage customs at **Thunder Pass**, the primary trade route between **Eastern Kingdoms** and dwarven territories. This authority persists to the present day.

### Modern Era (370-450 AS)

**Merchant Prince Network**: **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s trading network expanded dramatically, requiring guild transport for goods moving across **Catfolk** trade routes. The partnership between the guild and **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** became Aethelgard's commercial backbone.

**Queen Elara's Infrastructure**: **Queen Elara** invested in road improvements across **Eastern Kingdoms**, enabling faster guild transport. The queen recognized that efficient cargo movement supported economic growth and royal tax revenue.

**Agricultural Transport**: **Poppy Greenfields**' **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** required guild transport for massive grain shipments from **Eastern Plains** to **Silverhaven**. The guild developed dedicated agricultural transport routes.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guild Master**: Supreme authority, currently the human teamster **Bjorn Haulstead III**, descendant of the founding organizer. Guild Master Haulstead coordinates with **Queen Elara**, **King Thorin Stoneforge**, and **Mayor Ironscale** on trade infrastructure.

**Dock Masters**: Senior members managing port operations in **Crystal Bay**, **Silverhaven**, and **Drakkenheim**. Dock Masters oversee cargo loading, unloading, and warehousing.

**Route Masters**: Experienced teamsters managing overland transport routes. Route Masters plan efficient routes and coordinate with **Thunder Pass** customs officials.

**Porters and Teamsters**: Active workers handling cargo on docks, in warehouses, and on transport vehicles. This is the guild's largest membership category.

### Operations Divisions

**Dock Operations**: Cargo handling at all major ports. **Crystal Bay**'s dock operations are the guild's largest single operation, handling goods for **Trade Federation of Crystal Bay** merchants.

**Overland Transport**: Cart and wagon operations connecting all major cities. Route Masters coordinate with road authorities and customs officials for efficient transport.

**Warehousing**: Guild-operated warehouses in every major city provide secure storage for merchants. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** uses guild warehouses extensively.

**Customs Coordination**: **Thunder Pass** customs operations, authorized by **King Thorin Stoneforge**, inspect and process trade goods between **Eastern Kingdoms** and dwarven territories.

## Headquarters and Bases

The guild headquarters in **Silverhaven** doubles as Aethelgard's largest warehouse complex, with secure storage for thousands of cargo units. The headquarters includes route planning offices, dock coordination centers, and **Thunder Pass** customs administration. Regional operations centers in **Crystal Bay**, **Stonehold**, and the **Eastern Plains** manage local transport.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Efficient Commerce

**Reliable Transport**: The guild guarantees delivery timelines for goods bearing the guild seal. Merchants trust guild transport to deliver intact and on time. **Trade Federation of Crystal Bay** requires guild certification for insured cargo.

**Fair Pricing**: The guild offers standardized pricing based on weight, distance, and urgency. **Queen Elara** approves rate schedules to prevent price gouging during shortages.

**Complete Coverage**: The guild maintains transport routes to every settlement in Aethelgard, from **Silverhaven** to **Northern Wastes** outposts. **Poppy Greenfields**' grain shipments reach **Silverhaven** reliably because guild routes cover **Eastern Plains**.

### Worker Protection

**Fair Wages**: The guild sets minimum wages for porters and teamsters, preventing exploitation. **Queen Elara** endorses guild wage standards as **Eastern Kingdoms** labor policy.

**Safe Working Conditions**: Dock work and transport carry physical risks. The guild enforces safety standards—proper lifting techniques, load limits, and rest requirements. **King Thorin Stoneforge** mandates guild safety standards in dwarven territories.

**Benefits**: Guild members receive injury compensation, retirement support, and family assistance. These benefits reduce turnover and maintain experienced workforces.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Physical Strength**: Porters and teamsters must lift heavy cargo and work long hours. The guild tests candidates' strength and endurance before acceptance.

**Reliability**: Cargo handling requires punctuality and trustworthiness. The guild investigates candidates' backgrounds for reliability.

**Spatial Awareness**: Dock and warehouse work requires understanding three-dimensional cargo arrangement. Candidates must demonstrate basic organizational skills.

### Training Programs

**Dock Operations Training**: Six months learning cargo handling, loading procedures, and dock safety. Training occurs at **Crystal Bay**'s docks under experienced dock workers.

**Overland Transport Training**: One year learning route planning, animal handling, vehicle maintenance, and road safety. Route Masters supervise training on actual transport runs.

**Warehousing Training**: Six months learning storage systems, inventory management, and security procedures. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s representatives provide commercial inventory instruction.

**Customs Training**: Specialized training for **Thunder Pass** customs operations, including trade law, inspection procedures, and diplomatic protocols. **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s customs officials provide instruction.

### Membership Demographics

**Humans** (45%): The majority of porters and teamsters, handling cargo across **Eastern Kingdoms**. Human members dominate overland transport operations.

**Dwarves** (20%): Heavy cargo specialists and **Thunder Pass** customs operators. **King Thorin Stoneforge** ensures dwarven members manage all dwarven territory customs.

**Halflings** (15%): Agricultural transport specialists. **Poppy Greenfields**' **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** relies on halfling teamsters for grain transport.

**Catfolk** (10%): Urban delivery specialists. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** network handles city deliveries that require speed and agility.

**Other Species** (10%): **Minotaurs** for heavy lifting, **Lizardfolk** in **Southern Expanse** territories, and **gnomes** with specialized cargo operations.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**Trade Federation of Crystal Bay**: Primary commercial partner. The **Trade Federation of Crystal Bay** requires guild-certified transport for insured cargo. **Mayor Ironscale** coordinates port operations with the guild.

**Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**: **Catfolk** trader whose commercial network depends on guild transport. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** and the guild coordinate closely on routing and scheduling.

**Queen Elara**: Royal patron investing in road infrastructure and approving transport rates. **Queen Elara** recognizes that efficient cargo movement supports **Eastern Kingdoms** prosperity.

**Poppy Greenfields**: **Halfling** agricultural partner requiring reliable grain transport. The guild's **Eastern Plains** routes ensure **Poppy Greenfields**' harvests reach **Silverhaven**.

### Cautious Partners

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Dwarven patron authorizing **Thunder Pass** customs operations. **King Thorin Stoneforge** restricts some dwarven goods for security reasons, requiring careful customs inspection.

**Messengers' Guild**: Overlapping parcel delivery requires coordination. The two guilds cooperate on small package transport but compete for some parcel revenue. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** mediates disputes.

**Admiral Hornbreaker**: Maritime partner coordinating sea transport. **Admiral Hornbreaker** prioritizes naval operations over merchant shipping during conflicts, sometimes disrupting guild schedules.

### Ideological Opponents

**Black Market Smugglers**: Criminals bypassing **Thunder Pass** customs and guild transport standards. **King Thorin Stoneforge** prosecutes smugglers; the guild reports suspicious activities.

**Unlicensed Carters**: Independent operators ignoring guild safety and wage standards. Unlicensed operators undercut guild prices but provide unreliable, sometimes dangerous transport.

**Shadowfen Transporters**: **Malakor Shadowdancer**'s network moves goods outside legal channels. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** cargo and reports suspicious shipping patterns to royal authorities.
