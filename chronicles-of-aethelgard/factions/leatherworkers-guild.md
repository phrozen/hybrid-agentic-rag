# The Leatherworkers' Guild

## Overview

The Leatherworkers' Guild processes hides and creates leather goods across Aethelgard, with **Lizardfolk** swamp leather, **Eastern Kingdoms** cattle leather, and magical creature hides all represented. The Guild trains tanners, sets quality standards, and supplies armorers, bookbinders, and craftsmen. **Hunter Zek** advises on ethical sourcing from magical creatures.

## Founding and History

The Leatherworkers' Guild was founded in 335 AS, when the growing demand for quality leather goods across Aethelgard required organized production standards and sustainable sourcing practices. The guild emerged from disparate tanning operations that had varying quality, safety practices, and environmental impact.

### Founding Period (335-365 AS)

**Quality Crisis**: Inconsistent leather quality plagued **Eastern Kingdoms** military procurement. Boots that fell apart, armor that cracked, and harnesses that broke endangered soldiers and civilians alike. Human tanner **Garrett Hidecrafter** organized quality standards that evolved into the guild.

**Lizardfolk Integration**: **Shaman Vessska**'s predecessor brought **Lizardfolk** swamp leather techniques to the fledgling guild. **Lizardfolk** tanning methods, using swamp plant enzymes, produced uniquely supple and water-resistant leather that revolutionized the industry.

**Dwarven Adoption**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s predecessor recognized that dwarven military equipment required reliable leather components. The dwarven royal charter established **Stonehold**'s tanning operations under guild standards, ensuring dwarven leather met military requirements.

### Modern Era (365-450 AS)

**Magical Creature Hides**: **Hunter Zek**'s ethical sourcing policies transformed the guild's approach to magical creature leather. Previously indiscriminate hunting gave way to sustainable harvesting from naturally shed hides and ethically sourced materials.

**Expanded Market**: Population growth across **Eastern Kingdoms** and coastal cities created massive demand for leather goods—shoes, belts, bags, armor, and book covers. The guild expanded production to meet demand while maintaining quality.

**Craftsmanship Revival**: The guild revived endangered leatherworking techniques, including **Lizardfolk** decorative tooling, dwarven boiled-leather armor, and elven fine leather work. These revived traditions enrich guild production.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guild Master**: Supreme authority, currently the human master leatherworker **Garrett Hidecrafter III**, descendant of the founding tanner. Guild Master Hidecrafter coordinates ethical sourcing with **Hunter Zek** and maintains quality standards across all guild operations.

**Master Leatherworkers**: Senior artisans qualified to work with the finest materials, including magical creature hides. Masters specialize in tanning, tooling, armor, or fine leather goods.

**Journeyman Leatherworkers**: Qualified artisans producing standard guild products. Journeymen operate workshops across Aethelgard, supplying leather goods to merchants, military forces, and craftsmen.

**Apprentice Leatherworkers**: Students learning tanning, cutting, and working leather. Apprenticeships last 5-7 years, with specialization tracks in various leatherworking traditions.

### Regional Operations

**Silverhaven Workshop**: Largest production facility, supplying **Eastern Kingdoms** military and civilian markets. **General Ironfist**'s forces use guild leather for boots, belts, and armor components.

**Stonehold Workshop**: Dwarven armor and mining leather specialist. **King Thorin Stoneforge** contracts this workshop for all dwarven military leather. Boiled-leather armor remains a dwarven specialty.

**Southern Expanse Workshop**: **Lizardfolk** swamp leather production, using traditional enzymes for uniquely water-resistant leather. **Trader Slickscale** distributes swamp leather products across Aethelgard.

**Crystal Bay Workshop**: Maritime leather specialist producing salt-water-resistant products for **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s navy and **Crystal Bay**'s fishing fleet.

## Headquarters and Bases

The guild headquarters in **Silverhaven** includes tanning yards, workshops, and the **Hall of Leather**, a showcase of guild techniques from every species' tradition. The headquarters features a working tannery where apprentices learn basics. Regional workshops operate in **Stonehold**, the **Southern Expanse**, and **Crystal Bay**, each specializing in local leather types and techniques.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Quality and Sustainability

**Strict Standards**: Every piece of guild leather must meet species-specific quality standards. **King Thorin Stoneforge** requires dwarven military leather to withstand underground conditions; **Queen Elara** mandates human military leather for field durability.

**Ethical Sourcing**: **Hunter Zek**'s ethical sourcing framework governs magical creature hide procurement. Natural shedding, ethical hunting, and sustainable harvesting replace indiscriminate killing. **Shaman Vessska** approves all **Lizardfolk** territory sourcing.

**Environmental Responsibility**: Tanning produces significant waste. The guild developed environmentally sound disposal methods with **Poppy Greenfields**' advice on composting tanning byproducts. **Sylvaris Nightbreeze** and the **Forest Wardens** monitor tannery environmental impact.

### Craftsmanship and Tradition

**Technique Preservation**: The guild documents and teaches endangered leatherworking traditions. **Lizardfolk** swamp tanning, dwarven boiled-leather armor, and elven fine leather work are preserved through active teaching.

**Cross-Species Learning**: Guild members learn multiple species' techniques. **Lizardfolk** water-resistant treatment, dwarven hardening, and human tooling combine in pieces that single-species artisans cannot create.

**Innovation**: New tanning agents, decorative techniques, and product designs keep the guild relevant. **Gnomish Engineers Guild** collaborations produce innovative leather-based mechanical components.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Manual Skill**: Leatherworking requires cutting precision, stitching dexterity, and tooling artistry. Candidates demonstrate basic hand skills before acceptance.

**Sensory Tolerance**: Tanning involves strong odors, chemical processes, and biological materials. Candidates must tolerate these conditions without adverse reactions.

**Ethical Commitment**: Members must agree to **Hunter Zek**'s ethical sourcing standards. Violations—using poached hides, endangered species, or unethical practices—result in permanent expulsion.

### Training Programs

**Foundation Tanning**: Two years learning hide processing, tanning agents, and basic leather production. Apprentices work in tanning yards under journeyman supervision.

**Craft Specialization**: Two to three years focusing on armor, fine goods, tooling, or **Lizardfolk** techniques. **Hunter Zek** teaches magical creature hide working for approved specialists.

**Masterwork Examination**: Candidates produce a masterwork leather piece demonstrating their specialty. **King Thorin Stoneforge** occasionally observes **Stonehold** armor examinations.

### Membership Demographics

**Humans** (35%): Versatile leatherworkers producing military and civilian goods across **Eastern Kingdoms**. Human guild members dominate **Silverhaven** production.

**Lizardfolk** (20%): Swamp leather specialists producing uniquely water-resistant products. **Chief Ssithik** and **Shaman Vessska** ensure **Lizardfolk** traditions are preserved. **Trader Slickscale** distributes their products.

**Dwarves** (20%): Armor and mining leather specialists. Dwarven boiled-leather armor is legendary for its protective quality. **King Thorin Stoneforge** sponsors dwarven membership.

**Elves** (10%): Fine leather workers producing decorative tooled pieces. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** commissions elven leather goods for **Silvermoon** courts.

**Other Species** (15%): **Halflings** with agricultural leather, **Catfolk** with decorative pieces, and **Dragonborn** with fire-resistant leather working.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**Hunter Zek**: **Lizardfolk** guide advising on ethical magical creature hide sourcing. **Hunter Zek**'s framework transformed guild practices from indiscriminate to sustainable and ethical.

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Dwarven patron sponsoring **Stonehold** military leather production. **King Thorin Stoneforge** requires guild-certified leather for all dwarven military equipment.

**General Ironfist**: Military contractor for **Eastern Kingdoms** forces. **General Ironfist** requires guild leather for boots, belts, armor, and harnesses.

**Armorers' and Weaponsmiths' Guild**: Close partner producing combined leather-and-metal armor. The leatherworkers supply leather components; the armorers provide metal parts.

### Cautious Partners

**Tanners' and Dyers' Guild**: Close colleague but potential competitor for raw materials. Both guilds process hides—leatherworkers for leather, tanners' guild for rawhide and dye products. Coordination prevents resource conflicts.

**Forest Wardens**: **Sylvaris Nightbreeze**'s **Forest Wardens** monitor tannery environmental impact. The guild cooperates but finds the oversight sometimes constraining.

**Trader Slickscale**: **Lizardfolk** trader distributing **Southern Expanse** swamp leather. **Trader Slickscale** provides valuable market access but sometimes prioritizes commercial interests over ethical sourcing.

### Ideological Opponents

**Poachers**: Criminals hunting magical creatures for valuable hides. **Hunter Zek** and the guild oppose all poaching. **Queen Elara** prosecutes poachers on guild recommendation.

**Unethical Tanners**:Operators producing leather without environmental safeguards. Unregulated tanneries pollute waterways and produce inferior leather. The guild reports violations.

**Shadowfen Leather Seekers**: **Malakor Shadowdancer**'s agents seek magical creature hides for shadow operations. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** hide orders and reports suspicious inquiries.

## Notable Operations

### Military Leather Supply (Ongoing)

**General Ironfist** contracts the guild for all **Eastern Kingdoms** military leather—boots, belts, armor components, and harnesses. The ongoing program requires 10,000+ leather items monthly, testing the guild's production capacity.

### Ethical Sourcing Framework (430-450 AS)

**Hunter Zek** and Guild Master Hidecrafter developed a comprehensive ethical sourcing framework for magical creature hides. The framework covers natural shedding collection, ethical hunting permits, and sustainable harvesting quotas. **Shaman Vessska** and **Chief Ssithik** enforce **Southern Expanse** compliance.

### Lizardfolk Swamp Leather Program (432-450 AS)

**Chief Ssithik** authorized limited **Lizardfolk** swamp leather export through **Trader Slickscale**. The uniquely water-resistant leather found markets in **Crystal Bay** (maritime applications), **Silverhaven** (luxury goods), and **Stonehold** (mining equipment). **Shaman Vessska** ensures traditional techniques are preserved.

### Environmental Tannery Reform (440-448 AS)

**Poppy Greenfields** and the guild developed environmentally sound tannery waste disposal methods, converting tanning byproducts into agricultural compost. **Sylvaris Nightbreeze** and the **Forest Wardens** approved the new practices, reducing tannery environmental impact by 60% across **Eastern Kingdoms**.
