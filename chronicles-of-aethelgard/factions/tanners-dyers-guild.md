# The Tanners' and Dyers' Guild

## Overview

The Tanners' and Dyers' Guild processes hides and creates colored textiles using natural and magical dyes from across Aethelgard. Guild members source materials from **Eastern Plains**, **Western Reaches**, and **Southern Expanse**, with **Lizardfolk** swamp plants providing unique colors. The Guild trains dyers and maintains color standards.

## Founding and History

The Tanners' and Dyers' Guild was founded in 337 AS when the growing demand for colored textiles and processed hides exceeded the capacity of independent craftspeople. The discovery of magical dye sources and **Lizardfolk** color traditions created new possibilities that required organized development.

### Founding Period (337-370 AS)

**Color Revolution**: The discovery that **Lizardfolk** swamp plants produced vibrant, permanent colors transformed Aethelgard's textile industry. **Shaman Vessska**'s predecessor shared **Lizardfolk** dye secrets with the fledgling guild, enabling colors that human and dwarven techniques could not match.

**Royal Demand**: **King Aldric the Unifier**'s court required colored textiles for royal regalia, military uniforms, and ceremonial purposes. The king's demand for consistent, high-quality dyed fabrics drove the guild's founding and early development.

**Hide Processing**: Simultaneously, the tanning side of the guild organized hide processing for the **Leatherworkers' Guild** and other crafts. Standardized tanning processes improved hide quality across Aethelgard.

### Modern Era (370-450 AS)

**Magical Dyes**: Collaboration with the **Arcane College of Thaldor** produced magical dyes that change color with temperature, glow in darkness, or resist soiling. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**'s enchantment techniques opened entirely new markets.

**Weavers' Guild Partnership**: The **Weavers' Guild** became the primary consumer of dyed textiles. The two guilds coordinate closely—weavers produce undyed cloth; tanners and dyers color it to specification.

**Fashion Influence**: The guild's annual color announcements set Aethelgard's fashion trends. **Queen Elara** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** both wear guild-dyed fabrics, and their color preferences influence production.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guild Master**: Supreme authority, currently the human master dyer **Crimson Huewell**, whose family has dyed fabrics for **Queen Elara**'s court for three generations. Guild Master Huewell maintains color standards and coordinates with the **Weavers' Guild** on production.

**Master Dyers**: Senior artisans qualified to develop new colors and dye techniques. Masters specialize in natural dyes, magical dyes, or hide tanning.

**Journeyman Dyers**: Qualified artisans producing standard colors and processed hides. Journeymen operate workshops across Aethelgard.

**Apprentice Dyers**: Students learning dye chemistry, color theory, and tanning processes. Apprenticeships last 5-8 years, depending on specialization.

### Production Divisions

**Dye Production**: Creating natural and magical dyes from plant, mineral, and magical sources. **Lizardfolk** swamp plants, **Western Reaches** berries, and **Stormpeak Mountains** minerals provide raw materials.

**Textile Dyeing**: Coloring fabrics for the **Weavers' Guild**, fashion, and royal commissions. This division operates the largest workshops and produces the most visible guild work.

**Hide Tanning**: Processing hides for the **Leatherworkers' Guild** and other craft consumers. **Hunter Zek**'s ethical sourcing standards govern hide procurement.

**Color Research**: Developing new colors and dye techniques. The **Arcane College of Thaldor** collaborates on magical dye research, while **Lizardfolk** botanical knowledge provides natural dye innovations.

## Headquarters and Bases

The guild headquarters in **Silverhaven** includes vast dye vats, drying rooms, and the **Color Archive**—swatches of every color the guild can produce, with recipes and sourcing information. The archive is Aethelgard's most complete color reference. Regional workshops in **Stonehold** (mineral dyes), the **Southern Expanse** (**Lizardfolk** plant dyes), and **Silvermoon** (elven natural dyes) each specialize in local materials.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Color Excellence

**Permanent Colors**: The guild guarantees that dyed fabrics retain their color through washing, sunlight, and wear. Color fading is unacceptable. **Queen Elara** has worn guild-dyed gowns for years without fading.

**Color Consistency**: The guild's color standards ensure that "**Silverhaven** Blue" means the same color regardless of which workshop produced it. This consistency enables large-scale textile production for military uniforms and royal commissions.

**Color Innovation**: The guild constantly develops new colors through natural discovery and magical enhancement. **Lizardfolk** botanical knowledge and **Arcane College** enchantment generate novel colors each year.

### Sustainable Sourcing

**Plant Conservation**: The guild harvests dye plants sustainably, working with **Forest Wardens** and **Chief Ssithik** to prevent over-harvesting. **Shaman Vessska** provides **Lizardfolk** botanical guidance.

**Mineral Responsibility**: Mining dye minerals follows **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s environmental standards. The guild sources minerals from the **Miners' Union** with environmental safeguards.

**Hide Ethics**: **Hunter Zek**'s ethical sourcing standards govern hide procurement. Only properly sourced hides enter guild tanning operations.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Color Sensitivity**: Candidates must demonstrate exceptional color discrimination and an intuitive understanding of color relationships. The guild tests candidates' ability to match and mix colors precisely.

**Chemical Aptitude**: Dyeing requires understanding of chemical reactions, pH levels, and mordant chemistry. **Gnomes** and humans often excel in these assessments.

**Patience**: Dyeing and tanning are time-intensive processes. Candidates must tolerate long production cycles and meticulous quality control.

### Training Programs

**Foundation Dyeing**: Two years learning basic dye chemistry, color theory, and textile dyeing techniques. Apprentices produce standard colors under journeyman supervision.

**Specialization**: Two to four years focusing on natural dyes, magical dyes, or hide tanning. **Lizardfolk** dye masters teach plant-based techniques; **Arcane College** instructors teach magical dye processes.

**Color Mastery**: Master candidates create an original color or technique that advances the guild's knowledge. **Queen Elara** occasionally attends master certification ceremonies.

### Membership Demographics

**Humans** (35%): Versatile dyers producing standard and fashion colors for **Eastern Kingdoms** markets. Human guild members dominate **Silverhaven** production.

**Lizardfolk** (20%): Plant dye specialists producing colors impossible with any other technique. **Shaman Vessska** and **Chief Ssithik** ensure **Lizardfolk** traditions are preserved. **Trader Slickscale** distributes **Lizardfolk** dyes.

**Dwarves** (20%): Mineral dye specialists producing deep, permanent colors from **Stormpeak Mountains** minerals. **King Thorin Stoneforge** sponsors dwarven dye research.

**Elves** (15%): Natural dye experts using **Western Reaches** and **Whispering Woods** plant sources. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** restricts some elven dye recipes to approved members.

**Other Species** (10%): **Gnomes** with chemical dye innovations, **Catfolk** with fashion color expertise, and **halflings** with agricultural dye plants.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**Weavers' Guild**: Primary consumer of dyed textiles. The **Weavers' Guild** produces undyed cloth; the **Tanners' and Dyers' Guild** colors it. This partnership drives Aethelgard's textile industry.

**Queen Elara**: Royal patron commissioning court colors and fashion-setting fabrics. **Queen Elara**'s color preferences influence production across Aethelgard.

**Arcane College of Thaldor**: Research partner on magical dyes. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** provides enchantment techniques; the guild provides dye chemistry knowledge.

**Shaman Vessska**: **Lizardfolk** botanical expert providing plant dye knowledge from the **Southern Expanse**. **Shaman Vessska**'s contributions create colors impossible with any other technique.

### Cautious Partners

**Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**: **High Elf** ruler who restricts some elven dye recipes to elven members. The guild respects these restrictions while advocating for broader sharing.

**Hunter Zek**: **Lizardfolk** guide advising on ethical hide sourcing. **Hunter Zek**'s ethical framework governs hide procurement, but some traders find the standards constraining.

**Leatherworkers' Guild**: Close colleague for hide processing but potential competitor for raw materials. Coordination prevents resource conflicts.

### Ideological Opponents

**Color Fraudsters**: Criminals selling counterfeit dyes or mislabeled colors. The guild investigates color fraud and reports violations. **Queen Elara** prosecutes counterfeiters.

**Unlicensed Dyers**: Independent operators ignoring color standards and environmental regulations. Unlicensed operations produce inferior colors and environmental damage.

**Shadowfen Poison-Dyers**: **Malakor Shadowdancer**'s agents seek both **Lizardfolk** plant dye knowledge and magical dye formulas for shadow operations. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** inquiries and guards its formulas carefully.

## Notable Members

### Guild Master Crimson Huewell

Human master dyer whose family has colored **Queen Elara**'s court for three generations. **Huewell** develops the guild's annual color announcements that set Aethelgard's fashion trends. **Queen Elara** wears exclusively guild-dyed fabrics.

**Achievements**:
- Created the annual color announcement that sets Aethelgard's fashion trends
- Negotiated **Shaman Vessska**'s sharing of **Lizardfolk** plant dye secrets
- Developed magical temperature-sensitive dyes with **Arcane College** assistance
- Maintained color consistency standards across all guild workshops
- Colored **Queen Elara**'s coronation gown with a still-unsurpassed blue

### Master Deepindigo

Dwarf mineral dye specialist producing the deepest, most permanent colors from **Stormpeak Mountains** minerals. Master Deepindigo's blacks and blues are legendary for their intensity and durability.

### Journeyman Swampcolor

**Lizardfolk** dyer managing the **Southern Expanse** workshop. Journeyman Swampcolor produces colors from **Shaman Vessska**-approved plants that no other technique can replicate.

## Notable Operations

### Annual Color Announcement (Annual)

The guild announces each year's fashion colors, setting trends across Aethelgard. **Queen Elara** wears the announcement colors at court, driving demand. The announcement is awaited by weavers, tailors, and merchants across the continent.

### Lizardfolk Plant Dye Program (432-450 AS)

**Shaman Vessska** and **Chief Ssithik** authorized limited export of **Lizardfolk** dye plants through **Trader Slickscale**. The guild developed processing techniques for these plants, producing colors that became Aethelgard's most sought-after and expensive textiles.

### Magical Dye Research (435-450 AS)

**Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** collaborated with the guild on magical dyes—temperature-sensitive colors, glow-in-the-dark pigments, and soil-resistant treatments. These innovations opened entirely new markets and cemented the guild's reputation for cutting-edge color technology.

### Queen Elara's Coronation Colors (440 AS)

Guild Master Huewell personally dyed **Queen Elara**'s coronation gown in a blue so deep and permanent it has never been replicated. The color, known as "**Silverhaven** Coronation Blue," remains the guild's most celebrated achievement and sets the standard for dye permanence.
