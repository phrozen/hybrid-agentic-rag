# The Ironhand Guild

## Overview

The Ironhand Guild is a dwarven craftsmen organization based in **Stonehold**, dedicated to preserving and teaching **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s runemagic techniques. Led by Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge, the guild maintains strict quality standards for enchanted items and operates the only authorized runemagic training facility in the **Stormpeak Mountains**. The guild collaborates with the **Arcane College of Thaldor** on magical research while resisting efforts to fully share dwarven secrets with other species, maintaining **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s balance between cooperation and cultural preservation.

## Founding and History

The Ironhand Guild was founded in 350 AS by **Grimjaw Ironhand** himself, following his revolutionary collaboration with **Throndor the Wise** at the **Arcane College of Thaldor**. Grimjaw recognized that preserving runemagic required formal organization and standardized training.

### Founding Period (350-398 AS)

**Grimjaw's Vision**: The legendary runemaster established the guild to train both dwarves and worthy non-dwarves in runemagic. His first class included 20 dwarves, 5 gnomes, and 3 humans.

**Throndor-Ironhand Partnership**: **Throndor the Wise** provided magical theory expertise while Grimjaw contributed practical crafting knowledge. Their collaboration produced the Throndor-Ironhand runes still used today.

**Royal Charter**: **King Durin Stoneforge** (Grimjaw's patron) granted the guild monopoly on runemagic training in dwarven holds. Current **King Thorin Stoneforge** maintains this charter.

### Modern Era (398-450 AS)

**Guildmaster Succession**: After Grimjaw's death (398 AS), his apprentice Thrain Runeforge became Guildmaster. Thrain studied under Grimjaw for 80 years and maintains his master's standards.

**Stonehold Treasury Wards**: The guild maintains **Grimjaw**'s original runewards protecting the **Stonehold Treasury**. **King Thorin Stoneforge** contracts the guild exclusively for treasury maintenance.

**International Expansion**: The guild established workshops in **Silverhaven** and **Thaldor**, though core training remains in **Stonehold**. **General Ironfist** employs guild members for fortification enchantments.

## Organization and Structure

### Leadership Hierarchy

**Guildmaster**: Supreme leader, currently **Thrain Runeforge**. The Guildmaster examines all master-level candidates and approves major contracts. **King Thorin Stoneforge** consults the Guildmaster on runemagic policy.

**Master Runemasters**: 12 senior members who examine apprentices and maintain quality standards. Masters include both dwarves and non-dwarves who proved exceptional skill.

**Journeyman**: Trained runemakers authorized for standard enchantments. Journeymen can train apprentices but cannot create masterwork items.

**Apprentices**: Students in 20-year training programs. Apprentices spend first 10 years learning theory, second 10 years in practical workshops.

### Guild Facilities

**Stonehold Headquarters**: Main guildhall in **Stonehold**'s craftsman district. Features training halls, workshops, libraries, and the sacred forge where **Grimjaw** worked.

**Silverhaven Branch**: Commercial workshop handling surface contracts. **Queen Elara**'s court commissions weapons and armor from this branch.

**Thaldor Research Center**: Collaborative facility with **Arcane College of Thaldor**. Researchers study runemagic-Weave integration under **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**'s oversight.

## Goals and Philosophy

### Preservation of Tradition

**Grimjaw's Techniques**: The guild maintains exact records of **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s methods, ensuring no degradation over generations. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** blesses these records annually.

**Quality Standards**: Guild items bear the Ironhand mark only after rigorous testing. Substandard work results in expulsion. **King Thorin Stoneforge** backs guild quality enforcement.

**Cultural Balance**: The guild teaches non-dwarves while preserving dwarven cultural context. **Grimjaw** believed runemagic transcended species but required cultural understanding.

### Innovation Within Tradition

**Weave Integration**: Guild researchers collaborate with **Arcane College** on integrating runemagic with Weave theory. **Throndor the Wise**'s framework guides this research.

**New Applications**: The guild develops runemagic for modern needs - naval enchantments for **Admiral Hornbreaker**, volcanic crafting for **Pyre the Unquenchable**, and architectural wards for **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s building projects.

**Ethical Boundaries**: The guild refuses contracts for weapons of mass destruction or cursed items. **Queen Elara**'s requests for siege runes were denied; she respected the decision.

## Membership and Recruitment

### Admission Requirements

**Natural Aptitude**: Candidates must demonstrate innate magical sensitivity and crafting skill. **Gnomes** and **Fire Genasi** often show high aptitude.

**Character Evaluation**: The guild investigates candidates' backgrounds. Criminal records or oath-breaking disqualify applicants. **General Ironfist** assists with background checks.

**Sponsorship**: Existing members must sponsor candidates. **Grimjaw** originally sponsored non-dwarves; modern masters continue this tradition for worthy applicants.

### Training Program

**Decade of Theory**: Apprentices study runic alphabets, magical theory, materials science, and **Grimjaw**'s treatises. **Arcane College** professors guest-lecture on Weave theory.

**Decade of Practice**: Apprentices create increasingly complex enchanted items under master supervision. Final projects must equal journeyman-level work.

**Masterwork Examination**: Candidates create masterwork items examined by all 12 masters. **King Thorin Stoneforge** observes final examinations occasionally.

### Membership Demographics

**Dwarves** (70%): Majority membership, cultural inheritors of runemagic tradition.

**Gnomes** (15%): High aptitude for intricate rune inscription. **Thaldor**'s **Gnomish Innovation District** members often train with the guild.

**Humans** (10%): **Queen Elara**'s sponsors send promising enchanters. **Silverhaven University** students apprentice with the guild.

**Other Species** (5%): **Fire Genasi**, **elves**, and rare **Dragonborn** members. **Pyre the Unquenchable** holds honorary membership.

## Allies and Enemies

### Strategic Allies

**Arcane College of Thaldor**: Research partnership dating to **Throndor the Wise** and **Grimjaw Ironhand**. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** maintains the alliance.

**King Thorin Stoneforge**: Royal patronage and protection. The king funds guild operations and enforces quality standards.

**High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**: Spiritual support; blesses guild operations and sacred forges. The priesthood and guild coordinate on artifact authentication.

**General Ironfist**: Military contracts for fortification wards and weapon enchantments. **Thunder Pass** defenses feature guild work.

### Cautious Partners

**Queen Elara**: Human ruler respects guild independence while commissioning work. Diplomatic tensions arise when she requests restricted enchantments.

**Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**: **High Elf** cooperation on rune-spell hybrids. Elven isolationism limits deep partnership.

**Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**: **Catfolk** trader distributes guild items. Commercial interests sometimes conflict with quality standards.

### Ideological Opponents

**Unauthorized Runemakers**: Independent practitioners ignoring guild standards. **King Thorin Stoneforge** prosecutes illegal runemagic.

**Shadowfen Collective**: **Malakor Shadowdancer** seeks runemagic for shadow operations. The guild refuses all **Dark Elf** requests.

**Arcane College Radicals**: Some mages want runemagic fully open-sourced. **Throndor the Wise**'s memory keeps most in check.

## Notable Members

### Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge

**Grimjaw Ironhand**'s final apprentice and successor. Thrain studied under Grimjaw for 80 years, mastering all known runemagic techniques. He maintains **Grimjaw**'s balance between tradition and innovation.

**Achievements**:
- Maintained **Stonehold Treasury** wards for 50 years
- Trained 200+ apprentices to journeyman status
- Negotiated **Arcane College** research partnerships
- Advised **King Thorin Stoneforge** on runemagic policy
- Preserved **Grimjaw**'s original techniques without degradation

### Master Runehammer

**Gnome** runemaster specializing in miniature runes for jewelry and small items. Master Runehammer's work supplies **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s luxury markets.

### Journeyman Steelshaper

**Human** member operating the **Silverhaven** branch. Journeyman Steelshaper enchants **Queen Elara**'s royal guard equipment and maintains **Silverhaven Grand Temple** wards.

## Notable Operations

### Stonehold Treasury Maintenance

The guild maintains **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s original runewards protecting the **Stonehold Treasury**. Annual maintenance requires 12 masters working for 30 days. **King Thorin Stoneforge** funds this as highest priority.

### Silverhaven Defensive Wards

Guild members maintain the city wards **Grimjaw** designed during the **War of Shattered Skies**. **General Ironfist** coordinates with the guild on military upgrades.

### Arcane College Collaboration

**Thaldor** research center studies runemagic-Weave integration. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** and Guildmaster Thain co-author research papers.

### Thunder Pass Fortifications

**General Ironfist** commissioned guild members to enchant **Thunder Pass** defenses. The fortifications have never been breached.
