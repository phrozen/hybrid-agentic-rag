# The Eight Schools of Magic

## Overview

The Eight Schools of Magic form the foundational framework of magical theory in Aethelgard, standardized by **Throndor the Wise** at the **Arcane College of Thaldor** in 350 AS. This classification system organizes all known magical practices into eight distinct schools based on their effects, methods, and underlying principles. The system is taught at the **Arcane College**, the **Academy of Stars** in **Silvermoon**, and by dwarven runemasters in **Stonehold**, though each culture emphasizes different aspects.

## The Eight Schools

### 1. Abjuration (Protective Magic)

**Purpose:** Protection, warding, banishing, magical barriers

**Key Principles:**
- Defensive magic primary
- Ward creation and maintenance
- Magical dispelling
- Banishing extraplanar entities
- Protective enchantments

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Throndor the Wise** (master of abjuration)
- **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**
- **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** (elven wards)
- **Stonehold** runeward specialists

**Applications:**
- **Thunder Pass** fortifications
- **Moonwell** protection wards
- **Arcane College** security
- **Treaty of Silverhaven** magical enforcement
- Personal protection amulets

### 2. Conjuration (Summoning Magic)

**Purpose:** Creating objects, summoning creatures, teleportation

**Key Principles:**
- Object creation from nothing
- Creature summoning (elementals, spirits)
- Teleportation circles
- Gate creation
- Planar binding

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Archmage Summoner Vex** (**Thaldor** faculty)
- **Tiefling** planar scholars
- **Lizardfolk** spirit shamans

**Applications:**
- **Arcane College** summoning circles
- **Planar Gateway** research
- Merchant teleportation (limited)
- Emergency rescue spells
- Elemental servitors

### 3. Divination (Information Magic)

**Purpose:** Scrying, prophecy, truth-seeking, information gathering

**Key Principles:**
- Future sight (limited, cryptic)
- Remote viewing (scrying)
- Truth detection
- Past viewing (historical)
- Aura reading

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Throndor the Wise** (pioneering work)
- **Elven** seers (**Silvermoon**)
- **Catfolk** information brokers
- **Oracle Mirrorsong** (**Thaldor**)

**Applications:**
- **Moonwell** divinations
- **Bridge of Whispers** intelligence
- **Catfolk** trade networks
- **Queen Elara**'s spy network
- Archaeological research

### 4. Enchantment (Mind Magic)

**Purpose:** Mind control, charm, compulsion, emotional manipulation

**Key Principles:**
- Mind influence (subtle to absolute)
- Emotional manipulation
- Memory modification
- Compulsion spells
- Charm effects

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Enchanter Elysia** (**Thaldor**, controversial)
- **Elven** beguilers (**Western Reaches**)
- **Shadowfen Collective** assassins

**Applications:**
- Diplomatic negotiations (ethical concerns)
- Interrogation (regulated)
- Performance enhancement
- Crowd control
- **The Great Persecution** (misused)

### 5. Evocation (Energy Magic)

**Purpose:** Energy manipulation, elemental forces, destructive magic

**Key Principles:**
- Fire, cold, lightning, force
- Energy projection
- Area damage
- Weapon enhancement
- Elemental control

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Pyre the Unquenchable** (**Fire Genasi**, **Everflame Peak**)
- **Battle-mage Thunderstrike** (**Thaldor**)
- **Dragonborn** shamans

**Applications:**
- **Arcane College Security Force** combat magic
- **Stonehold** weapon enchantments
- **Everflame Peak** forges
- Military applications
- Mining (controlled explosions)

### 6. Illusion (Deception Magic)

**Purpose:** Sensory deception, invisibility, disguise, phantasms

**Key Principles:**
- Visual illusions
- Auditory deception
- Invisibility spells
- Disguise magic
- Phantasmal constructs

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Master Illusionist Shade** (**Thaldor**)
- **Dark Elf** shadowmancers (**Shadowfen**)
- **Catfolk** stealth experts

**Applications:**
- **Forest Wardens** camouflage
- **Shadowfen Collective** operations
- **Tiefling Refugee Network** safe houses
- Entertainment industry
- Military reconnaissance

### 7. Necromancy (Death Magic)

**Purpose:** Death, undeath, soul manipulation, life force

**Key Principles:**
- Undead creation (regulated)
- Soul binding (forbidden)
- Life drain
- Death touch
- Spirit communication

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Necromancer Mortis** (**Thaldor**, restricted)
- **Order of the Silver Dawn** (spirit communication)
- **Shaman Vessska** (**Lizardfolk**, ancestral)

**Applications:**
- **Order of the Radiant Dawn** spirit work
- Ancestral communication (ethical)
- **The Great Persecution** victims memorialized
- Medical applications (life force)
- **Arcane College** research (heavily regulated)

### 8. Transmutation (Change Magic)

**Purpose:** Transformation, polymorph, matter manipulation, enhancement

**Key Principles:**
- Shape changing
- Matter transformation
- Physical enhancement
- Object transmutation
- Alchemical processes

**Notable Practitioners:**
- **Transmuter Gold** (**Thaldor**)
- **Grimjaw Ironhand** (runemagic transmutation)
- **Alchemist Silverleaf** (**Western Reaches**)

**Applications:**
- **Stonehold** metalworking enhancement
- **Ironhand Guild** runemagic
- **Poppy Greenfields** agricultural magic
- **Crystal Bay** shipbuilding
- Medical prosthetics

## Historical Development

### Pre-Throndor Era (Pre-342 AS)

**Chaotic Classification:**
- Magic categorized by effect only
- No unified theory
- Species-specific systems
- Conflicting terminologies
- Knowledge fragmented

**Species Traditions:**
- **Elves**: Moon-based, nature magic
- **Dwarves**: Runemagic, earth-based
- **Humans**: Elemental, practical
- **Dragonborn**: Ancestral, fire-based
- **Lizardfolk**: Spirit, swamp magic

### Throndor's Standardization (342-350 AS)

**The Classification Project:**
- **Throndor the Wise** led research
- Studied all known magical traditions
- Identified common principles
- Created eight-school framework
- Published "Foundations of Weave Manipulation"

**Arcane College Adoption:**
- 350 AS: Official curriculum
- Faculty trained in new system
- Textbooks written
- Research reorganized
- Students benefited from clarity

### Modern Era (350-470 AS)

**Widespread Acceptance:**
- **Eastern Kingdoms**: Universal adoption
- **Western Reaches**: **Academy of Stars** uses (modified)
- **Stonehold**: Dwarven integration
- **Southern Expanse**: Growing acceptance
- **Arcane College** standard

**Ongoing Refinements:**
- **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** updates
- New subschools identified
- Cross-school synergies discovered
- Ethical guidelines developed
- International cooperation

## Ethical Considerations

### Regulated Schools

**Necromancy:**
- Undead creation: Forbidden (mostly)
- Spirit communication: Permitted (respectful)
- Life drain: Absolutely forbidden
- Death magic: Heavily regulated
- **Order of the Silver Dawn** oversight

**Enchantment:**
- Mind control: Forbidden without consent
- Charm spells: Regulated
- Memory modification: Forbidden
- Emotional manipulation: Ethical boundaries
- **Queen Elara**'s laws strict

**Conjuration:**
- Planar binding: Permitted (consensual)
- Creature summoning: Regulated
- Gate creation: Dangerous (restricted)
- Teleportation: Licensed only
- **Arcane College Security** enforcement

### Forbidden Practices

**Soul Magic:**
- Soul binding: Capital crime
- Soul trapping: Forbidden
- Necromantic servitude: Illegal
- **The Great Persecution** lessons applied
- **Order of the Radiant Dawn** monitors

**Reality Manipulation:**
- Time magic: Theoretically impossible
- Planar destruction: Forbidden
- Mass enchantment: Illegal
- Wild magic creation: Dangerous
- **Shattered Isles** warnings heeded
