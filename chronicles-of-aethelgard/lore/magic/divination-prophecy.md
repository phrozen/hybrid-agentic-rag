# Divination and Prophecy

## Overview

Divination magic enables scrying, prophecy, and information gathering, with **Oracle Mirrorsong** at **Thaldor** among its foremost practitioners. The school faces ethical debates about privacy and free will, with **Queen Elara** regulating divination used for intelligence gathering. The **Moonwell** amplifies divination magic, and **Catfolk** information brokers use minor divination in their networks.

## Origins and History

Divination ranks among the oldest magical traditions in Aethelgard, predating the formalization of the eight schools by **Throndor the Wise**. Elven seers in **Silvermoon** practiced prophecy through lunar observation centuries before the **Arcane College of Thaldor** was founded, and **Lizardfolk** shamans read the patterns of swamp waters and animal movements for guidance. The **Moonwell**, a sacred site now under **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s protection, has amplified divination magic since before elven records begin.

**Throndor the Wise** incorporated divination into his eight-school framework during the **Arcane College**'s founding, but he also established the first regulations governing its use. His original treatise included warnings about scrying as an invasion of privacy and prophecy as a potential tool of political manipulation. These concerns proved prescient when subsequent centuries saw diviners employed as spies, inquisitors, and propagandists.

**Oracle Mirrorsong** emerged as the most prominent diviner of the modern era, operating from the **Arcane College of Thaldor** while maintaining professional independence. Her prophecies, delivered in cryptic verse, have guided **Queen Elara**'s policies on several occasions. During the **War of Shattered Skies**, diviners on all sides provided critical intelligence, and post-war regulations increased significantly.

**Queen Elara**'s intelligence apparatus employs diviners under strict legal frameworks that limit domestic scrying, though enforcement remains contested. The **Catfolk** information broker networks, led by figures like **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**, use minor divination cantrips to gather market intelligence and political rumors, operating in a legal gray area that concerns regulators.

## Core Principles

Divination operates through three primary methods, each accessing the **Weave** differently. Scrying uses the Weave's informational threads to perceive distant events in real time, allowing practitioners to observe remote locations, conversations, or activities. Scrying requires a connection to the target—either personal familiarity, a possession, or geographic knowledge—and can be blocked by abjuration wards like those **Throndor the Wise** developed.

Prophecy accesses the Weave's temporal dimension, perceiving possible futures rather than current events. Prophecies are inherently uncertain because the Weave's future threads remain mutable. **Oracle Mirrorsong** emphasizes that prophecy reveals probabilities, not certainties, and that acting on prophecy often changes the outcome predicted.

The third method, psychometry, reads the Weave's impression on objects and locations, extracting historical information from places and things. **Lizardfolk** shamans practice a form of psychometry through spirit communication, while **Arcane College** researchers use more systematic approaches. The **Moonwell** enhances all three methods, making it the most powerful divination site in Aethelgard.

## Practices and Traditions

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** teaches divination as a structured discipline requiring years of meditative practice and theoretical study. Students begin with minor detection spells before progressing to scrying and eventually prophecy. **Oracle Mirrorsong** supervises the advanced program, accepting only students who demonstrate natural aptitude and, crucially, ethical judgment.

Elven divination traditions at the **Academy of Stars** emphasize intuitive and lunar methods. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s seers conduct major readings during full moons at the **Moonwell**, where divination magic reaches peak strength. These ceremonies blend religious devotion to Corellon with practical information gathering, reflecting the elven view that prophecy and faith are inseparable.

**Lizardfolk** shamanic divination, practiced by **Shaman Vessska** and others, combines spirit communication with natural observation. Rather than scrying through the Weave directly, **Lizardfolk** shamans ask ancestral spirits to share knowledge, and read patterns in water, weather, and animal behavior. While **Arcane College** researchers consider these methods imprecise, practitioners argue their traditions access information that formal divination misses.

**Catfolk** information brokers employ minor divination in their trade networks, using cheap scrying cantrips to monitor shipments, overhear negotiations, and track market movements. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** has formalized this practice into an intelligence service that rivals **Queen Elara**'s official diviners in some domains.

## Notable Practitioners

**Oracle Mirrorsong** is Aethelgard's most renowned living diviner, known for her cryptic but accurate prophecies and her strict ethical standards. She splits her time between the **Arcane College of Thaldor** and private consultations with heads of state.

**Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** practices elven divination specifically through lunar methods, conducting significant readings at the **Moonwell** during astronomical events. Her centuries of experience give her interpretations rare depth. **Shaman Vessska** represents the shamanic divination tradition, communicating with ancestral spirits for guidance that serves **Chief Ssithik** and the **Lizardfolk** of **Ssithik's Landing**.

**Throndor the Wise** contributed foundational divination theory, including the classification of divination methods and the development of counter-divination wards. His **Throndor's Mirror Ward** remains the standard defense against scrying.

## Related Factions

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** regulates divination practice and research, maintaining ethical guidelines that all licensed diviners must follow. **Queen Elara**'s government employs diviners in intelligence roles under legal supervision. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** uses divination for humanitarian purposes—locating lost persons, detecting threats to communities, and guiding relief efforts.

The **Catfolk** information networks operate semi-legal divination services through **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s organization. The **Academy of Stars** conducts pure research into divination theory. The **Forest Wardens** use divination to monitor threats in **Veilwood Forest**, and the **Ironhand Guild** employs diviners to assess mineral deposits and structural integrity in **Stonehold**.

## Cultural Impact

Divination's most significant cultural impact is the tension between knowledge and privacy. **Queen Elara**'s regulations attempt to balance legitimate security needs against individual rights, but the debate remains unresolved. Citizens of **Silverhaven** and other major cities live with the awareness that skilled diviners could observe their private moments, and counter-divination measures have become a thriving industry.

Prophecy affects politics and warfare at the highest levels. **Oracle Mirrorsong**'s predictions influence **Queen Elara**'s strategic decisions, while military leaders seek divinatory intelligence before major campaigns. The belief that the future can be known—however imperfectly—shapes Aethelgard's approach to governance, diplomacy, and conflict in ways both beneficial and dangerous.
