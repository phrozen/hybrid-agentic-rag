# Planar Connections and the Outer Planes

## Overview

Aethelgard exists on the Material Plane but connects to various Outer Planes through magical gateways, with **tieflings** tracing heritage to infernal planes and **aasimar** to celestial realms. The **Planar Gateway** in the **Shattered Isles** enables research into planar mechanics, while **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** monitors Feywild crossings in **Veilwood Forest**. **The Great Persecution** targeted planar-touched species due to fear of these connections.

## Origins and History

Planar connections trace back to **the Sundering**, when the catastrophic magical event tore holes between the Material Plane and surrounding Outer Planes. Before the Sundering, planar boundaries were stable and rarely permeable. The disaster created permanent tears at locations like the **Shattered Isles**, where the **Planar Gateway** now stands as a controlled access point to what was once a dangerous rift.

**Throndor the Wise** studied planar connections extensively during the early years of the **Arcane College of Thaldor**, classifying the known Outer Planes into celestial, infernal, elemental, and fey categories. His "Treatise on Interplanar Topology" remains the foundational text for planar research. **Tieflings**, whose ancestors were touched by infernal planar energy during and after the Sundering, carry heritage that manifests in their appearance and innate magical abilities. **Aasimar** similarly trace their celestial connections to planar bleedthrough from Upper Planes.

During **The Great Persecution**, fear of planar connections drove widespread discrimination against planar-touched species. **Tieflings** were driven from many settlements and forced into **Infernia**, while **aasimar** faced suspicion about celestial loyalties. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** was established to protect these communities and advocate for their rights.

## Core Principles

Planar connections operate on several key principles that govern how the Material Plane interacts with Outer Planes. The first principle is permeability: planar boundaries are not uniform walls but varying membranes, thin at some locations and thick at others. The **Shattered Isles** represent the thinnest boundaries, while regions like **Stonehold**'s mountain depths are among the thickest.

The second principle is resonance: certain individuals and materials resonate with specific planes. **Tieflings** resonate with infernal frequencies, granting them resistance to fire and access to innate hellish magic. **Aasimar** resonate with celestial vibrations, enabling healing abilities and radiant power. **Fire Genasi** resonate with the Plane of Fire, as seen in **Pyre the Unquenchable**'s extraordinary evocation talents.

The third principle is contagion: prolonged planar exposure alters local reality. The **Shattered Isles** exhibit flora and fauna warped by centuries of planar bleedthrough, creating creatures found nowhere else in Aethelgard. The **Arcane College** monitors these effects carefully.

## Practices and Traditions

Planar magic practitioners fall into several distinct traditions. Conjurors at the **Arcane College of Thaldor** study controlled planar gateways, learning to open temporary portals for travel, summoning, and communication. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** oversees the College's planar research division, which includes safeguards against uncontrolled planar breaches.

The **Planar Gateway** research facility in the **Shattered Isles** operates under strict protocols established after a containment failure three decades ago. Researchers there catalog planar phenomena, study wild magic zone interactions, and develop new warding techniques. The facility employs specialists from multiple species, including **tiefling** scholars whose innate planar resistance proves invaluable in hazardous research conditions.

Elven planar traditions focus almost exclusively on the Feywild. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintains a network of **Forest Wardens** who monitor Feywild crossings throughout the **Veilwood Forest** and **Whispering Woods**. These wardens patrol known crossing points, document shifts in fey activity, and intervene when dangerous fey creatures cross into the Material Plane.

## Notable Practitioners

**Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** stands as the foremost authority on Feywild connections, maintaining elven protocols for safe fey interaction. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** leads the **Arcane College**'s planar research division, combining theoretical knowledge with practical warding expertise. The **tiefling** community in **Infernia** has produced several notable planar scholars, including researchers who helped establish the **Planar Gateway** safety protocols.

**Nightwhisper**, the **tiefling** abjurer, contributed significantly to modern planar warding techniques, developing methods that protect against infernal intrusion while allowing legitimate planar magic. **Shaman Vessska** works with spirit worlds that occupy a unique position between the Material Plane and other Outer Planes, traditions the **Lizardfolk** have maintained for generations.

## Related Factions

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** regulates all planar research and maintains the most comprehensive library on planar theory. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** advocates for planar-touched species and maintains safe havens in **Silverhaven** and other cities. The **Forest Wardens** under **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** manage Feywild border security.

The **Ironhand Guild** in **Stonehold** contributes runemagic-based warding techniques used in planar containment. **Queen Elara**'s government funds the **Planar Gateway** research facility and enforces regulations on unauthorized planar experimentation. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** provides humanitarian support to communities affected by planar phenomena.

## Cultural Impact

Planar connections profoundly shape Aethelgard's demographics and politics. **Tiefling** and **aasimar** communities exist because of planar bleedthrough, and their experiences during **The Great Persecution** continue to influence inter-species relations. The **Shattered Isles** remain a dangerous but lucrative destination for researchers and adventurers seeking planar knowledge and materials.

Trade in planar goods passes through regulated channels in **Silverhaven** and **Thaldor**, where **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** information brokers track the movement of exotic planar materials. The **Dragonborn** temples of **Bahamut** in **Drakkenheim** teach that planar connections carry moral responsibilities, a philosophy that influences their paladin orders.
