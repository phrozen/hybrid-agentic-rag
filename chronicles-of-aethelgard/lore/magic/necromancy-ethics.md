# Necromancy: Ethics and Regulation

## Overview

Necromancy remains Aethelgard's most controversial magical school, heavily regulated by the **Arcane College of Thaldor** and monitored by the **Order of the Silver Dawn** following abuses during **The Great Persecution**. Legitimate necromantic research focuses on spirit communication and memorial magic, while undead creation is forbidden except in extreme circumstances. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** advocates for ethical necromancy that respects the dead.

## Origins and History

Necromancy has existed since before **the Sundering**, originally practiced as a form of ancestor veneration and spirit communication. Early **Lizardfolk** shamans, dwarven tomb-keepers, and elven mourners all practiced variants of what would eventually be classified as necromancy. These traditions treated the dead with reverence, believing that the spirits of the deceased could offer wisdom and guidance to the living.

**The Sundering** transformed necromancy profoundly. The massive loss of life and the disruption of the **Weave** created ideal conditions for undead manifestation, and opportunistic mages began animating corpses for labor and military purposes. The centuries following the Sundering saw increasingly abusive necromantic practices, culminating in organized undead creation during regional conflicts. The dead were used as tireless soldiers, disposable labor, and instruments of terror.

**The Great Persecution** represented the nadir of necromantic abuse. Rogue necromancers employed undead enforcers against **tiefling** communities, animating the corpses of persecuted victims to terrorize survivors. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** and the **Order of the Radiant Dawn** were formed partially in response to these atrocities, and the **Arcane College of Thaldor** instituted the strict regulatory framework that governs necromancy today.

Modern necromantic regulation balances legitimate research against the potential for abuse. The **Arcane College** permits spirit communication under supervision, memorial magic that preserves memories of the deceased, and emergency undead animation solely for defense against existential threats. **Throndor the Wise** established these categories, and **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** enforces them rigorously.

## Core Principles

Ethical necromancy, as defined by the **Arcane College** regulatory framework, rests on three foundational principles. The first is consent: no spirit may be contacted against its will, and no corpse may be animated without the deceased's prior written authorization or explicit familial consent. The College maintains a registry of individuals who have provided advance consent for posthumous spirit communication.

The second principle is dignity: even when necromantic practices are permitted, they must respect the sanctity of death and the feelings of the living. Undead animation for labor or combat violates this principle by treating human remains as tools. Memorial magic, which preserves the essence of a person's memory without trapping their spirit, satisfies this principle.

The third principle is limitation: all necromantic effects must be temporary, reversible, and contained. Permanent undead creation is categorically forbidden. Spirit communication sessions must have defined endpoints. Any necromantic effect must have a predetermined termination condition. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** enforces these limitations with regular inspections of licensed necromancers.

## Practices and Traditions

Legitimate necromantic practice in modern Aethelgard encompasses several regulated disciplines. Spirit communication, the most common form, allows bereaved families to speak with deceased loved ones one final time. Licensed facilitators at the **Arcane College** and **Order of the Silver Dawn** temples conduct these sessions under strict supervision, ensuring that spirits are contacted willingly and released promptly.

Memorial magic preserves the memories and personality impressions of the deceased in crystalline matrices, creating records that historians and genealogists can access without disturbing actual spirits. The **Arcane College** maintains an extensive memorial archive, and the **Order of the Sacred Anvil** incorporates memorial magic into dwarven tomb traditions.

Battlefield necromancy remains the most controversial legitimate application. During the **War of Shattered Skies**, emergency protocols permitted temporary undead animation for defense against overwhelming odds. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** supervised these animations, ensuring that animated soldiers were destroyed immediately after combat ended. This precedent remains contentious, with opponents arguing that no circumstance justifies undead creation and supporters maintaining that defense of the living takes priority.

**Lizardfolk** spirit communication practices, led by **Shaman Vessska**, occupy a unique position in the regulatory framework. These traditions involve ongoing relationships with ancestral spirits rather than one-time contact. The **Arcane College** has granted exemptions for **Lizardfolk** shamanic practices after determining they operate on fundamentally different principles than formal necromancy.

## Notable Practitioners

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** maintains a small but rigorously vetted necromancy department. Its faculty specialize in spirit communication methodology, memorial magic technique, and regulatory enforcement. The department operates under constant oversight from **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** and **Order of the Silver Dawn** observers.

**Shaman Vessska** practices the **Lizardfolk** tradition of ancestral spirit communication, maintaining ongoing relationships with lineage spirits rather than conducting one-time sessions. Her work has contributed to **Arcane College** understanding of alternative necromantic frameworks and influenced the exemption policies for traditional practices.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** trains specialists in ethical necromancy, emphasizing the distinction between respectful spirit communication and the exploitative practices that marked **The Great Persecution**. These specialists serve as consultants for the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s enforcement efforts and as grief counselors for bereaved families.

## Related Factions

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** holds regulatory authority over all necromantic practice, licensing practitioners and enforcing ethical standards. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** monitors necromancy on behalf of the crown, investigating illegal practice and providing humanitarian oversight. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** advocates for ethical necromancy and assists in enforcement.

**Queen Elara**'s government maintains the legal framework criminalizing unauthorized undead creation, with severe penalties including permanent magical ability suppression. The **Ironhand Guild** provides tomb warding services that prevent unauthorized necromantic access to dwarven dead. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** enforces elven prohibitions against any form of undead within **Western Reaches** territory.

## Cultural Impact

Necromancy's cultural impact is defined primarily by fear and caution. The abuses of **The Great Persecution** remain within living memory for longer-lived species, and communities that suffered under undead enforcers view all necromancy with suspicion. **Tiefling** communities in **Infernia** are particularly skeptical of regulated necromancy, given their historical experience with its worst applications.

Despite this fear, legitimate necromancy provides genuine comfort and utility. Memorial magic allows future generations to experience the wisdom and personality of historical figures. Spirit communication provides closure for grieving families. The **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s careful stewardship of these practices has gradually built public trust, though deep-seated prejudices persist, particularly in rural communities where folk traditions equate all necromancy with evil.
