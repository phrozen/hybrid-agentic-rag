# The Bahamut Dragon Temples

## Overview

Bahamut Dragon Temples serve **Dragonborn** worshippers across the **Southern Expanse**, with the primary temple in **Drakkenheim** under **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s patronage. The temples house dragon eggs, maintain ancestral records, and train paladins dedicated to justice. **Bahamut**'s clergy opposes tyranny and protects the weak, influencing **Dragonborn** participation in anti-piracy efforts.

## Domains and Portfolios

Bahamut the Platinum Dragon governs domains essential to **Dragonborn** identity and civilization. Justice stands paramount among these—Bahamut's teachings hold that the strong must protect the weak, that truth must prevail over deception, and that law must serve all people equally. This domain produces the paladin orders that are Bahamut's most visible manifestation in Aethelgard, warriors who combine martial skill with divine channeling to pursue justice against tyrants and oppressors.

The Protection domain complements Justice by emphasizing defensive power. Bahamut does not teach aggression for its own sake; rather, his followers protect the innocent, shield communities from harm, and stand as living barriers against evil. The temples' dragon egg guardians exemplify this domain—dedicated protectors who safeguard the future of **Dragonborn** civilization. The **Southern Expanse**'s relative stability owes much to Bahamut's protective influence through his paladins and clergy.

The Dragons domain connects Bahamut's worshippers to their draconic heritage, preserving the memory of the dragons who once dominated Aethelgard and the **Dragonborn** who served alongside them. This domain provides magical abilities related to draconic power—breath weapon resistance, enhanced senses, and communication with dragon-kind. The **Dragon Wastes** remain sacred under this domain, preserved as memorials to the lost draconic civilization.

The Honor domain governs oath-keeping, personal integrity, and the **Dragonborn** code of conduct that makes their word an unbreakable bond. Bahamut's emphasis on honor influences **Dragonborn** participation in diplomacy, trade, and military alliances; a **Dragonborn** oath carries weight across Aethelgard precisely because Bahamut's followers treat their commitments as sacred obligations enforced by divine power.

## Worship and Rituals

Bahamut's worship centers on oaths, justice, and draconic heritage. The most significant ritual is the Oath of the Scales, taken by every **Dragonborn** at coming of age. The oath commits the individual to justice, protection, honor, and service to community. It is sworn before the temple altar with a **Dragonborn**'s ancestors' records present, connecting the oath-taker to generations of predecessors who swore the same commitment.

Weekly justice ceremonies at the **Drakkenheim** temple provide a forum for resolving disputes, hearing grievances, and renewing communal commitments to fairness. **Lady Zara Goldscale** presides over these ceremonies personally, her authority reinforced by Bahamut's divine endorsement of just leadership. The proceedings combine legal arbitration with religious solemnity, reflecting the **Dragonborn** integration of faith and governance.

The annual **Dragon Remembrance** ceremony honors the lost draconic civilization and the **Dragonborn** duty to carry draconic heritage forward. Held in the **Dragon Wastes**, this pilgrimage reinforces the connection between present **Dragonborn** and their mythological ancestors. The Remembrance includes storytelling, oath renewal, and the lighting of ceremonial fires that symbolize draconic breath continuing to illuminate the world.

Military readiness ceremonies, conducted monthly, bless **Dragonborn** warriors and paladins for their protective duties. These ceremonies combine combat drilling with religious devotion, reinforcing the **Dragonborn** conviction that military skill serves divine purpose. The **anti-piracy patrols** in the **Southern Expanse** waters receive Bahamut's blessing before each deployment, linking maritime security to religious duty.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The **Primary Dragon Temple** in **Drakkenheim** is the center of Bahamut worship, a massive structure incorporating draconic architectural elements—soaring vaulted ceilings, scale-patterned stonework, and fire-resistant construction that honors draconic heritage. The temple houses the **Ancestral Records**, meticulously maintained genealogical archives that trace **Dragonborn** lineages back to the pre-Sundering era. **Lady Zara Goldscale** oversees temple operations with the High Scale Priests.

The **Dragon Egg Vaults** beneath the **Drakkenheim** temple represent the most sacred trust in **Dragonborn** religion. These climate-controlled chambers house preserved dragon eggs—dormant but not destroyed, maintained in the hope that dragons might someday return. The vault guardians are selected from the most devoted paladins, and their duty is considered the highest honor in Bahamut's service.

Regional temples throughout the **Southern Expanse** and beyond serve **Dragonborn** communities outside **Drakkenheim**. The **Crystal Bay** temple serves the maritime **Dragonborn** community, while **Sunscorch** maintains a desert temple adapted to the harsh climate. Each regional temple maintains its own copy of the **Ancestral Records** and coordinates with **Drakkenheim** on religious matters.

The **Dragon Wastes** themselves constitute Aethelgard's largest sacred site—hallowed ground where the ancient draconic civilization fell. Pilgrimages to the Wastes are undertaken by every devout **Dragonborn** at least once in their lifetime. The fossilized remains scattered across the landscape are treated with religious reverence; removing anything from the Wastes is considered a profound sacrilege.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Scale Priests** form the core of Bahamut's clergy, ordained through rigorous training in justice, protection, and draconic heritage. Scale Priests serve dual roles as religious leaders and legal authorities in **Dragonborn** communities, reflecting the faith's integration of spiritual and civic duty. Their vestments incorporate platinum and scale patterns, identifying them as Bahamut's representatives.

The **Platinum Paladins** are Bahamut's militant order, warriors who have sworn the most stringent oaths of justice, protection, and honor. Their training combines divine channeling with martial excellence, producing warriors who fight with both sword and faith. The **Paladins** lead **Dragonborn** participation in anti-piracy patrols and serve as guardians for the most vulnerable communities in the **Southern Expanse**.

The **Ancestral Keepers** maintain the **Ancestral Records** and **Dragon Egg Vaults**, serving as historians, genealogists, and guardians of **Dragonborn** heritage. This order requires the most rigorous training in Bahamut's lore, and its members are held to the highest standards of personal conduct. An Ancestral Keeper who violates their oath is stripped of office permanently—a consequence so severe that violations are virtually unknown.

**Lady Zara Goldscale** serves as both political leader and religious patron, embodying the **Dragonborn** conviction that just governance is itself a form of worship. Her support of Bahamut's temples ensures their financial stability and political influence, while the temples provide her with moral authority and the loyalty of oath-bound paladins.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

The **Platinum Codex** is Bahamut's primary sacred text, chronicling the history of dragon-kind, the **Dragonborn** creation myth, and the code of justice that governs faithful conduct. The Codex is maintained in multiple copies, each temple preserving its own version with local annotations. The **Drakkenheim** temple holds the original, written on platinum-inlaid tablets that **Dragonborn** tradition holds were inscribed by Bahamut's own claw.

Bahamut's primary symbol is the platinum dragon—a winged draconic form rendered in the precious metal that gives the deity his epithet. Platinum dragon imagery adorns every temple, appears on **Dragonborn** ceremonial armor, and features prominently in civic architecture throughout **Drakkenheim**.

Dragon scales, whetheractual shed scales from living dragons or symbolic representations, serve as secondary sacred symbols. **Dragonborn** carry personal scale tokens representing their individual oaths, and temple rituals often incorporate physical scales from the temple's collection of preserved draconic materials.

The **Scales of Justice**, depicting a balanced scale with a platinum dragon atop, represents Bahamut's Justice domain. This symbol appears in **Dragonborn** courtrooms, legal documents, and paladin regalia, reinforcing the connection between divine justice and civic order.

## Relations with Other Faiths

Bahamut's faith maintains the strongest interfaith relationship with **Pelor**'s human worship, sharing commitments to justice and protection that transcend species boundaries. **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Queen Elara** coordinate on humanitarian and anti-piracy efforts, their personal friendship reinforcing the institutional cooperation between their faiths.

With **Moradin**'s dwarven faith, relations are based on shared values of honor and craftsmanship. **Dragonborn** metalsmiths respect dwarven forging traditions, and the two faiths cooperate on practical matters including **Dragonborn** participation in **Ironhand Guild** projects. The primary theological difference—Bahamut's emphasis on justice versus Moradin's on creation—produces respectful debate rather than conflict.

**Corellon**'s elven faith presents more challenges. The **Dragonborn** emphasis on direct action and justice contrasts with elven preferences for subtlety and artistry. However, **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintain diplomatic courtesy, and their respective military forces cooperate on mutual security concerns.

**Lizardfolk** spirit traditions create the most complex interfaith dynamic. Both **Dragonborn** and **Lizardfolk** occupy the **Southern Expanse**, and their fundamentally different theological frameworks—deity worship versus spirit veneration—create misunderstandings. **Shaman Vessska** has worked patiently to explain spirit traditions to **Dragonborn** clergy, and progress toward mutual respect continues slowly.

## Mythology and Stories

Bahamut's founding myth describes the Platinum Dragon creating the **Dragonborn** from his own essence, instilling in them the draconic virtues of strength, honor, and justice. This creation narrative establishes the **Dragonborn** obligation to uphold justice as an expression of their very nature—they are just because they were made for justice, not because they choose it arbitrarily.

The **Fall of the Dragons** is the central tragic myth, recounting how the ancient draconic civilization was destroyed during **the Sundering** despite dragons' immense power. The myth teaches that even the mightiest can fall, and that justice and honor matter more than power. The **Dragon Wastes** serve as physical proof of this lesson—a civilization reduced to fossilized memories.

The **Platinum Oath** story tells of Bahamut's promise that **Dragonborn** will endure as long as they uphold justice, protecting the weak and opposing tyranny. This covenant explains the **Dragonborn** persistence after the dragons' fall and gives meaning to their continued existence. Every Oath of the Scales ceremony renews this covenant individually, connecting each **Dragonborn** to Bahamut's promise.

The story of **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s leadership during the **War of Shattered Skies** has already entered Bahamut's living mythology. Her defense of **Southern Expanse** communities against invasion, her coordination of anti-piracy fleets, and her unwavering commitment to protecting non-**Dragonborn** civilians are told as modern examples of Bahamut's principles in action. These contemporary stories reinforce the connection between ancient mythology and present obligation.
