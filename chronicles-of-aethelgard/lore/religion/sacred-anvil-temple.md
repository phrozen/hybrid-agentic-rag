# The Sacred Anvil Temple

## Overview

The Sacred Anvil Temple in **Stonehold** is the heart of dwarven faith where **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** serves **Moradin** and the **Ironhand Guild** blesses runemagic work. The temple's **Forge Eternal** has burned for millennia, and **King Thorin Stoneforge** is crowned before Moradin's altar. The temple houses **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s memorial and trains dwarven priests.

## Domains and Portfolios

The Sacred Anvil Temple serves Moradin's four primary domains: Craftsmanship, Protection, Family, and Earth. The Craftsmanship domain is foremost—Moradin forged the first dwarves from iron and stone, and every act of creation honors this divine precedent. The temple's blessing of **Ironhand Guild** work connects craft to worship directly; every item forged in Moradin's name is both functional tool and devotional act. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s legendary masterworks are considered the finest examples of devotional craftsmanship in Aethelgard.

The Protection domain manifests in the temple's warding and fortification work. The Sacred Anvil itself radiates protective divine energy, and the temple's priests specialize in abjuration-based warding that protects **Stonehold**'s structures, vaults, and citizens. The **Thunder Pass** fortifications, blessed by Sacred Anvil clergy, combine dwarven runemagic with Moradin's protective domain to create defenses that have never been breached.

The Family domain governs dwarven kinship bonds, marriage, and lineage. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** personally blesses every dwarven marriage in **Stonehold**, and the temple maintains genealogical records that rival the **Hall of Archmages** in thoroughness. The **Ancestral Records** housed in the temple connect living dwarves to their predecessors, reinforcing the clan bonds that define dwarven society.

The Earth domain connects dwarven faith to the stone and metal from which Moradin created the dwarven race. Mining, stonework, and metalworking are all considered devotional acts under this domain. The temple's location deep within **Stonehold**'s mountain reflects this connection, embedding dwarven worship in the very earth Moradin governs.

## Worship and Rituals

Moradin's worship centers on the act of creation as devotional practice. The **Forge Eternal**, burning continuously since the temple's founding, symbolizes both Moradin's eternal creative fire and the dwarven commitment to productive labor. Every item forged in the Sacred Anvil's presence is considered blessed, and master smiths competing at the annual Forge Festival work before the Forge Eternal specifically to invoke Moradin's creative inspiration.

Daily worship at the Sacred Anvil Temple involves the Morning Hammering, a dawn ritual where temple clergy strike Moradin's anvil rhythmically, calling the dwarven community to work and worship simultaneously. The sound resonates through **Stonehold**'s stone corridors, marking the beginning of each day with a reminder that labor and devotion are inseparable under Moradin's faith.

The **Forge Festival**, held during the autumn equinox, is the dwarven religious calendar's most important event. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** opens the festival by lighting the ceremonial forge from the **Forge Eternal**'s flame, symbolizing the transmission of Moradin's creative fire to the community. Artisans spend days crafting their finest work in competition, and completed pieces are blessed and displayed before the community. **King Thorin Stoneforge** personally judges the most prestigious categories, connecting civic and religious authority.

Coronation ceremonies at the Sacred Anvil Temple connect dwarven political authority to divine endorsement. **King Thorin Stoneforge** was crowned before Moradin's altar, his crown blessed by **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and placed on his head by the senior Ancestral Keeper. This ceremony establishes the king's authority as divinely sanctioned, linking the monarch's legitimacy directly to Moradin's favor.

Marriage blessings, naming ceremonies for newborns, and funeral rites all take place at the Sacred Anvil Temple, integrating every major life event into Moradin's worship. Dwarves mark their lives through these temple ceremonies, creating a seamless connection between individual biography and religious practice.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The Sacred Anvil Temple itself is **Stonehold**'s most important structure, carved deep into the mountain heart at the intersection of the strongest **ley lines**. The temple's architecture embodies dwarven spiritual principles—massive stone pillars representing dwarven endurance, iron fixtures reflecting Moradin's forge, and deep chambers that connect worshippers to the earth domain. The main sanctuary houses the **Forge Eternal**, Moradin's Anvil, and the **Ancestral Records**.

The **Forge Eternal**, burning continuously for millennia, is the temple's holiest feature. The flame is never extinguished and cannot be extinguished by natural means—it is considered Moradin's own creative fire, maintained by divine will. When **Stonehold** faced siege during the **War of Shattered Skies**, the Forge Eternal's continued burning was taken as proof that Moradin protected the city.

Moradin's Anvil stands at the sanctuary's center, a massive iron anvil where sacred smithing occurs and where **King Thorin Stoneforge** was crowned. The Anvil is carved with runes that glow during ceremonies, believed to be Moradin's own inscription. Only **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and the most senior clergy may touch the Anvil directly.

The **Ancestral Records** occupy the temple's deepest chambers, climate-controlled vaults preserving genealogical records, clan histories, and personal effects of notable dwarves. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s memorial is housed here, alongside his finest preserved masterworks. These records serve both religious and practical purposes, connecting living dwarves to their ancestors and providing legal documentation of clan membership and inheritance rights.

Smaller shrines to Moradin exist throughout **Stonehold**'s mining tunnels and residential quarters, providing spaces for brief devotions during the workday. These shrines feature miniature anvils and eternal flames (lit from the Forge Eternal), allowing dwarves to honor Moradin without traveling to the main temple.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Order of the Sacred Anvil** serves as Moradin's primary religious organization, based in the Sacred Anvil Temple under **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**. The Order trains clergy in Moradin's domains, blesses **Ironhand Guild** work, advises **King Thorin Stoneforge** on religious matters, and maintains the **Ancestral Records**. Membership requires demonstrated skill in a craft, theological study, and community service.

**High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** is the most influential dwarven religious figure in Aethelgard. His authority extends beyond the Sacred Anvil Temple to all Moradin worshippers, and his counsel is sought by dwarves across the continent. **Thrain Sacredforge** was a master smith before his religious calling, and his understanding of both craftsmanship and faith informs his leadership.

The **Ironhand Guild** maintains a close relationship with the Sacred Anvil Temple, though it is a craft organization rather than a religious one. Guild masters participate in temple ceremonies, and the Guild's finest work is dedicated to Moradin before being sold or displayed. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s integration of runemagic with conventional enchantment was blessed by **Thrain Sacredforge**, establishing the theological precedent for runemagic as a devotional practice.

Ancestral Keepers are specialized clergy who maintain the **Ancestral Records**, perform funeral rites, and facilitate connection between living dwarves and their predecessors. Their work ensures that the Family domain remains vibrant, connecting genealogical knowledge with spiritual practice. The Keepers also preserve the personal effects of notable dwarves, creating a material archive that complements the written records.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

The **Forge Eternal Records** are Moradin's primary sacred texts, compiled over millennia from temple records, clan histories, and theological treatises. Unlike single-author scriptures, the Records are a living document, continuously updated with new theological reflections, craftsmanship innovations, and community milestones. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** personally oversees the Records' maintenance, ensuring that each addition meets the temple's scholarly standards.

Moradin's primary symbol is the hammer and anvil, representing the divine act of creation that forged the dwarven race. This symbol appears everywhere in dwarven architecture, on clergy vestments, and as personal devotional items. The crossed hammer and anvil specifically represents Moradin's creative power, while the anvil alone represents the stability and endurance that Moradin's worship provides.

Mountain peaks serve as secondary symbols, representing Moradin's domain over earth and stone. Temple architecture incorporates peak motifs, and the Sacred Anvil Temple itself is carved into a mountain peak, physically connecting worshippers to the earth domain. The **Stormpeak Mountains** visible from **Stonehold** are considered sacred landmarks, their silhouettes appearing in dwarven art and religious iconography.

Forged weapons and tools represent the Craftsmanship domain at its most practical. A weapon forged in Moradin's name is simultaneously a functional tool and a devotional object, embodying the dwarven principle that creation serves both practical and spiritual purposes. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s masterworks, preserved in the **Ancestral Records** vault, are considered the finest examples of devotional craftsmanship ever produced.

## Relations with Other Faiths

The Sacred Anvil Temple maintains **Stonehold**'s most important interfaith relationships through **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**'s diplomatic skill. With **Pelor**'s faith, relations are positive and practical, centered on shared values of protection and community service. **Thrain Sacredforge** and **Queen Elara**'s clergy cooperate on humanitarian projects, and the **Forge Festival** includes interfaith guests who appreciate the craftsmanship on display.

With **Corellon**'s elven faith, relations are cordial but philosophically strained. The dwarven emphasis on craftsmanship as worship contrasts with the elven emphasis on artistry, and **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintain diplomatic courtesy while acknowledging genuine differences. The most productive area of cooperation involves the **Ironhand Guild**'s practical metalwork and the **Academy of Stars**'s theoretical magic, which complement rather than conflict.

**Bahamut**'s **Dragonborn** faith shares the Sacred Anvil Temple's emphasis on honor and protection, creating natural alliance opportunities. **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Lady Zara Goldscale** have developed a productive diplomatic relationship, and their faiths cooperate on security matters in the **Southern Expanse** approaches.

**Lizardfolk** spirit traditions remain the most challenging relationship. The Sacred Anvil Temple's emphasis on structure, hierarchy, and crafted worship spaces fundamentally conflicts with **Lizardfolk** animistic, nature-centered practice. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** has authorized limited interfaith dialogue, but genuine theological engagement is minimal.

## Mythology and Stories

Moradin's founding myth describes the All-Father forging the first dwarves from iron and stone, breathing life into his creations with the same divine fire that burns in the **Forge Eternal**. This narrative establishes craftsmanship as the highest form of worship—every act of creation echoes Moradin's primordial forge, and every completed work honors the divine smith.

The story of Moradin's gift of the Sacred Anvil to the first dwarven community explains the temple's holiest object. According to tradition, Moradin himself placed the Anvil in **Stonehold**'s mountain heart, inscribing its runes with his own divine hammer. The Anvil's continued presence and power validate the dwarven belief that **Stonehold** is Moradin's chosen city.

**Grimjaw Ironhand**'s life has entered dwarven religious mythology as a story of devotional craftsmanship elevated to divine partnership. His legendary masterworks, preserved in the temple vault, are treated as religious relics—physical evidence that Moradin rewards faithful crafters with extraordinary ability. The annual **Forge Festival** includes retellings of **Ironhand**'s greatest creations, inspiring new generations of smiths.

The **War of Shattered Skies** produced new Sacred Anvil mythology: stories of the **Forge Eternal** burning through the siege, of dwarven smiths forging weapons under bombardment, of **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** blessing troops with Moradin's protective power. These narratives reinforce the connection between devotional craftsmanship and military defense, and they validate the **Order of the Sacred Anvil**'s dual role as both religious institution and defensive asset.
