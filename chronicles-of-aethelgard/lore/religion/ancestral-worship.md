# Ancestral Worship Traditions

## Overview

Ancestral worship forms the spiritual core of multiple Aethelgard species, with **Dragonborn** clans, **Lizardfolk** tribes, and dwarven holds all venerating their forebears through distinct traditions. **Chief Ssithik**'s **Lizardfolk** maintain **Shaman Vessska**'s spirit communication practices, while **Dragonborn** in **Drakkenheim** honor draconic ancestors. The **Order of the Sacred Anvil** incorporates ancestral veneration into dwarven faith, and **The Great Persecution** victims are memorialized by the **Order of the Radiant Dawn**.

## Domains and Portfolios

Ancestral worship across Aethelgard operates through domains that differ from deity-centered faiths while serving similar social functions. The Ancestor domain itself provides connection to predecessors who offer wisdom, guidance, and warnings from beyond death. For **Lizardfolk**, this domain is the primary spiritual channel—**Shaman Vessska** communicates directly with specific ancestors who address specific community concerns. The **Dragonborn** approach ancestors as exemplars of honor and duty rather than active advisors, invoking draconic ancestors as models for emulation rather than sources of direct guidance.

The Family domain extends ancestor veneration beyond individual predecessors to encompass the entire clan, lineage, or tribe. Dwarven ancestral worship under the **Order of the Sacred Anvil** connects living dwarves to their entire clan history through the **Ancestral Records**, creating a sense of identity that transcends individual lifetimes. **Dragonborn** clan records maintained in **Drakkenheim** temples serve similar functions, mapping each individual's obligation to the ancestors who preceded them.

The Memory domain preserves knowledge, skills, and cultural practices from previous generations. This domain operates differently across traditions: dwarven Memory is preserved in written records, **Dragonborn** Memory in oral and genealogical traditions, and **Lizardfolk** Memory in the direct testimony of ancestor spirits accessed through **Shaman Vessska**'s trances. Each method produces different relationships with the past—written records allow detailed research, oral traditions emphasize storytelling, and spirit communication provides direct but subjective ancestral testimony.

The Heritage domain governs the physical and cultural inheritance that ancestors leave to their descendants. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s masterworks are dwarven heritage artifacts, preserved as both religious relics and practical inspiration. The **Dragon Wastes** draconic remains are **Dragonborn** heritage, treated with reverence as physical connections to the lost draconic civilization. **Lizardfolk** heritage includes the ecological knowledge transmitted by ancestor spirits through **Shaman Vessska**.

## Worship and Rituals

Ancestral worship practices vary dramatically across Aethelgard's species, reflecting different relationships with the dead. Dwarven ancestral veneration occurs within the **Sacred Anvil Temple** framework, where **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** incorporates ancestor acknowledgment into every major life ceremony. Marriages include recognition of both families' ancestors, naming ceremonies invoke ancestral blessing, and funeral rites transition the deceased into ancestor status with formal ritual.

The **Dragonborn** practice of honoring draconic ancestors centers on the **Platinum Codex** genealogies and the **Dragon Remembrance** ceremony. **Lady Zara Goldscale** leads annual pilgrimages to the **Dragon Wastes**, where **Dragonborn** honor the ancient dragons whose fossilized remains testify to their lost civilization. The Oath of the Scales, taken at coming of age, explicitly invokes ancestral witnesses—each oath-taker swears before the memory of their predecessors.

**Lizardfolk** ancestor communication, led by **Shaman Vessska**, represents the most direct form of ancestral worship. During trance ceremonies, **Vessska** serves as a medium through which living **Lizardfolk** speak with specific deceased community members. These are not symbolic invocations but believed to be genuine conversations with ancestor spirits who retain personality, memory, and opinion. **Chief Ssithik** consults ancestors before major decisions, integrating their guidance into governance.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** practices a form of ancestral memorialization unique to **aasimar** and **tiefling** communities. Rather than venerating specific ancestors, they memorialize the collective victims of **The Great Persecution**, honoring those who suffered for their planar heritage. Annual remembrance ceremonies at the **Silverhaven Temple of Light** name individual victims and affirm the community's commitment to ensuring such persecution never recurs.

## Temples and Holy Sites

Dwarven ancestral veneration occurs at the **Sacred Anvil Temple**, where the **Ancestral Records** vault preserves genealogical documentation and the personal effects of notable dwarves. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s memorial is maintained here, his masterworks displayed as exemplars of devotional craftsmanship. The vault extends deep into **Stonehold**'s mountain, connecting the dead to the earth domain that Moradin governs. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** oversees the Records' maintenance, ensuring every significant dwarf is documented.

**Dragonborn** ancestral sites center on the **Dragon Wastes**, where fossilized draconic remains are treated with profound reverence. Removing anything from the Wastes is sacrilege; the remains are left undisturbed as memorials to the lost draconic civilization. The **Drakkenheim Dragon Temples** house **Ancestral Records** that trace **Dragonborn** lineages to pre-Sundering ancestors, maintained by Ancestral Keepers who consider their work sacred duty.

**Lizardfolk** ancestral sites are natural locations within the **Southern Expanse** wetlands—specific trees, rock formations, and pools associated with particular ancestors. **Shaman Vessska** maintains these sites during her travels through the swamp, ensuring they remain undisturbed and accessible. The sunken ruins of **Aqualonis** serve as the most significant ancestral site, where the spirits of the drowned city communicate with the living through **Vessska**'s intercession.

The **Silverhaven Temple of Light** houses the **Order of the Radiant Dawn**'s memorial to **The Great Persecution** victims. This site is unique in Aethelgard as an ancestral worship location for people connected by persecution rather than blood. The memorial includes names of known victims, tributes to those whose names are lost, and ongoing documentation of **tiefling** and **aasimar** family histories that persecution disrupted.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Order of the Sacred Anvil** incorporates ancestral veneration into every aspect of dwarven religious practice. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** personally officiates life ceremonies that reference ancestors, and the Order's Ancestral Keepers maintain the **Ancestral Records** and funerary practices. These specialized clergy serve both religious and secular functions, providing legal documentation of clan membership alongside spiritual connection to predecessors.

**Dragonborn** Ancestral Keepers in the **Drakkenheim Dragon Temples** maintain genealogical records that document every known **Dragonborn** lineage back to pre-Sundering ancestors. This role carries immense religious responsibility—the Ancestral Keepers ensure that the **Oath of the Scales** can invoke specific named ancestors, providing the genealogical foundation for **Dragonborn** honor culture.

**Shaman Vessska** serves as **Ssithik's Landing**'s primary connection to ancestor spirits, conducting trance ceremonies that allow direct communication between the living and the dead. Her role differs from clergy in deity-centered faiths—she doesn't interpret divine will but rather facilitates direct spirit access, allowing ancestors to speak for themselves.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** maintains memorial clergy who document the stories of **The Great Persecution** victims, conduct remembrance ceremonies, and support **tiefling** and **aasimar** families seeking to reconstruct disrupted lineages. This work represents an innovative form of ancestral worship, extending veneration to people connected by shared suffering rather than by blood.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

Each tradition maintains its own approach to preserving ancestral knowledge. The dwarven **Ancestral Records** are meticulous written documents stored in the **Sacred Anvil Temple** vaults, preserving genealogies, personal effects, and biographical information about notable dwarves. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** ensures these records meet exacting standards of accuracy and completeness.

**Dragonborn** **Ancestral Records** are maintained in the **Drakkenheim Dragon Temples**, genealogical archives that trace lineages back to pre-Sundering ancestors. These records are considered sacred texts in their own right, consulted during oath-swearing ceremonies and legal disputes where ancestral authority resolves conflicts. The **Platinum Codex** preserves the broader mythological framework within which individual genealogies are understood.

**Lizardfolk** ancestral knowledge is preserved entirely through oral tradition and direct spirit communication. **Shaman Vessska** serves as the primary repository of oral traditions, maintaining stories, ecological knowledge, and spirit communication protocols through memory rather than written documentation. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** has begun recording these traditions with **Vessska**'s permission, but the living practice of oral transmission remains primary.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn**'s memorial at the **Silverhaven Temple of Light** includes inscribed names, preserved personal effects, and documentary testimony about **The Great Persecution** victims. This collection represents ancestral documentation for people whose family records were destroyed by persecution, creating new heritage for communities that lost their past.

## Relations with Other Faiths

Ancestral worship traditions create both bridges and barriers between Aethelgard's faiths. The **Order of the Sacred Anvil** and the **Drakkenheim Dragon Temples** share a structural similarity—both maintain genealogical records and honor predecessors—that facilitates cooperation despite theological differences. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and **Lady Zara Goldscale** have exchanged visits to each other's ancestral archives, finding common ground in their commitment to preserving heritage.

**Lizardfolk** ancestor communication through **Shaman Vessska** presents a challenge for deity-centered faiths. The concept of directly speaking with the dead contradicts most faiths' belief that the deceased reside in divine afterlives rather than remaining accessible to the living. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** has concluded that **Lizardfolk** spirit communication operates on a different spiritual framework that doesn't necessarily conflict with afterlife theology, but many clergy remain uncomfortable with the practice.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn**'s persecution memorial has created unexpected interfaith dialogue about how societies remember their victims. **Queen Elara** has visited the memorial publicly, and her presence there reinforces the political commitment to preventing future persecution. Other faith leaders have followed, acknowledging that their own traditions also include victims worthy of remembrance.

**Corellon**'s elven faith has the least developed ancestral veneration, as elven longevity means that many ancestors are still living. This creates a cultural difference that sometimes produces misunderstanding in interfaith contexts, where other traditions expect reverence for the dead that elves reserve for their living elders.

## Mythology and Stories

Ancestral worship mythology centers on the continuing presence and influence of the dead in the lives of the living. The dwarven creation story describes Moradin forging the first dwarves from iron and stone, establishing the **Ancestral Records** by inscribing their names on his anvil. Every subsequent dwarf added to the Records continues this divine documentation, making genealogy a form of scripture.

**Dragonborn** mythology describes the ancient draconic civilization at its height, when dragons and **Dragonborn** lived in partnership. The **Fall of the Dragons** during **the Sundering** shattered this partnership, leaving **Dragonborn** to carry draconic heritage forward alone. The **Dragon Wastes** pilgrimage reinforces this story annually, reminding **Dragonborn** of their obligation to honor what was lost and preserve what remains.

**Lizardfolk** stories told by **Shaman Vessska** describe ancestor spirits joining the ecological spirit community after death, contributing their wisdom to the collective guidance that **Vessska** channels. The **Sinking of Aqualonis** myth serves as ancestral warning—those who violate the ecological compact join Aqualonis's drowned spirits, whose fate reminds the living to maintain balance.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn**'s memorial mythology tells of **tiefling** and **aasimar** communities destroyed during **The Great Persecution**, their stories preserved by survivors who refused to let their memories die. These narratives serve dual purposes: honoring the dead and reinforcing the commitment to ensuring that persecution never recurs. The annual remembrance ceremony transforms grief into resolve, embodying the principle that ancestral veneration includes learning from past failures.
