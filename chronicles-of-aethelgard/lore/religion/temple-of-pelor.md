# The Temple of Pelor

## Overview

The Temple of Pelor is **Silverhaven**'s primary religious site dedicated to Pelor the Sun Father, where **Queen Elara** worships and the **Order of the Silver Dawn** maintains headquarters. The temple's healing clinics serve all citizens regardless of faith, and its agricultural blessings support the **Eastern Plains**. **Pelor**'s clergy works closely with the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** on farming magic.

## Domains and Portfolios

Pelor the Sun Father governs domains central to human civilization in the **Eastern Kingdoms**: the sun, which provides light, warmth, and the growing season that makes agriculture possible; agriculture itself, which sustains human settlements and communities; healing, which Pelor's clergy practices through charitable clinics and training programs; and justice, which the Sun Father represents through the metaphor of light revealing truth and darkness hiding wrongdoing.

The solar domain extends beyond physical sunlight to encompass moral illumination. Pelor's teachings hold that truth, like sunlight, should be accessible to all and that concealing truth is an offense against the natural order. This theological principle underpins the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s commitment to transparency and their opposition to the deception and persecution that characterized **The Great Persecution**.

Agricultural blessings form the practical heart of Pelor's worship for most **Eastern Kingdoms** citizens. The temple's clergy travel to farming communities throughout the **Eastern Plains**, blessing fields, livestock, and harvests. These blessings provide genuine magical benefit—crops blessed by Pelor's clergy resist disease and drought more effectively than unblessed plantings, a contribution that **Poppy Greenfields**'s **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** has documented and verified.

Healing represents Pelor's most visible domain for urban worshippers. The Temple's charitable clinics in **Silverhaven** provide medical care to all comers regardless of their ability to pay or their faith, embodying Pelor's teaching that healing should be freely given as sunlight is freely given. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** operates these clinics, training both divine healers and conventional physicians.

## Worship and Rituals

Pelor's worship follows the solar calendar, with major ceremonies occurring at sunrise on significant dates. The Dawn Prayer, performed daily at sunrise, is the most common Pelorian observance—worshippers face the rising sun and offer gratitude for the return of light. This simple ritual connects urban worshippers to the agricultural cycles that sustain their civilization.

The Sun Day festival, occurring at the summer solstice, is the largest annual celebration in the **Eastern Kingdoms**. **Queen Elara** presides over the **Silverhaven** ceremony, which includes dawn prayers, communal feasting, healing blessings, and agricultural rites. The festival coincides with the height of the growing season, reinforcing the connection between Pelor's solar domain and the agricultural bounty of the **Eastern Plains**.

Harvest Home, celebrated at the autumn equinox, marks the end of the growing season with thanksgiving ceremonies. Worshippers bring first fruits and harvest products to Pelor's temples as offerings, which the clergy distributes to the needy. This redistribution embodies Pelor's teaching that abundance should be shared, not hoarded.

Private devotion to Pelor emphasizes acts of charity, truth-telling, and hope. Worshippers are encouraged to perform daily acts of kindness as offerings to the Sun Father, believing that spreading warmth and light in small ways accumulates divine favor. Many **Silverhaven** merchants attribute their success to Pelorian business ethics—honest weights, fair prices, and charitable giving.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The **Grand Temple of Pelor** in **Silverhaven** is the largest and most important Pelorian site in Aethelgard. Its architecture emphasizes light—enormous windows, pale stone, gilded surfaces—and its main sanctuary faces east so that dawn sunlight illuminates the altar during morning prayers. **Queen Elara** attends state ceremonies here, and the **Order of the Silver Dawn** administers the temple complex.

The Temple houses Aethelgard's premier healing clinic, where **Order of the Silver Dawn** healers provide free medical care. The clinic's reputation attracts patients from across the continent, and its training program produces skilled healers who serve throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The integration of divine and conventional medicine at the clinic serves as a model for the **Healers' Guild**.

Smaller Pelorian temples and shrines dot the **Eastern Plains**, serving farming communities that depend on Pelor's agricultural blessings. These rural temples are typically simple structures—sun-oriented stone buildings with healing gardens and blessing fields where clergy demonstrate the practical benefits of Pelorian worship. The **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** works closely with these rural temples.

The **Thronehold Royal Chapel** serves as **Queen Elara**'s private worship space, connecting the monarchy to Pelor's divine authority. While smaller than the Grand Temple, the Chapel is architecturally refined and houses sacred objects including a sunstone that glows with genuine divine light, gifted to the monarchy by the **Order of the Silver Dawn** at the founding of the **Eastern Kingdoms**.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Order of the Silver Dawn** serves as Pelor's primary religious organization, combining clerical duties with humanitarian mission and interfaith coordination. Led by the **Archbishop Radiant Dawn** in **Silverhaven**, the Order operates Pelor's temples, healing clinics, and charitable programs throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**.

Pelor's clergy are organized hierarchically, from initiate healers through ordained priests to the Archbishop. Advancement requires demonstrated proficiency in healing magic, theological study, and charitable service. The Order's training program is rigorous, producing clergy who are both spiritually committed and practically skilled. Many graduates serve in the **Healers' Guild** alongside non-religious medical practitioners.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn**, a sub-order focused on protecting **aasimar** and **tiefling** rights, operates under the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s umbrella. Founded in response to **The Great Persecution**, the Radiant Dawn advocates for the acceptance of planar-touched species and provides spiritual support for communities recovering from persecution. This advocacy work reflects Pelor's domain of justice—the sun reveals all hidden things, including injustice.

Rural clergy serve agricultural communities throughout the **Eastern Plains**, traveling circuits that bring blessings, healing, and religious instruction to remote villages. These circuit priests form the backbone of Pelorian worship for most citizens, their practical service reinforcing theological teachings about generosity and care.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

The **Book of Dawns** is Pelor's primary sacred text, a compilation of hymns, prayers, theological discourses, and practical guidance for clergy. The Book is organized around the solar cycle, with sections corresponding to each season and time of day. **Order of the Silver Dawn** scholars maintain annotated editions that update practical guidance while preserving theological core content.

Pelor's symbolic system centers on the sun disk, a golden circle representing the Sun Father's all-seeing, all-warming presence. Sun disks appear in every Pelorian temple, on clergy vestments, and as personal devotional items among worshippers. Gold and yellow are sacred colors, and sunstone—a mineral that catches and amplifies light—is the most prized liturgical material.

Wheat sheaves represent Pelor's agricultural domain, appearing frequently in temple decoration and harvest festival regalia. The combination of sun disk and wheat sheaf—light sustaining life—embodies the practical theology of Pelorian worship: divine blessing made tangible in crops and healing.

The **Flame of Hope**, a perpetual sacred fire maintained in the Grand Temple's sanctuary, symbolizes Pelor's eternal presence and the obligation to carry light into dark places. The Flame has burned continuously since the Temple's founding, tended by dedicated acolytes who consider their service the highest honor.

## Relations with Other Faiths

Pelor's faith maintains Aethelgard's most extensive interfaith relationships through the **Order of the Silver Dawn**. With **Moradin**'s dwarven faith, cooperation focuses on shared values of craftsmanship and protection—Pelor's clergy appreciates dwarven practical skill, while **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** respects Pelorian healing expertise. Tensions arise over resource extraction in disputed areas, but the **Treaty of Silverhaven** provides a framework for resolution.

With **Corellon**'s elven faith, theological differences are acknowledged but managed respectfully. The sun-moon contrast between Pelor and Corellon creates natural theological tension, but both faiths emphasize protection and the **Order of the Silver Dawn** maintains warm relations with **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s religious establishment.

**Bahamut**'s **Dragonborn** faith shares Pelor's commitment to justice and protection, creating natural alliance. **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Queen Elara** coordinate on humanitarian efforts, and their clergy exchange visits regularly. **Lizardfolk** spirit traditions present more challenges, as animistic spirituality differs fundamentally from Pelor's deity-centered theology, but the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s commitment to interfaith dialogue maintains respectful relationships.

The most significant interfaith tension involves **Corellon**'s emphasis on artistry versus Pelor's emphasis on practical service. **Silvermoon** aesthetics sometimes clash with **Silverhaven** functionality, but both faiths share enough common ground to maintain productive cooperation through the **Order of the Silver Dawn**.

## Mythology and Stories

Pelor's mythology emphasizes the sun as a metaphor for divine generosity—freely given, universally accessible, and essential for life. The foundational myth tells of Pelor kindling the first sunrise after primordial darkness, choosing to share light rather than hoard it. This act of cosmic generosity establishes the theological principle that drives Pelorian charity: those who receive light are obligated to share it.

The **Great Persecution** produced powerful Pelorian stories about light confronting darkness. The **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s founding narrative tells of Pelorian clerics who sheltered **tiefling** refugees in their temples, the sun disk on their doors signaling that all were welcome within. These stories reinforce the Order's ongoing commitment to protecting the persecuted.

Agricultural myths describe Pelor blessing the first farmers with knowledge of seasonal cycles, seed saving, and soil stewardship. These practical teachings are presented as divine gifts that must be passed forward—a theology of agricultural knowledge sharing that **Poppy Greenfields**'s **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** embodies today.

Healing stories focus on Pelor restoring light to the sick and injured, both literally—through healing magic that produces warm light—and metaphorically—through the hope and dignity that healing provides. The Temple's clinics are living expressions of these myths, demonstrating that Pelorian theology becomes tangible practice through service.
