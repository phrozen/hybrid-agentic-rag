# The Elven Moon Temples

## Overview

Elven Moon Temples dot the **Western Reaches**, dedicated to Corellon and lunar deities, with the primary temple in **Silvermoon** near **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s estate. The **Academy of Stars** conducts lunar magic research at these temples, and the **Moonwell** serves as the most sacred moon site. Monthly ceremonies align with lunar phases, and **Forest Wardens** protect remote temples.

## Domains and Portfolios

The Moon Temples serve Corellon's lunar and magical domains, with additional veneration of minor lunar deities who govern specific aspects of moonlight, tides, and nocturnal phenomena. Corellon's primary manifestation at the Moon Temples is as the Lunar Creator, the deity who shaped elven spirits from moonlight and starfire and who continues to channel magical power through the moon's cycles. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** teaches that Corellon's lunar aspect represents the elven capacity for renewal—just as the moon wanes and waxes, so too can elves regenerate, transform, and transcend their limitations.

The Magic domain is particularly strong at Moon Temples due to the **Moonwell**'s amplification effect. Lunar magic operates with enhanced potency during full moons, and the temples' positioning along **ley line** nexuses further concentrates magical energy. The **Academy of Stars** leverages this concentration for research into lunar magic, divination, and enchantment, producing advances that benefit the wider magical community.

The Mystery domain governs the aspects of elven experience that resist direct understanding—prophecy, artistic inspiration, and the boundary between the Feywild and the Material Plane. The Moon Temples' ceremonies embrace mystery rather than attempting to resolve it, and the temples' architecture incorporates shadow, reflection, and partial concealment that symbolize the elven acceptance of unknowable truths.

The Protection domain manifests through the **Forest Wardens** who guard remote Moon Temples, the abjuration wards that protect temple sites, and Corellon's protective aspect that watches over elven territories during the moonlit hours when the **Veilwood Forest** crossings are most active. The temples serve as safe havens for travelers, refugees, and those seeking Corellon's shelter.

## Worship and Rituals

Moon Temple ceremonies follow the lunar calendar, with each phase representing different aspects of Corellon's influence. New moon ceremonies emphasize reflection and renewal—a time for individual meditation, artistic creation, and magical study. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** uses new moon periods for private consultation with the **Academy of Stars** faculty, reviewing magical research and setting priorities for the coming month.

Waxing moon ceremonies focus on growth and preparation. Apprentice mages begin new projects, artists start new works, and **Forest Wardens** increase patrol intensity as fey activity increases with the growing moonlight. The **Academy of Stars** schedules practical magical training during waxing phases, taking advantage of the gradually increasing ambient magical energy.

Full moon ceremonies are the most significant observances, conducted at the **Moonwell** and at temples positioned to catch maximum moonlight. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** leads the primary full moon ceremony in **Silvermoon**, where the Moonwell's amplification makes divination and enchantment most potent. These ceremonies include magical demonstrations, artistic performances, and divinatory readings that guide elven governance for the coming month.

Waning moon ceremonies emphasize release and rest. Projects are completed or intentionally set aside, completed artworks are dedicated to Corellon, and **Forest Wardens** reduce patrol intensity as fey activity diminishes. This phase represents the elven practice of completing cycles rather than fighting them, accepting natural rhythms rather than imposing will on the unavoidable.

The **Moon Celebration**, Corellon's major annual festival, occurs during the first full moon of autumn and combines all lunar phases' ceremonial elements in a three-day observance. Day one features new moon-style reflection, day two waxing-moon growth activities, and day three a full moon grand ceremony at the **Moonwell**. The Moon Celebration draws elves from across the **Western Reaches** and visitors from other regions who wish to experience Corellon's worship at its most powerful.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The primary Moon Temple complex in **Silvermoon** includes the **Moonwell**, the **Academy of Stars**, and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s estate, forming an integrated religious-educational-political center. The temple's architecture maximizes moonlight exposure—open courtyards, reflective pools, silver-inlaid walls, and astronomically aligned windows that channel lunar light into ceremonial spaces during specific moon phases.

The **Moonwell** is the most sacred site in elven religion, a natural formation where lunar magic concentrates with extraordinary intensity. The well's waters amplify magical effects during full moons, making it Aethelgard's most powerful site for divination, enchantment, and lunar magic research. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** conducts major divinatory readings at the Moonwell during full moon ceremonies, providing guidance that shapes elven governance.

Remote Moon Temples throughout the **Western Reaches** serve local elven communities, providing regular worship spaces closer to home than **Silvermoon**. Each temple features a reflective pool or moon-aligned architectural element that echoes the **Moonwell**'s design at smaller scale. **Forest Wardens** patrol routes between temples, ensuring safe passage for pilgrims and maintaining the temples' protective wards.

The **Whispering Woods** hosts several hidden Moon Temples accessible only through fey crossings, sites where the boundary between the **Feywild** and the Material Plane is particularly thin. These temples serve as diplomatic meeting points between elven authorities and fey courts, their liminal positioning reflecting their role as bridges between worlds. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** authorizes access to these sites only for experienced diplomats who understand fey protocols.

**Veilwood Forest** temples operate under heightened security due to active fey crossings. These temples combine religious, magical, and defensive functions, with **Forest Wardens** garrisons maintaining constant vigilance while clergy conduct ceremonies that Corellon's teachings require. The dual purpose of these sites reflects the elven understanding that worship and protection are inseparable in dangerous territories.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Moon Priestesses** form the highest tier of Corellon's clergy, all female elves who have demonstrated magical mastery, artistic excellence, and spiritual depth. They lead major ceremonies at the **Moonwell** and advise **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** on religious and governance matters. Selection to this order requires demonstrated Corellon's favor through magical sign or artistic achievement, ensuring that spiritual authority in elven religion derives from divine endorsement rather than institutional appointment.

**Star Guardians** serve as Corellon's militant religious order, combining clerical training with **Forest Wardens** military service. They protect temples, escort pilgrims through dangerous territory, and enforce religious law within elven communities. The Star Guardians are particularly active during full moons, when fey crossings are most active and temple security requires enhancement.

Academic priests at the **Academy of Stars** pursue Corellon's magical and artistic aspects through scholarship and teaching. Their role bridges religious and educational institutions, ensuring that magical instruction includes devotional components and that ceremonies incorporate magical demonstration. The **Academy of Stars** faculty include some of Aethelgard's most accomplished mages and artists, whose work simultaneously serves religious and academic purposes.

**Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** holds unique dual authority as both temporal ruler and religious leader. Her position reflects the elven integration of spiritual and political governance—her decisions at the **Moonwell** are both divinely informed and politically consequential. This combination sometimes creates tension with other Aethelgard leaders who maintain clearer separation between religious and secular authority.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

The **Starsong Codex** is the Moon Temples' primary sacred text, a collection of devotional poems, magical treatises, and astronomical data compiled over millennia by elven clergy. Each Moon Temple maintains its own annotated edition, reflecting local traditions and accumulated scholarship. The **Silvermoon** temple preserves the oldest and most complete version, written on enchanted silver parchment that glows during full moon ceremonies.

Corellon's primary symbol is the crescent moon, representing divine light illuminating darkness. Silver and moonstone are sacred materials, appearing in temple architecture, clergy vestments, and personal devotional items throughout the **Western Reaches**. The eight-pointed star represents Corellon's connection to all eight magical schools, signifying that magical mastery in every discipline honors the deity.

Music serves as a living sacred text in Moon Temple worship. Hymns passed through oral tradition encode theological principles in lyrics and magical theory in melodies. The **Academy of Stars** preserves the most complete collection, and performances there are simultaneously artistic, religious, and educational. New compositions that achieve excellence are incorporated into the liturgical canon, keeping Corellon's worship creatively alive.

The **Moonwell**'s water is distributed to temples throughout the **Western Reaches** during full moon ceremonies, connecting remote communities to the primary sacred site. This practice ensures that even distant Moon Temples maintain a direct connection to the most powerful lunar site in Aethelgard.

## Relations with Other Faiths

The Moon Temples' primary interfaith relationship involves **Pelor**'s solar faith, creating a natural lunar-solar complementarity that can become theological competition. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** and **Queen Elara** manage this relationship through the **Order of the Silver Dawn**, emphasizing the complementary rather than contradictory nature of lunar and solar worship. The **Grand Temple** includes a lunar chapel that **Silvermoon** clergy consider respectful acknowledgment of Corellon, while the Moon Temples include solar observances that honor Pelor's seasonal importance.

With **Moradin**'s dwarven faith, the Moon Temples' emphasis on artistry and mystery contrasts with dwarven emphasis on craftsmanship and practicality. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintain diplomatic courtesy, but genuine theological engagement is limited by these fundamental differences. Practical cooperation occurs on matters of mutual security, particularly via the **Treaty of Silverhaven**.

**Bahamut**'s **Dragonborn** faith shares the Moon Temples' emphasis on honor and protection, creating opportunities for limited cooperation. **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** exchange visits during their respective major festivals, and their military forces cooperate on border security in the **Southern Expanse** approaches.

**Lizardfolk** spirit traditions remain the most challenging relationship for Moon Temple clergy. The elven emphasis on formal liturgy, written texts, and architectural temples conflicts with **Lizardfolk** animistic, oral, nature-centered practice. **Shaman Vessska** has made patient efforts to explain spirit worship to elven clergy, but the theological gap remains wide.

## Mythology and Stories

Corellon's founding myth describes the Lunar Creator shaping the first elves from moonlight and starfire, instilling in them the capacity for artistic creation that would perpetually honor their divine origin. This narrative establishes artistic excellence as the highest form of devotion—elves create art not merely for pleasure but because creation itself honors Corellon.

The **Sundering Migration** story tells of Corellon guiding the elves to the **Western Reaches** after the catastrophe, using moonlight to illuminate safe paths through the chaos. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** traces her authority to this myth, claiming that Corellon's guidance established the elven claim to the **Western Reaches** that endures today. The **Moonwell**'s continued amplification of lunar magic is presented as evidence of Corellon's ongoing protection.

The **War of Shattered Skies** produced new mythology emphasizing Corellon's protective aspect. Stories of elven warriors blessed with moonlight shielding, mages whose spells shone with divine radiance, and **Forest Wardens** who fought with supernatural endurance reinforce Corellon's martial role in elven consciousness. These narratives are retold at every **Moon Celebration**, connecting contemporary elves to their mythological heritage through the living tradition of lunar worship.
