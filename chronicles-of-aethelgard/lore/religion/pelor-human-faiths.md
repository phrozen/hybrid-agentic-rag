# Pelor and the Human Faiths

## Overview

Pelor the Sun Father is the primary deity worshipped in the **Eastern Kingdoms**, god of sun, agriculture, and healing whose temples dot the **Eastern Plains**. **Queen Elara** maintains Pelor's **Grand Temple** in **Silverhaven**, where the **Order of the Silver Dawn** also operates. Pelor's clergy emphasizes healing, protection of the innocent, and agricultural blessing, working closely with the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** to ensure bountiful harvests.

## Domains and Portfolios

Pelor governs four interconnected domains that define Pelorian theology and practice. The Sun domain represents light, truth, and revelation—Pelor's light exposes deception, guides the lost, and sustains all living things. Clerics channeling the Sun domain produce radiance that repels undead, reveals hidden things, and provides warmth in cold darkness. The **Grand Temple** in **Silverhaven** was designed to maximize natural sunlight, embodying this domain's centrality to Pelorian worship.

The Agriculture domain provides Pelorian clergy with magic that enhances crop growth, protects harvests from disease and pests, and ensures favorable weather for planting and reaping. **Poppy Greenfields** and the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** work directly with Pelorian clergy to maximize these blessings, creating a partnership between divine magic and agricultural science that feeds the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The annual Sun Day festival coincides with the solstice specifically to align religious observance with agricultural cycles.

The Healing domain is Pelor's most universally visible contribution. Every Pelorian temple operates a healing clinic, and the **Grand Temple**'s clinic is Aethelgard's finest medical facility, combining divine healing with conventional medicine. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** trains healers in both traditions, producing practitioners who can address injuries and illnesses through whichever method is most effective for each case.

The Justice domain emphasizes truth, fairness, and protection of the weak. Pelor's light reveals deception and illuminates wrongdoing, making Pelorian clergy natural investigators and arbitrators. **Queen Elara** employs Pelorian truth-seekers in her justice system, and the **Order of the Silver Dawn** advocates for the persecuted using Justice domain magic to expose injustice.

## Worship and Rituals

Pelorian worship centers on the daily cycle of the sun, with major observances aligned to solar events. Dawn Prayer, performed at sunrise, is the most essential daily ritual—worshippers face the rising sun and offer gratitude for the return of light. This practice is so entrenched in **Eastern Kingdoms** culture that even non-worshippers often pause at dawn, a cultural habit that delights Pelorian clergy.

Sun Day, the summer solstice festival, is the **Eastern Kingdoms**' largest annual celebration. **Queen Elara** presides over the **Silverhaven** ceremony, which draws thousands for dawn prayers, communal feasting, healing blessings, and agricultural rites. The festival doubles as a civic celebration of the **Eastern Kingdoms**' prosperity, linking Pelor's blessings to national well-being.

Harvest Home, marking the autumn equinox, focuses on gratitude and redistribution. Worshippers bring first fruits and agricultural products to Pelorian temples, which the clergy distributes to the needy. This practice embodies Pelor's teaching that the sun's generosity should be mirrored by human generosity—those who receive abundance should share it.

Winter Solstice observances emphasize hope in darkness. The longest night of the year culminates in a vigil that concludes at dawn, symbolizing Pelor's victory over darkness. **The Great Persecution** gave this observance new significance, as **tiefling** survivors and their **Order of the Silver Dawn** allies saw in the returning dawn a metaphor for justice prevailing over oppression.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The **Grand Temple of Pelor** in **Silverhaven** dominates the city's religious and civic landscape. Its architecture maximizes natural light through enormous east-facing windows, pale stone walls, and gilded surfaces that reflect sunlight throughout the interior. The main sanctuary seats thousands for major ceremonies, while side chapels provide space for smaller gatherings and private devotion. The Temple complex includes the healing clinics where the **Order of the Silver Dawn** provides free medical care.

Rural Pelorian temples throughout the **Eastern Plains** serve farming communities that depend on agricultural blessings for their livelihoods. These temples are typically simple stone structures with healing gardens and blessing fields where clergy demonstrate the practical benefits of Pelorian worship. The **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** maintains close relationships with rural Pelorian clergy, coordinating blessing schedules with planting and harvest cycles.

The **Thronehold Royal Chapel** serves as **Queen Elara**'s personal worship space, connecting the monarchy to Pelor's divine authority. Sacred objects in the Chapel include a sunstone that glows with divine light, gifted by the **Order of the Silver Dawn** at the monarchy's founding. The Chapel's intimate scale contrasts with the Grand Temple's monumentality, providing **Queen Elara** with a space for private devotion and counsel with Pelorian clergy.

Roadside shrines to Pelor mark significant points along **Eastern Kingdoms** travel routes, offering travelers a place for brief prayers and healing. These shrines typically feature a sun disk, a small healing well, and an offering box for charitable donations that fund the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s humanitarian work.

## Clergy and Orders

The **Order of the Silver Dawn** serves as Pelor's primary clergy, though the Order trains practitioners in multiple divine traditions. Pelorian clergy within the Order receive specialized training in Sun, Healing, Agriculture, and Justice domains. The Order's hierarchical structure progresses from initiate through ordained priest to bishop, with the **Archbishop Radiant Dawn** serving as supreme authority.

Rural circuit priests form the backbone of Pelorian worship for **Eastern Plains** communities. These clergy travel fixed routes, bringing blessings, healing, and religious instruction to villages too small to support permanent temples. Their practical service—healing the sick, blessing crops, resolving disputes—reinforces Pelorian theology through action rather than abstract preaching.

The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** operates as a specialized sub-order focused on protecting **aasimar** and **tiefling** communities. Founded during **The Great Persecution** to shelter the hunted, the Radiant Dawn now advocates for acceptance of planar-touched species and provides spiritual support for communities healing from persecution. Their clergy combine Justice domain magic with pastoral care, using truth-revealing abilities to expose ongoing discrimination while supporting victims.

Pelor's paladins serve as militant clergy who combine Justice and Protection domain channeling with combat training. These warriors protect pilgrims, defend temples, and pursue justice against those who would harm the innocent. The most famous Pelorian paladin order, the **Dawn Guard**, operates from the **Grand Temple** and answers to **Queen Elara** in times of crisis.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

The **Book of Dawns** is Pelor's primary sacred text, organized around the solar year with sections for each season and daily cycle. The Book includes hymns, prayers, theological discourses, and practical guidance for clergy. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** maintains annotated editions that update practical guidance while preserving theological content, ensuring the text remains relevant across changing circumstances.

Pelor's primary symbol is the sun disk—a golden circle representing the Sun Father's all-seeing, all-warming presence. Sun disks appear everywhere in Pelorian worship: temple architecture, clergy vestments, personal jewelry, and roadside shrines. Gold and yellow are sacred colors, associated with sunlight, divine generosity, and agricultural bounty.

Wheat sheaves represent Pelor's agricultural domain, symbolizing the connection between divine light and earthly sustenance. Combined sun disk and wheat sheaf imagery appears throughout Pelorian iconography, reinforcing the practical theology that links worship to daily survival.

The **Flame of Hope**, a perpetual fire in the **Grand Temple** sanctuary, represents Pelor's eternal presence and the obligation to carry light into darkness. Maintained by dedicated acolytes, the Flame has burned continuously since the Temple's founding and serves as the source for all new sacred fires in Pelorian temples throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**.

## Relations with Other Faiths

Pelor's faith maintains Aethelgard's most extensive interfaith network through the **Order of the Silver Dawn**. Relations with **Moradin**'s dwarven faith are generally positive, centered on shared values of craftsmanship and protection. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and **Queen Elara**'s clergy cooperate on practical matters, though theological tensions between industrious craftsmanship and contemplative artistry persist.

With **Corellon**'s elven faith, the sun-moon contrast creates gentle theological competition rather than conflict. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** and **Queen Elara** model respectful cooperation, and their clergy coordinate on humanitarian efforts. The **Academy of Stars** and the **Grand Temple** occasionally collaborate on healing research, combining lunar and solar magical traditions with productive results.

**Bahamut**'s **Dragonborn** faith is Pelorian's closest theological ally, sharing commitments to justice, protection, and the welfare of the innocent. **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Queen Elara** have developed genuine friendship, and their cooperation on humanitarian projects demonstrates that faith-based alliances can produce practical results.

**Lizardfolk** spirit traditions remain the most challenging interfaith relationship. The animistic, spirit-centered theology of **Shaman Vessska**'s practice doesn't map onto deity-centered frameworks. However, the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s commitment to respect for all faiths ensures that practical cooperation continues despite theological differences. Joint environmental projects in the **Southern Expanse** demonstrate that different faiths can work together on shared concerns.

## Mythology and Stories

Pelor's mythology emphasizes the sun as divine generosity—freely given, universally accessible, and essential for life. The founding myth describes Pelor igniting the first sunrise to end primordial darkness, choosing to share his light with all creation rather than keeping it for himself. This generous act establishes the theological principle that drives all Pelorian charity: those who receive light must share it.

Stories of Pelor's intervention during **the Sundering** describe the Sun Father sheltering human communities with protective light, guiding refugees to safe territories, and healing the wounded. These narratives reinforce the belief that Pelor actively protects his followers during catastrophe, a belief that sustained the **Eastern Kingdoms** through the **War of Shattered Skies**.

The **Great Persecution** produced the **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s founding mythology: Pelorian clerics sheltering **tiefling** refugees by the light of their faith, the sun disk on their doors signaling that all were welcome. These stories remain central to the Order's identity and fuel their ongoing advocacy for the persecuted.

Agricultural myths connect Pelor's solar generosity directly to human survival, describing the deity blessing early farmers with knowledge of planting cycles, seed selection, and soil stewardship. These practical teachings are presented as sacred gifts that must be shared—precisely the principle that **Poppy Greenfields** and the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** embody today through their partnership with Pelorian clergy.
