# Corellon and the Elven Traditions

## Overview

Corellon Larethian is the primary deity of the elven pantheon, archgod of magic, arts, and warfare who guided elves to **Western Reaches** after **the Sundering**. The **Academy of Stars** in **Silvermoon** teaches Corellon's magical traditions alongside practical studies, while **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintains elven religious sites. Corellon's worship emphasizes artistic excellence, magical mastery, and protection of elven territories guarded by the **Forest Wardens**.

## Domains and Portfolios

Corellon Larethian presides over domains central to elven identity and civilization. As god of magic, Corellon inspires the arcane traditions practiced at the **Academy of Stars** and studied systemically at the **Arcane College of Thaldor**. Elven mages consider their craft a form of worship, pursuing magical excellence as a devotional act that honors Corellon's primacy over the magical arts.

As god of arts, Corellon encompasses music, poetry, visual creation, and all forms of aesthetic expression. Elven culture interprets artistic creation as a spiritual practice, and the finest elven artworks are dedicated to Corellon in temples throughout the **Western Reaches**. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** personally sponsors artists whose work exemplifies Corellon's ideals of beauty and emotional depth.

As god of warfare, Corellon represents the elven ideal of martial excellence in defense of home and people. Unlike human war gods who celebrate conquest, Corellon's martial aspect emphasizes protection and just resistance. The **Forest Wardens** embody this defensive martial tradition, bearing arms not for expansion but for the preservation of elven lands and the natural world under their stewardship.

Corellon's lunar aspect connects to the elven relationship with moonlight and nighttime. The **Moonwell** in **Silvermoon** serves as Corellon's most sacred site, where lunar magic amplifies divine connection during full moon ceremonies. This lunar association distinguishes Corellon's worship from solar traditions like **Pelor**'s, creating theological distinctions that interfaith scholars at the **Order of the Silver Dawn** continue to explore.

## Worship and Rituals

Corellon's worship follows a ritual calendar aligned with lunar phases, reflecting the deity's lunar associations. Full moon ceremonies at the **Moonwell** represent the most significant regular observances, where **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** leads elven clergy in chanting, magical demonstration, and artistic performance. These ceremonies blend religious devotion with magical practice, reflecting the elven belief that magic and faith are inseparable aspects of devotion to Corellon.

New moon observances emphasize contemplation and renewal, with individual elves engaging in private meditation, artistic creation, or magical study. The **Academy of Stars** suspends regular classes during new moon periods, encouraging students to pursue personal projects that honor Corellon through excellence. Many of Aethelgard's finest elven artworks were created during new moon devotional periods.

Seasonal festivals mark the solstices and equinoxes with elaborate communal celebrations. The **Moon Celebration**, Corellon's major annual festival, occurs during the summer solstice and features three days of magical demonstrations, artistic competitions, and **Forest Wardens**' martial exhibitions. Representatives from the **Order of the Silver Dawn** attend as observers, maintaining the interfaith dialogue that **Queen Elara**'s government promotes.

Personal devotion to Corellon takes many forms among elves. Artisans dedicate their finest work to Corellon's temples. Mages pursue magical breakthroughs as offerings. **Forest Wardens** dedicate their protective service to Corellon's martial aspect. This diversity of devotional expression reflects Corellon's broad portfolio and the elven belief that excellence in any domain honors their deity.

## Temples and Holy Sites

The **Moonwell** in **Silvermoon** serves as the most sacred Corellon site in Aethelgard, a natural formation where lunar magic concentrates with extraordinary intensity. The **Moonwell** amplifies both divine and arcane magic during full moons, making it the site of Corellon's most powerful ceremonies. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintains the **Moonwell** and its surrounding temple complex as her primary religious responsibility.

The **Elven Moon Temples** scattered through the **Western Reaches** serve as local worship centers for elven communities. Each temple features a reflective pool or moon-aligned architectural element that echoes the **Moonwell**'s design at smaller scale. **Forest Wardens** protect these temples, ensuring that even remote elven communities maintain access to Corellon's worship.

The **Academy of Stars** in **Silvermoon** serves dual religious and educational functions. Its grand hall transforms into a temple during major ceremonies, with astronomical instruments and magical laboratories doubling as devotional spaces. This integration of learning and worship reflects the elven conviction that knowledge pursuit is a sacred act.

Remote shrines in the **Whispering Woods** and **Veilwood Forest** mark locations where Corellon's presence is felt with particular intensity. These shrines range from simple stone altars to elaborate clearing sanctuaries, maintained by **Forest Wardens** and local elven communities. Pilgrimages to these shrines are common during personal transitions—coming of age, marriage, or bereavement.

## Clergy and Orders

Corellon's clergy are drawn exclusively from elven species, with **High Elves** dominating the hierarchy and **Wood Elves** serving as rural clergy and shrine keepers. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** serves as both temporal ruler and religious authority in **Silvermoon**, her dual role reflecting the elven integration of spiritual and political leadership.

The **Moon Priestesses** form the highest tier of Corellon's clergy, all female elves who have demonstrated magical mastery, artistic excellence, and spiritual depth. They lead major ceremonies at the **Moonwell** and advise **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** on religious matters. Selection to this order requires demonstrating Corellon's favor through magical sign or artistic achievement.

The **Star Guardians** serve as Corellon's militant religious order, combining clerical training with **Forest Wardens** military service. They protect temples, escort pilgrims through dangerous territory, and enforce religious law within elven communities. The **Star Guardians** are particularly active during periods of external threat, when religious and defensive duties merge seamlessly.

Academic priests at the **Academy of Stars** pursue Corellon's magical and artistic aspects through scholarship and teaching. Their role bridges the religious and educational institutions, ensuring that magical instruction includes devotional components and that religious ceremonies incorporate magical demonstration.

## Sacred Texts and Symbols

Corellon's sacred texts include the **Starsong Codex**, a collection of devotional poems and magical treatises composed over millennia by elven clergy. The Codex exists in multiple versions, each temple maintaining its own annotated copy that reflects local traditions and accumulated scholarship. The **Moonwell** temple preserves the oldest known version, written on enchanted silver parchment that glows during full moon ceremonies.

Corellon's primary symbol is the crescent moon, representing divine light illuminating darkness. Silver and moonstone are sacred materials, featured extensively in temple architecture, ceremonial objects, and personal devotional items. The eight-pointed star represents Corellon's connection to all eight magical schools, signifying that magical mastery in every discipline honors the deity.

Music serves as a living sacred text among elven worshippers. Hymns passed down through oral tradition encode theological principles in their lyrics and magical theory in their melodies. The **Academy of Stars** maintains the most complete collection of these musical traditions, and performances there are considered both artistic events and religious observances.

The **Moonwell**'s water is considered sacred, collected during full moon ceremonies and distributed to temples throughout the **Western Reaches** for use in blessings and healing rituals. This practice connects remote communities to the **Moonwell** and, through it, to Corellon's direct presence.

## Relations with Other Faiths

Corellon's faith maintains careful relationships with other Aethelgard religions. The most significant theological tension exists with **Moradin**'s dwarven faith, which emphasizes craftsmanship over artistry and creation over appreciation. While **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** maintain diplomatic courtesy, their traditions' differing values create friction over priorities like resource extraction in disputed territories.

With **Pelor**'s human faith, relations are positive. Both deities emphasize protection, and their clergy cooperate on humanitarian efforts through the **Order of the Silver Dawn**. Theological differences between lunar and solar worship are treated as complementary rather than contradictory, a position the **Order** actively promotes.

**Bahamut**'s **Dragonborn** temples share Corellon's emphasis on martial excellence in defense, creating natural affinity between the faiths. **Lady Zara Goldscale** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** have established cordial relations, and their priests regularly exchange visits.

**Lizardfolk** spirit traditions puzzle Corellon's clergy, who struggle to reconcile spirit worship with their own deity-centered theology. **Shaman Vessska**'s patient explanations have improved mutual understanding, but significant theological differences remain. The **Order of the Silver Dawn** facilitates ongoing dialogue.

## Mythology and Stories

Corellon's mythology centers on themes of creation through artistry, protection through mastery, and the eternal struggle between civilization and chaos. The foundational myth tells of Corellon crafting the first elves from moonlight and starfire, shaping them as works of art that could themselves create art—a recursive devotional cycle that elves believe honors Corellon most deeply.

The **Sundering Migration** myth describes Corellon guiding the elves to the **Western Reaches** after the catastrophe, using moonlight to illuminate safe paths through the chaos. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** traces her authority to this myth, claiming that Corellon's guidance established the elven claim to the **Western Reaches** that endures today.

The **War of Shattered Skies** produced new Corellon mythology emphasizing the deity's protective aspect. Stories of elven warriors blessed with moonlight shielding, mages whose spells shone with divine radiance, and **Forest Wardens** who fought with supernatural endurance reinforce Corellon's martial role in elven consciousness. These stories are retold at every **Moon Celebration**, connecting contemporary elves to their mythological heritage.
