# The Plague of Shadows

## Overview

The Plague of Shadows was a devastating magical disease that swept through the **Eastern Kingdoms** from 318-320 AS, killing approximately 30% of the affected population and serving as the catalyst for **The Great Persecution**. The plague's mysterious origins and disproportionate effect on planar-touched species led to false accusations against **tieflings** and **aasimar**, fueling religious extremism and mob violence. Modern research by the **Arcane College of Thaldor** suggests the plague was a natural magical phenomenon, but the damage to inter-species relations proved long-lasting.

## Date and Era

- **Outbreak**: 318 AS (After Sundering)
- **Peak**: 319 AS (highest mortality)
- **End**: 320 AS (plague burned out naturally)
- **Era**: Late Age of Expansion / Pre-Great Persecution
- **Duration**: Approximately two years of active plague
- **Legacy**: Directly triggered **The Great Persecution** (320-380 AS)

## Participants and Factions

### Medical and Magical Responders

**Arcane College of Thaldor:**
- **Throndor the Wise** personally led the College's response effort
- Dispatched healers and researchers to affected areas
- Established quarantine protocols based on magical sensing
- Conducted research into the plague's origins and treatment
- Ultimately concluded the plague was a natural magical phenomenon, not deliberate

**Order of the Silver Dawn:**
- Religious order provided spiritual comfort and basic care
- Moderate members treated all victims regardless of species
- Extremist faction argued the plague was divine punishment
- The order's internal division foreshadowed its role in **The Great Persecution**
- Healers from the order were among the first to contract and die from the plague

**Local Healers and Herbalists:**
- Overwhelmed by the scale and nature of the disease
- Traditional remedies proved useless against magical illness
- Many healers died after exposure to patients
- Knowledge gaps contributed to fear and rumor
- Some healers were accused of causing the plague

### Victims and Affected Communities

**Eastern Kingdoms Population:**
- Urban centers hit hardest (density accelerated transmission)
- **Thronehold** lost 35% of its population
- **Silverhaven** lost 25% (better quarantine protocols)
- Rural areas suffered 20% losses
- Total estimated deaths: 200,000+ across the **Eastern Kingdoms**

**Planar-Touched Species:**
- **Tieflings** and **aasimar** showed different disease patterns
- **Tieflings** appeared to contract the plague at higher rates (later proven false)
- **Aasimar** showed resistance initially but suffered delayed onset
- These patterns fueled conspiracy theories about deliberate origin
- The actual explanation involved magical affinity, not deliberate action

### Persecution Advocates

**Prophet Marcus Pureheart (emerged during plague):**
- Charismatic religious leader who blamed planar-touched species
- Founded the "Pure Faith" movement during the plague's peak
- Argued that **tieflings** and **aasimar** were agents of the plague
- His preaching directly incited mob violence against planar-touched communities
- Laid the ideological groundwork for **The Great Persecution**
- Died of natural causes in 365 AS, having never recanted his beliefs

**Lord Geoffrey Ironwall:**
- Noble who seized **tiefling** properties during the chaos
- Used plague fear as cover for personal enrichment
- Funded anti-planar-touched militias
- Became one of the primary architects of legal persecution
- His descendants still hold confiscated properties

## Causes and Context

### The Nature of the Plague

The Plague of Shadows was a magical disease unlike anything Aethelgard had experienced. Victims developed shadowy discoloration of the skin, extreme sensitivity to light, progressive weakness, and eventually death. The disease spread through proximity—both physical and, unusually, through magical resonance. Mages and magical creatures were more susceptible than non-magical individuals, which contributed to the false belief that the plague was deliberately created.

**Disease Characteristics:**
- Incubation period: 7-14 days
- Initial symptoms: shadow discoloration, light sensitivity, fatigue
- Progressive symptoms: weakness, organ failure, magical instability
- Transmission: proximity (physical and magical resonance)
- Mortality rate: approximately 60% without treatment
- Eventual natural burnout after two years

### Origin Theories

**Natural Magical Phenomenon (Current Scientific Consensus):**
- The **Arcane College of Thaldor**'s research concluded the plague was a natural magical phenomenon
- Ley line instability following the **Stormpeak Mountains** seismic activity may have triggered it
- Magical diseases are rare but not unprecedented in Aethelgard's history
- The plague's patterns are consistent with natural epidemiology, not deliberate design
- Similar but less severe magical illnesses have occurred since

**Deliberate Attack (Persecution-Era Belief, Now Discredited):**
- **Prophet Marcus Pureheart** claimed **tieflings** had created the plague
- No evidence ever supported this claim
- **Throndor the Wise** investigated and found no evidence of deliberate design
- The theory was used to justify persecution, not based on scientific observation
- Modern magical forensics have conclusively ruled out deliberate creation

**Divine Punishment (Religious Belief, Varies):**
- Some religious groups interpreted the plague as divine punishment for moral failings
- The **Order of the Silver Dawn**'s moderate faction viewed it as a test of faith
- Extremist factions viewed it as justification for purifying society
- This interpretation fueled persecution regardless of its theological merit
- The moderate **Order of the Silver Dawn** officially repudiated this interpretation in 380 AS

## Key Battles/Moments

### The Initial Outbreak (318 AS, Spring)

The plague first appeared in **Thronehold**'s poorest districts, where population density and poor sanitation created ideal conditions for transmission. Initial reports described a "shadow sickness" that turned victims' skin dark and made them recoil from sunlight. Local healers were baffled; traditional remedies had no effect, and the disease spread faster than anyone could respond.

### The Spread (318 AS, Summer-Fall)

Within months, the plague had spread from **Thronehold** to every major city in the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The disease's magical transmission mechanism meant that it spread not only through physical contact but also through proximity to magical practitioners. Mages, **tieflings**, and **aasimar**—all of whom had higher magical affinity—contracted the plague at apparently higher rates, creating the false impression that they were either the source of or more susceptible to the disease.

### The Persecution Begins (319 AS)

As death tolls mounted and no cure was found, fear turned to anger. **Prophet Marcus Pureheart** began preaching that **tieflings** and **aasimar**—beings with planar heritage—were the source of the plague. His message resonated with a terrified population desperate for someone to blame. The first **tiefling** businesses were burned in **Thronehold** in the winter of 319 AS, marking the beginning of **The Great Persecution**.

### Throndor's Intervention (319 AS)

**Throndor the Wise** arrived in **Thronehold** with a team of healers and researchers from the **Arcane College of Thaldor**. He established quarantine protocols, treated victims, and began investigating the plague's origins. His team's research—conducted under extraordinarily dangerous conditions—established that the plague was a natural magical phenomenon, not a deliberate attack. However, his findings were ignored by the increasingly violent persecution movement.

### The Natural Burnout (320 AS)

The plague ended not through human intervention but through natural burnout. By 320 AS, the disease had infected most of the susceptible population in the **Eastern Kingdoms**, and those who survived had developed natural immunity. The plague's magical resonance also appeared to diminish over time, as if the underlying magical instability that had triggered it was resolving. The **Arcane College of Thaldor**'s records indicate that no new cases were reported after Month 9 of 320 AS.

## Outcome and Consequences

### Immediate Death Toll

The Plague of Shadows killed approximately 200,000 people across the **Eastern Kingdoms**—roughly 30% of the affected population. Urban centers suffered most severely, with **Thronehold** losing a third of its population. The death toll included disproportionate numbers of mages, healers, and magical practitioners who were more susceptible due to their higher magical affinity.

### The Great Persecution

The plague's most devastating long-term consequence was **The Great Persecution** (320-380 AS). The false belief that **tieflings** and **aasimar** had caused or worsened the plague provided the justification for systematic discrimination, violence, and exile. Even after the plague ended, the persecution continued for sixty years, causing vastly more suffering than the disease itself. The **Treaty of Thaldor** (380 AS) officially ended the persecution, but its scars remain.

### Medical and Magical Advances

The plague prompted significant advances in magical medicine and disease prevention. **Throndor the Wise**'s research team developed new quarantine protocols, magical disease detection methods, and treatment approaches that remain standard practice. The **Arcane College of Thaldor** established a permanent disease research department that continues to study magical illness.

## Historical Significance

The Plague of Shadows is remembered as the catastrophe that revealed the fragility of inter-species trust. A natural magical disease—terrible but unavoidable—became a weapon of prejudice when frightened people needed someone to blame. The plague's true lesson is not about disease but about the human capacity for fear-driven cruelty. **Throndor the Wise**'s research proved the plague was natural, but that proof came too late to prevent **The Great Persecution**. The **Arcane College of Thaldor**'s ongoing research into magical disease remains one of **Throndor**'s most important legacies, a commitment to understanding rather than to blaming.
