# The Siege of Thronehold

## Overview

The Siege of Thronehold was a major military engagement in 395 AS during the **War of Shattered Skies**, where orcish forces attempted to capture the **Eastern Kingdoms** royal capital. **King Aldric the Unifier**'s descendants held the city for six months until relief forces arrived, marking one of the war's longest and most costly sieges. The successful defense proved the **Eastern Kingdoms**' military resilience and strengthened the alliance with **Stonehold** that would later enable victory.

## Date and Era

- **Dates**: 395 AS, Months 4-9 (six-month siege)
- **Era**: War of Shattered Skies (370-450 AS)
- **Campaign**: Orcish Eastern Campaign (390-400 AS)
- **Relieved**: 395 AS, Month 9, by combined human-dwarven forces
- **Anniversary**: Commemorated annually as "Thronehold Day" in Eastern Kingdoms

- Queen Marguerite: organized civilian defense and medical care

- Citadel garrison: 2,000 professional soldiers

**Thronehold City Forces:**
- City militia: 8,000 citizen-soldiers
- Temple guard: 1,000 religious defenders

**Notable Defenders:**
- General Aldric Steelhand: overall military commander
- Captain Sarah Ironside's predecessor: eastern wall hero
- Archmage Aldric Flameheart: magical defense coordinator

**Orcish Siege Army:**
- Commander: Warlord Grommash Bloodaxe, son of Chieftain Bloodtooth

**Eastern Kingdoms Army:**
- 15,000 soldiers under General Marten Hammerfall

**Dwarven Expeditionary Force:**
- 8,000 dwarven warriors under **Grimjaw Ironhand**

- Brought siege-breaking equipment and runic weapons

## Causes and Context

### Strategic Situation

By 395 AS, the **War of Shattered Skies** had been raging for two and a half decades. The orcish Bloodfang Confederation, rebuilt after **Chieftain Bloodtooth**'s defeat at **Thunder Pass** (380 AS), had launched a massive eastern campaign under his son **Grommash Bloodaxe**. This new offensive targeted the political heart of the **Eastern Kingdoms**: the ancient capital of **Thronehold**.

**Orcish Strategic Logic:**
- Capturing **Thronehold** would decapitate human political leadership
- The city's fall would demoralize the entire **Eastern Kingdoms**
- Control of **Thronehold** would provide a secure base for further operations
- The siege would force **Eastern Kingdoms** armies to commit to relief, creating other opportunities
- Winter supply needs demanded either capturing stored provisions or retreating

**Eastern Kingdoms Preparedness:**
- **Thronehold** had been fortified over centuries with massive stone walls
- Food stores were sufficient for eight months of siege
- Water supply came from deep wells immune to surface interference
- **King Aldric III** had ordered preparations for exactly this scenario
- Messenger birds had been dispatched requesting dwarven and allied assistance

### Pre-Siege Evacuation

Word of the approaching orcish army reached **Thronehold** two weeks before the siege began. **King Aldric III** ordered the evacuation of non-essential personnel—young children, the elderly, and the infirm were sent west toward **Silverhaven** under armed escort. Approximately 15,000 civilians were evacuated, but 30,000 remained, either too old or infirm to travel or choosing to stay and defend their homes.

## Key Battles/Moments

### The Investment (Month 4, Week 1)

**Grommash Bloodaxe**'s army surrounded **Thronehold** in a classic siege formation, cutting all supply routes and establishxing siege lines. Orcish engineers began constructing siege towers and rams while sappers started mining operations aimed at undermining the city's eastern wall. The investment was professional and methodical, reflecting Grommash's more sophisticated approach compared to his father's brute-force methods.

### The Eastern Wall Assault (Month 4, Week 3)

The first major assault came against **Thronehold**'s eastern wall, where Prince Aldric IV commanded the defense. Siege towers rolled forward under covering fire from orcish catapults. The fighting was fierce and lasted three days. The eastern wall held, but at a cost: 800 defenders killed and the wall's outer face severely damaged. Prince Aldric IV was wounded in the fighting but refused to leave the walls, inspiring his troops with his visible courage.

### The Mining Threat (Month 5)

Orcish sappers made significant progress on tunnel operations aimed at collapsing the eastern wall's foundations. Counter-mining operations led by dwarven volunteers within the city (dwarven traders who had been trapped by the siege) located and destroyed three of five mining tunnels. The remaining two tunnels were flooded by **Thronehold**'s engineers, who diverted underground water sources into the orcish excavations, drowning over 200 sappers.

### The Great Sortie (Month 6, Week 2)

With supplies dwindling and morale declining, General Steelhand proposed a desperate sortie—a surprise attack on the orcish siege works. At dawn on the longest day of the year, 4,000 defenders burst from the western gate while archers on the walls provided covering fire. The sortie destroyed six siege towers, three catapults, and killed an estimated 2,000 orcish warriors before retreating back inside the walls. The success boosted morale enormously.

### The Relief (Month 9, Week 1)

After six months of siege, **Thronehold**'s food stores were nearly exhausted and the defenders were down to half strength. Then the signal fires lit on the western horizon: the relief army had arrived. General Hammerfall's 15,000 **Eastern Kingdoms** soldiers attacked from the west while **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s 8,000 dwarven warriors struck from the north. Caught between the relief forces and the city garrison's own sortie, Grommash's army was shattered. The siege was broken in three days of fighting that saw the orcish forces routed and pursued for fifty miles.

## Outcome and Consequences

### Military Consequences

The successful defense of **Thronehold** marked the beginning of the end for the orcish eastern campaign. The loss of 12,000 warriors and most of their siege equipment rendered the Bloodfang Confederation incapable of further offensive operations in the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The battle proved that fortified cities could be held against overwhelming force and that the human-dwarven alliance was militarily decisive.

### Political Consequences

The siege cemented the alliance between the **Eastern Kingdoms** and **Stonehold**. Dwarven warriors had fought and died alongside humans, and **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s relief force was celebrated across the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The political debt of gratitude created bonds that would last for generations and ultimately lead to the **Treaty of Silverhaven**. **King Aldric III**'s reputation as a steadfast defender who did not abandon his city became a cornerstone of human royal mythology.

### Casualties

- **Defenders**: 4,500 killed, 3,000 wounded (of 14,500 initial)
- **Attackers**: 12,000 killed, 5,000 wounded, 3,000 captured (of 35,000 initial)
- **Civilians**: 2,000 killed during siege (starvation and bombardment)
- **Relief forces**: 2,000 killed, 4,000 wounded

## Historical Significance

The Siege of Thronehold demonstrated that the **Eastern Kingdoms** could withstand the war's greatest challenges and that the human-dwarven alliance was more than a diplomatic formality. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s relief march from **Thunder Pass** to **Thronehold**—covering 200 miles of hostile territory in just twelve days—remains one of the most celebrated forced marches in military history. The siege also produced innovations in urban defense and counter-siege tactics that influenced fortification design across Aethelgard. **Queen Elara**'s predecessor, who lived through the siege as a child, carried its lessons forward and ensured that **Eastern Kingdoms** defenses were never again allowed to weaken.
