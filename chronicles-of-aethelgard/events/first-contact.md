# The First Contact

## Overview

The First Contact refers to the historic meeting between **King Aldric the Unifier**'s human explorers and **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s dwarven delegation in 150 AS, marking the beginning of sustained human-dwarven diplomatic relations. The meeting occurred at Thunder Pass and resulted in the Thunder Pass Trade Agreement, establishing the foundation for centuries of cooperation between the species. The event is commemorated annually in both **Silverhaven** and **Stonehold**, with **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Queen Elara** exchanging diplomatic messages.

## Date and Era

- **Date**: 150 AS (After Sundering)
- **Era**: Age of Expansion (50-250 AS)
- **Historical Period**: Early human-dwarven relations
- **Anniversary**: Commemorated annually on the summer solstice in both kingdoms
- **Calendar Impact**: Marked beginning of formal inter-species diplomacy in Aethelgard

## Participants and Factions

### Human Delegation

**King Aldric the Unifier:**
- Personally authorized the expedition after years of planning
- Appointed his most trusted diplomat, Lord Marten Silverhand, as delegation leader
- Instructed the team to offer fair trade terms and respect dwarven sovereignty
- Awaited news at **Thronehold** with anxiety; the venture was politically risky
- Later called the meeting "the most important day since the Sundering"

**Delegation Composition:**
- 20 human diplomats, traders, and scholars
- 50 armed escorts (ceremonial, not military)
- Translators who had spent years learning dwarven trade-pidgin
- Gift bearers carrying human crafts, textiles, and foodstuffs
- Cartographers tasked with mapping the **Stormpeak Mountains** approaches

### Dwarven Delegation

**Grimjaw Ironhand:**
- Led the dwarven delegation despite being a runemaster rather than a diplomat
- Chosen for his openness to surface engagement—a rarity among dwarves of that era
- Spoke passable Common Tongue from previous surface encounters
- Recognized the strategic importance of trade with growing human kingdoms
- Reported directly to King Durin Deepforge of **Stonehold**

**Delegation Composition:**
- 15 dwarven craftmasters and negotiators
- 30 dwarven warriors in ceremonial armor
- Master translators proficient in early human languages
- Samples of dwarven metalwork, gemstones, and runic items
- Stonehold's formal trade proposal documentation

### Other Interested Parties

**Elves:**
- Observed from a distance through scrying and scouts
- **Silvermoon** council debated intervention but chose non-involvement
- Some elves warned that human-dwarven cooperation would marginalize elven interests
- The contact reinforced elven tendencies toward isolation

**Gnomes:**
- Small gnomish communities in the **Stormpeak Mountains** served as early intermediaries
- Gnomish translators facilitated initial communication attempts
- Gnome traders had maintained limited contact with both species for decades
- Their role was essential but often overlooked in official accounts

## Causes and Context

### Pre-Contact Isolation

For the first century and a half after the Sundering, humans and dwarves existed in near-total isolation. Dwarven holds in the **Stormpeak Mountains** had been sealed since the early Age of Delving, and human settlements on the **Eastern Plains** were too scattered and preoccupied with survival to attempt mountain crossings. The **Stormpeak Mountains** formed a natural barrier that neither species had strong incentive to breach.

**Human Motivations for Contact:**
- **King Aldric the Unifier** sought metal goods that human smiths could not produce
- Growing human settlements needed dwarven-quality tools and weapons
- Trade would strengthen the still-fragile unified kingdoms
- Curiosity about the mountain dwellers传闻已久 (long-rumored) for centuries
- Strategic need to secure **Thunder Pass** against future threats

**Dwarven Motivations for Contact:**
- Dwarven ore production exceeded internal demand
- Surface agricultural products were scarce underground
- **Grimjaw Ironhand** advocated for expanded surface engagement
- Younger dwarves chafed at isolationist elder policies
- Strategic interest in understanding surface political developments

### The Gnomish Connection

The contact would not have occurred without gnomish intermediaries. Small gnomish communities had maintained trading relationships with both humans and dwarves for over two decades, moving between surface and underground markets. When **King Aldric**'s agents asked gnomish traders about the mountain dwellers, the gnomes offered to arrange an introduction—for a substantial fee. The gnomish caravan master Fizzlewick Copperpin served as the initial go-between, carrying messages between **Thronehold** and **Stonehold** for over a year before the formal meeting was arranged.

## Key Battles/Moments

### The Approach (148-150 AS)

**King Aldric** commissioned three separate expeditions toward the **Stormpeak Mountains** between 148 and 150 AS. The first two were turned back by harsh weather and dwarven border patrols who communicated only through warning shots and barricades. The third expedition, led by Lord Marten Silverhand, carried formal diplomatic credentials and gifts chosen to appeal to dwarven sensibilities: hand-forged steel blades of the finest human manufacture, rare surface woods that dwarves prized but could not obtain, and a formal letter from King Aldric written in painstakingly translated dwarven script.

### The Meeting at Thunder Pass (150 AS)

The actual First Contact took place on a narrow shelf of rock at the **Thunder Pass** summit, midway between human and dwarven territories. Both sides approached with military escorts that remained at a careful distance. **Grimjaw Ironhand** and Lord Silverhand met alone at the center, seated on stone benches that gnomish advance teams had carved into the mountainside.

The meeting lasted three days. Initial conversations were halting, conducted through gnomish translators and supplemented with gestures and diagrams drawn in the dust. By the second day, **Grimjaw** and Silverhand had established enough rapport to begin discussing trade terms directly, with occasional translator assistance. By the third day, they had drafted the Thunder Pass Trade Agreement—a remarkably concise document that established trade rights, border protocols, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

**Key Provisions of the Thunder Pass Trade Agreement:**
1. Dwarven metalwork and gems would be traded for human agricultural products and textiles
2. **Thunder Pass** would serve as the primary trade route, with dwarven customs stations
3. Both species would respect the other's territorial sovereignty
4. Disputes would be resolved through joint arbitration, not military action
5. The agreement would be reviewed and renewed every fifty years

### The Return (150 AS)

Lord Silverhand's return to **Thronehold** was celebrated as a triumph. **King Aldric** personally received the delegation and declared a week of festivities. The trade agreement was ratified by the human council within a month. In **Stonehold**, **Grimjaw Ironhand** presented the agreement to King Durin Deepforge, who ratified it after three days of debate among the clan elders. The First Contact had transformed two isolated societies into partners.

## Outcome and Consequences

### Immediate Effects

**Trade Expansion:**
- Regular caravans began traversing **Thunder Pass** within months
- Dwarven metalwork transformed human agriculture and construction
- Human food and textiles improved dwarven quality of life
- Gnomish intermediaries lost their monopoly but gained increased volume
- **Stonehold**'s economy began its long growth trajectory

**Diplomatic Framework:**
- First formal inter-species agreement in Aethelgard's post-Sundering history
- Established precedent for negotiation over conflict
- Created joint arbitration model still referenced in the **Treaty of Silverhaven**
- Proved that different species could find common ground
- Launched **King Aldric**'s reputation as a visionary peacemaker

### Long-term Consequences

The First Contact set in motion a chain of events that reshaped Aethelgard. The human-dwarven trade relationship created economic interdependence that discouraged conflict and encouraged cooperation. When the **War of Shattered Skies** threatened both species centuries later, the alliance forged at **Thunder Pass** provided the foundation for military cooperation that proved decisive. **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s role in the First Contact established his reputation as a bridge-builder between species, a legacy that continued through his later partnership with **Throndor the Wise** at the **Arcane College of Thaldor**. The annual commemoration—involving diplomatic message exchanges between **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Queen Elara**—reminds both species that cooperation begins with a single conversation.

## Historical Significance

The First Contact is regarded as the single most important diplomatic event in post-Sundering Aethelgard. It proved that species separated by biology, culture, and geography could establish peaceful, mutually beneficial relationships through direct communication. The Thunder Pass Trade Agreement became the template for every subsequent inter-species accord, including the **Treaty of Silverhaven**. **King Aldric the Unifier**'s decision to pursue contact rather than conquest defined human-dwarven relations for centuries and established a model that other species would eventually follow.
