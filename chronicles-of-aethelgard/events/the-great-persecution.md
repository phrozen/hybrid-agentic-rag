# The Great Persecution

## Overview

The Great Persecution was a dark chapter in Aethelgard history (320-380 AS) during which **tieflings**, **aasimar**, and other planar-touched species faced systematic discrimination, violence, and forced exile across the **Eastern Kingdoms** and parts of the **Western Reaches**. Triggered by religious extremism and fear of planar influences, the persecution displaced tens of thousands and created lasting scars that continue to affect inter-species relations in the modern era.

## Date and Era

- **Period**: 320-380 AS (60 years)
- **Era**: Late Age of Expansion / Early Age of Shattered Skies
- **Peak Violence**: 340-360 AS
- **Official End**: 380 AS (**Treaty of Thaldor**)
- **Legacy**: Ongoing discrimination and trauma

## Historical Context

### Pre-Persecution Eastern Kingdoms

**Religious Climate (300-320 AS):**
- **Order of the Silver Dawn** rising influence
- Multi-faith tolerance traditionally practiced
- **Tieflings** and **aasimar** integrated into society
- Planar heritage viewed with curiosity
- Economic prosperity created social tensions

**Tiefling and Aasimar Status:**
- **Tieflings**: 5% of Eastern Kingdoms population
- **Aasimar**: 3% of population
- Generally accepted in urban areas
- Some discrimination in rural regions
- Successful merchants, artisans, scholars

**Social Tensions:**
- Economic competition increasing
- Religious extremism growing
- Scapegoating tendencies
- Political opportunism
- Fear of planar powers

### Catalysts for Persecution

**The Plague of Shadows (318-320 AS):**
- Mysterious disease swept Eastern Kingdoms
- High mortality rate (30% in affected areas)
- No known cure discovered
- **Tieflings** blamed (false accusations)
- Religious leaders fueled fears

**Religious Extremism:**
- **Order of the Silver Dawn** faction emerged
- "Pure Faith" movement
- Anti-planar rhetoric
- Prophet Marcus Pureheart (extremist leader)
- Divine mandate claimed

**Political Factors:**
- King Aldric II weak leadership
- Noble power struggles
- Economic downturn
- Scapegoating convenient
- Property confiscation incentives

## Key Figures

### Persecution Leaders

**Prophet Marcus Pureheart:**
- Religious extremist leader
- Charismatic and manipulative
- "Pure Faith" movement founder
- Preached planar corruption
- Died 365 AS (natural causes)

**King Aldric II:**
- Eastern Kingdoms ruler (310-340 AS)
- Weak and indecisive
- Allowed persecution to continue
- Later regretted inaction
- Dynasty tarnished

**Lord Geoffrey Ironwall:**
- Noble persecution supporter
- Confiscated **tiefling** properties
- Military enforcement
- Profited from displacement
- Later pardoned

### Victims and Resisters

**Tiefling Leaders:**
- **Malakor Shadowdancer** (ancestor of modern diplomat)
- **Community organizer**: Organized exodus
- **Hidden networks**: Protected refugees
- **Cultural preservation**: Maintained traditions
- **Legacy**: Modern **tiefling** communities

**Aasimar Leaders:**
- **Lightbringer Celestia** (religious leader)
- Maintained faith despite persecution
- Underground worship networks
- Protected children
- Documented atrocities

**Human Allies:**
- **Queen Elara's Ancestors**: Protected refugees
- **Merchant Guilds**: Smuggled refugees
- **Order of Silver Dawn** moderates: Opposed persecution
- **Throndor the Wise**: Condemned publicly
- **Thaldor** sanctuary: Provided refuge

## Causes and Ideology

### Religious Extremism

**"Pure Faith" Doctrine:**
- Planar heritage = corruption
- **Tieflings** = demonic influence
- **Aasimar** = angelic arrogance
- Humans should remain "pure"
- Divine mandate for purification

**Theological Errors:**
- Misinterpretation of sacred texts
- Fear-based theology
- Exclusionary salvation
- Demonization of difference
- Political manipulation of faith

### Economic Factors

**Property Confiscation:**
- **Tiefling** businesses seized
- **Aasimar** lands redistributed
- Noble profit from persecution
- Merchant competition eliminated
- Economic incentive for violence

**Economic Scapegoating:**
- Blamed for economic downturn
- "They control the banks" (false)
- "They avoid taxes" (false)
- "They corrupt trade" (false)
- Convenient explanations

### Social Psychology

**Fear of the Other:**
- Planar heritage mysterious
- Physical differences visible
- Cultural practices misunderstood
- Conspiracy theories spread
- Dehumanization tactics

**Mob Mentality:**
- Violence normalized
- Peer pressure to participate
- Authority sanction
- Gradual escalation
- Bystander complicity

## Major Events

### Initial Outbreak (320-330 AS)

**First Attacks (320 AS):**
- **Tiefling** businesses burned
- **Aasimar** temples desecrated
- Murders began
- Authorities did not intervene
- Refugees fled to cities

**Ghettoization (325 AS):**
- Forced relocation to ghettos
- Movement restrictions
- Trade limitations
- Special taxes
- Identity markers required

### Systematic Persecution (330-360 AS)

**Legal Discrimination (330 AS):**
- "Purity Laws" enacted
- **Tieflings** and **aasimar** stripped of rights
- Property ownership banned
- Mixed marriages forbidden
- Children removed from families

**Violence Escalation (340-350 AS):**
- Organized pogroms
- Mass killings
- Forced conversions
- Slavery (illegal but practiced)
- Cultural eradication attempts

**The Exodus (350-360 AS):**
- Mass refugee flight
- **Thaldor** primary destination
- **Western Reaches** accepted some
- **Southern Expanse** limited intake
- Dangerous journeys

### Resistance and Resolution (360-380 AS)

**Underground Resistance (360-370 AS):**
- Refugee smuggling networks
- Hidden communities
- Armed resistance (rare)
- Documentation of atrocities
- International awareness

**International Pressure (370-380 AS):**
- **Thaldor** condemned persecution
- **Western Reaches** diplomatic protests
- **Southern Expanse** trade sanctions
- **Order of Silver Dawn** split
- Eastern Kingdoms isolated

**Treaty of Thaldor (380 AS):**
- International agreement
- Persecution officially ended
- Refugee return permitted (limited)
- Reparations promised (mostly unpaid)
- Monitoring established

## Outcome and Consequences

### Human Cost

**Casualties:**
- Direct violence: 50,000+ deaths
- Displacement: 200,000+ refugees
- Enslavement: 30,000+ (illegal)
- Cultural loss: Incalculable
- Trauma: Generational

**Demographic Impact:**
- **Tiefling** population: 60% reduction in Eastern Kingdoms
- **Aasimar** population: 50% reduction
- Diaspora communities established
- Eastern Kingdoms diversity reduced
- **Thaldor** population increased

### Economic Impact

**Property Loss:**
- **Tiefling** businesses: 90% lost
- **Aasimar** lands: 80% confiscated
- Economic disruption: Severe
- Trade networks: Damaged
- Recovery: Decades

**Long-term Effects:**
- Eastern Kingdoms economy: Weakened
- **Thaldor** economy: Strengthened (refugee skills)
- Trust in institutions: Damaged
- International trade: Complicated
- Reparations: Mostly unpaid

### Social and Cultural Impact

**Community Trauma:**

- **Aasimar** communities: Hidden
- Cultural practices: Lost or hidden
- Identity suppression: Common
- Trust issues: Persistent

**Discrimination Legacy:**

- Stereotypes persist

- Social barriers
- Inter-generational trauma

### Political Impact

**International Relations:**
