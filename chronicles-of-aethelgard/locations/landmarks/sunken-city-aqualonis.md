# The Sunken City of Aqualonis

## Overview

Aqualonis is an ancient underwater ruin located in **Crystal Bay**, **Southern Expanse**, originally a coastal **Lizardfolk** city that sank during a magical catastrophe in 320 AS. The submerged ruins now house a unique ecosystem of magical sea life, **Lizardfolk** shamans who can breathe underwater, and treasures lost when the city fell. **Chief Ssithik** maintains territorial claims, while **Dragonborn** salvagers and **Arcane College** researchers seek access to the ancient magical archives rumored to lie beneath the waves.

## Geography and Climate

Aqualonis rests in sixty feet of water roughly two miles off **Crystal Bay**'s harbor, on a submarine shelf that drops sharply into the deep ocean channel connecting the bay to the open sea. The ruins cover approximately one hundred acres of seabed, their stone streets and plaza floors still visible through the clear tropical water as a grid of pale lines amid the coral and kelp that have colonized the collapsed buildings. Visibility at the site varies with weather and season, averaging thirty feet during calm periods and dropping to near zero during storms that churn the bay's sediment.

The water temperature around Aqualonis remains remarkably constant year-round, roughly seventy degrees regardless of season, which marine scholars attribute to residual magical energy warming the surrounding substrate. This thermal anomaly supports an ecosystem unlike anything else in the **Southern Expanse**: luminescent fish that navigate the ruins' corridors, coral species found nowhere else in Aethelgard's waters, and the famous spirit-jellyfish, translucent creatures whose bioluminescence illuminates the city's interior chambers in shifting patterns of blue and green.

Access to Aqualonis is complicated by the strong currents that sweep across the submarine shelf, particularly during tidal changes when water rushes between the bay and the open ocean. Experienced divers learn to time their entries with the slack tide, and the **Lizardfolk** shamans who serve as the site's primary guardians navigate the currents with an ease that suggests either exceptional skill or innate aquatic adaptation. The ruins' depth means that **Human** divers cannot reach them without magical assistance, a fact that has limited archaeological exploration to those who can afford breathing enchantments or who possess natural underwater capability.

## History and Founding

Aqualonis was founded circa 200 AS as a coastal **Lizardfolk** trading port, built on the submarine shelf's shallow edge where the settlement could straddle land and water. The **Lizardfolk** who built Aqualonis were amphibious by nature, equally comfortable onshore and beneath the waves, and their architecture reflected this dual nature, with buildings that had entrances both above and below the waterline. The city prospered for over a century, becoming the **Southern Expanse**'s premier center for maritime trade and earning a reputation for the quality of its coral-carved artwork and its shamans' water magic.

The catastrophe that destroyed Aqualonis occurred in 320 AS, when a magical experiment gone wrong in the city's central temple triggered a sympathetic resonance in the submarine shelf's geological structure. The exact nature of the experiment remains disputed — **Lizardfolk** oral tradition preserves only fragments, and the **Arcane College of Thaldor**'s attempts to reconstruct events have produced competing theories — but the result was unequivocal: the shelf fractured, and Aqualonis slid into the bay over the course of a single night. The city's approximately five thousand residents had just enough warning to evacuate onto boats and swimming creatures, and while many escaped, the city's magical archives, temple treasures, and institutional knowledge were lost to the depths.

In the centuries since the sinking, **Lizardfolk** shamans have maintained a continuous presence in the ruins, establishing a small community of approximately fifty permanent residents who live in the city's intact lower chambers. These shamans possess a unique water-breathing ability derived from rituals performed at the city's central shrine, and they regard Aqualonis as sacred ground that must be protected from despoilers. **Chief Ssithik**, the current leader of the **Lizardfolk** territorial council, has asserted sovereignty over the ruins on behalf of his people, a claim that **Mayor Ironscale** of **Crystal Bay** has formally recognized while reserving the right to regulate access.

The discovery of intact magical archives in Aqualonis's lower chambers has transformed the site from a cultural curiosity into a strategic prize. The archives are believed to contain pre-**Sundering** texts on water magic, planar geometry, and the **Lizardfolk** civilization's astronomical observations, which the **Arcane College** considers invaluable for understanding Aethelgard's magical history. Negotiations over access have been ongoing for decades, complicated by the **Lizardfolk** shamans' insistence that the archives are sacred and the **Arcane College**'s conviction that knowledge belongs to all peoples.

## Population and Culture

Aqualonis's permanent population consists of roughly fifty **Lizardfolk** shamans who live in the ruins' intact lower chambers, supported by an additional two hundred **Lizardfolk** who live onshore and commute to the ruins for rituals and maintenance. The underwater community operates under strict ecological and spiritual protocols derived from the **Lizardfolk** tradition of treating the ocean as a living entity deserving respect. Waste is minimal, resources are harvested sustainably, and the shamans cultivate the coral and kelp gardens that give the ruins their distinctive appearance.

The shamans' water-breathing ability is not natural but ritual-enhanced, requiring periodic renewal at the city's central shrine. This dependency ties the community to the site and creates a strong incentive to protect the shrine from damage or defilement. The shamans communicate through a combination of **Lizardfolk** vocalizations and a sign language adapted for underwater use, though they can also speak Common when surfacing. Their culture is deeply insular, viewing surface-dwellers with suspicion born of centuries of experience with treasure hunters and would-be colonizers.

The onshore **Lizardfolk** community at **Crystal Bay** serves as intermediary between the underwater shamans and the surface world, handling trade, diplomacy, and the complex negotiations over archaeological access. **Chief Ssithik** leads the onshore council and has proven an effective advocate for **Lizardfolk** interests, resisting pressure from both **Mayor Ironscale** and **Lady Zara Goldscale** to open the archives without adequate safeguards. His position is strengthened by the **Lizardfolk** shamans' unique ability to navigate the ruins safely, which makes unauthorized exploration effectively impossible without their cooperation.

## Economy and Trade

Aqualonis itself generates little direct economic activity, but the ruins' cultural and historical value has created a significant indirect economy. The **Lizardfolk** community sells limited diving access to approved researchers, with fees that support both the onshore community and the underwater guardians. The **Arcane College of Thaldor** funds expeditions under **Chief Ssithik**'s supervision, and the resulting research generates publications that enhance the **Southern Expanse**'s reputation as a center of magical scholarship.

More commercially significant is the harvest of Aqualonis's unique marine resources. The spirit-jellyfish's bioluminescent compounds are used in **Gnomish** optical instruments, the coral species found only in the ruins produce pigments valued by artists across Aethelgard, and the medicinal herbs cultivated by the **Lizardfolk** shamans in the kelp gardens have proven effective against conditions that resist conventional treatment. **Mayor Ironscale** has established carefully regulated harvesting quotas that protect the ecosystem while generating revenue.

Tourism is limited but growing. Glass-bottomed boat tours operate from **Crystal Bay**'s harbor, allowing visitors to view the ruins from above without disturbing the site. More ambitious tourists can arrange guided dives with **Lizardfolk** shamans, though only the upper ruins are accessible to visitors and strict time limits apply. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** has proposed building a permanent underwater viewing gallery, but **Chief Ssithik** has rejected the plan as disrespectful to the sacred site.

## Notable Features

### The Central Shrine

The city's spiritual heart, a domed chamber where the magical catastrophe originated. The shrine's ceiling bears scorch marks from the failed experiment, but the altar itself remains intact and continues to generate the water-breathing enchantment that sustains the **Lizardfolk** shamans. The shrine is visible from above during calm weather as a darker patch in the ruins' center, and its preservation is the shamans' highest priority.

### The Archives Tower

A partially intact tower in the city's eastern district that houses the pre-**Sundering** texts the **Arcane College** most desires to study. The tower's lower three floors are flooded but structurally sound, and the **Lizardfolk** shamans maintain careful environmental controls to preserve the water-damaged scrolls and codices within. Access requires both **Chief Ssithik**'s permission and a **Lizardfolk** escort familiar with the tower's layout.

### The Coral Gardens

The shamans' most visible achievement, a network of cultivated coral and kelp beds that have transformed the ruins' open spaces into an underwater garden of extraordinary beauty. The gardens serve practical purposes — food cultivation, water filtration, and habitat for the fish that sustain the community — but they are also an expression of **Lizardfolk** aesthetic values, with color arrangements that shift seasonally and compositions that echo the ruins' original architecture.

## Rulers and Leaders

### Chief Ssithik

The appointed leader of the **Lizardfolk** territorial council and the primary voice of **Lizardfolk** interests in the **Southern Expanse**, **Chief Ssithik** is a pragmatic negotiator who has balanced development pressure against cultural preservation for two decades. His relationship with **Mayor Ironscale** is cordial but cautious, built on mutual benefit rather than trust. He has rejected multiple offers from **Lady Zara Goldscale** to purchase archival access, insisting that the archives' contents will be shared only when adequate protections are in place.

### Shamaness Thylassa

The senior **Lizardfolk** shaman responsible for the underwater community's spiritual welfare and the central shrine's maintenance, **Thylassa** has lived in the ruins for forty years and knows every intact chamber. She is deeply suspicious of surface-dwellers and has opposed every proposal to expand access, arguing that the city's destruction was caused by the same kind of unchecked curiosity that researchers bring. Her opinion carries significant weight in the territorial council.

## Connected Events

- **The Magical Catastrophe of 320 AS**: The disaster that sank Aqualonis remains the defining event in **Lizardfolk** cultural memory, shaping the community's cautious approach to magic and their insistence on controlling access to the ruins' remaining archives
- **Crystal Bay Free Port Declaration**: **Chief Ssithik** negotiated formal recognition of **Lizardfolk** sovereignty over the ruins as part of the declaration, securing territorial claims that have protected the site from uncontrolled scavenging by **Dragonborn** salvagers
- **War of Shattered Skies**: The ruins served as a hidden anchorage for **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s smaller vessels during the Battle of the Shattered Strait, and the **Lizardfolk** shamans' knowledge of local currents provided tactical advantages that contributed to the victory
