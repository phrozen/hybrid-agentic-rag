# Thunder Pass

## Overview

Thunder Pass is a strategic mountain pass through the **Stormpeak Mountains**, serving as the primary land route between the **Eastern Kingdoms** and **Western Reaches**. Named for the frequent thunderstorms that rage through the narrow defile, the pass has been the site of numerous battles throughout history, most notably the **Battle of Thunder Pass** (380 AS) where **Grimjaw Ironhand** led dwarven forces to victory against overwhelming odds. Fortified by **King Thorin Stoneforge**, the pass remains a critical trade route and defensive chokepoint.

## Geography and Climate

### Location and Topography

**Geographic Position:**
- **Mountain Range**: **Stormpeak Mountains**
- **Elevation**: 8,500 feet at summit
- **Length**: 12 miles (traversable route)
- **Width**: 200-500 feet (varies)
- **Coordinates**: Central Stormpeaks

**Topographical Features:**
- Narrow defile (natural chokepoint)
- Steep cliff walls (1,000+ feet high)
- Rocky outcroppings (defensive positions)
- Boulders and debris (natural barriers)
- Single main route (easily controlled)

**Approaches:**
- **Eastern Approach**: Gradual ascent (3 miles)
- **Western Approach**: Steeper climb (4 miles)
- **Summit**: Flat area (fortress location)
- **Switchbacks**: Multiple (defensible)
- **Alternative Routes**: None practical

### Climate

**Alpine Weather Patterns:**
- **Thunderstorms**: Frequent (pass name source)
- **Wind**: Strong channeling effect
- **Temperature**: Variable (elevation)
- **Precipitation**: Heavy (rain and snow)
- **Visibility**: Often poor

**Seasonal Conditions:**
- **Spring** (Months 1-3): Snowmelt, mud, dangerous
- **Summer** (Months 4-6): Thunderstorms daily
- **Autumn** (Months 7-9): Clear but cold
- **Winter** (Months 10-12): Snow, often closed

**Thunderstorm Phenomena:**
- Daily storms (summer months)
- Lightning strikes common
- Thunder echoes amplify
- Flash floods possible
- Magical resonance (theorized)
