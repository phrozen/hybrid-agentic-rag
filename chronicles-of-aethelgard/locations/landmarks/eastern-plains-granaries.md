# The Eastern Plains Granaries

## Overview

The Eastern Plains Granaries are massive agricultural storage facilities operated by the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** throughout the **Eastern Plains**, ensuring **Silverhaven**'s food security under **Queen Elara**'s strategic reserves. **Poppy Greenfields**' techniques maximize grain preservation, and the granaries' magical climate control prevents spoilage. During the **War of Shattered Skies**, the granaries sustained **Eastern Kingdoms** through sieges, and they remain critical to regional stability.

## Geography and Climate

The Eastern Plains Granaries are distributed across the **Eastern Plains**' most productive agricultural region, a flat expanse of fertile loam stretching west from the River Aethel to the foothills of the central highlands. Each granary complex consists of a central storage tower surrounded by processing buildings, workers' housing, and the magical infrastructure that keeps millions of bushels of grain from spoiling. The towers are visible for miles across the open plains, their white silhouettes serving as landmarks for travelers and navigation points for the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative**'s distribution network.

The **Eastern Plains** climate is ideal for grain cultivation: warm summers with regular rainfall, cold winters that kill pests naturally, and reliable spring thaws that allow predictable planting schedules. The granaries' locations were chosen to minimize transport distances from the surrounding fields, with each complex serving roughly fifty square miles of farmland. The climate control enchantments inside the granaries maintain constant temperature and humidity regardless of external conditions, a necessity in a region where summer heat can bake grain and winter moisture can foster mold.

The granary network is connected by maintained roads that converge on **Silverhaven**, where the strategic reserve is stored in the largest and most heavily fortified complex. These roads are **Queen Elara**'s personal investment, paved and patrolled to ensure that food can reach the capital even during crisis. Cross-roads connect the granaries to each other, allowing redistribution when one region experiences crop failure and another has surplus. **Poppy Greenfields** has proposed extending this network to **Crystal Bay** and **Grommashar**, though funding for expansion remains uncertain.

## History and Founding

The Eastern Plains Granaries were not built as a single project but evolved over centuries from scattered family storehouses into the coordinated network that exists today. The **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** traces its origins to 280 AS, when a series of failed harvests convinced **Halfling** farming communities that individual storage was insufficient to weather natural disasters. The first cooperative granary was a converted watchtower in the central plains, its thick stone walls providing natural insulation that kept grain cool in summer and above freezing in winter.

The network expanded steadily over the following century, with new granaries constructed as farming communities grew and old facilities were upgraded. The critical innovation came in 390 AS, when **Poppy Greenfields** — then a recently graduated agricultural scientist from **Thaldor**'s research programs — joined the Cooperative and introduced magical climate control systems that eliminated spoilage entirely. **Greenfields**' techniques, refined over decades of field trials, remain the standard for grain preservation across Aethelgard and have been adopted by **Crystal Bay** and **Silvermoon** for their own food reserves.

The **War of Shattered Skies** demonstrated the granaries' strategic value beyond all expectation. When enemy forces besieged **Silverhaven** and cut overland supply routes, the granary network's distributed design allowed the **Eastern Kingdoms** to continue feeding its population even while individual complexes were isolated. **Poppy Greenfields** orchestrated a brilliant redistribution campaign, routing grain through back roads and hidden paths that the enemy never discovered, ensuring that no **Eastern Kingdoms** citizen starved during the siege. **Queen Elara** later cited this achievement as the single most important factor in the kingdom's wartime resilience.

Postwar expansion doubled the network's capacity, with **Queen Elara** funding new construction and the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** providing operational expertise. The strategic reserve at **Silverhaven** was expanded to hold eighteen months of emergency rations, and each regional granary was equipped with the defensive fortifications that had proven necessary during the war. The network today is a model of coordinated agricultural infrastructure that **Stonehold** and **Drakkenheim** have studied with an eye toward replicating.

## Population and Culture

Each granary complex supports a permanent staff of thirty to fifty **Halfling** workers, supplemented by seasonal labor during harvest and distribution periods. The network's total workforce numbers approximately two thousand, making the **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative** one of the **Eastern Kingdoms**' largest employers. Workers live in housing provided by the Cooperative, creating small communities clustered around each granary tower that develop distinct personalities based on their local leadership and the crops they process.

**Halfling** culture dominates the granary communities, with an emphasis on communal meals, shared childcare, and collective decision-making that contrasts with the hierarchical structures of **Silverhaven**'s urban institutions. Each granary elects a manager from among its workers, and the managers convene quarterly to set network-wide policy, approve budgets, and resolve disputes. **Poppy Greenfields** serves as the network's chief scientist and de facto executive director, a role she assumed after the previous director retired following the **War of Shattered Skies**.

The seasonal workforce that swells the granary communities during harvest includes **Human** farmhands from the surrounding countryside, **Orcish** laborers from **Grommashar** who have found steady employment in the **Eastern Plains**, and **Gnomish** engineers who maintain the climate control systems. This diversity creates cultural friction that the Cooperative manages through clear workplace rules and a profit-sharing system that aligns incentives across ethnic lines. The annual Harvest Festival, held simultaneously at every granary complex, celebrates the network's multicultural workforce with food, music, and storytelling from every tradition represented.

## Economy and Trade

The Eastern Plains Granaries are the **Eastern Kingdoms**' agricultural backbone, storing and distributing the grain harvest that feeds approximately eight million people. The granary network processes wheat, barley, oats, rye, and corn, with specialty crops like **Poppy Greenfields**' drought-resistant quinoa handled at specific facilities equipped for their unique storage requirements. The Cooperative sells grain to **Silverhaven**'s markets at market rates, with the strategic reserve portion purchased by the crown at a guaranteed minimum price that protects farmers from price fluctuations.

The granaries' economic impact extends beyond storage. Processing facilities at each complex clean, sort, and mill grain before distribution, adding value that would otherwise be captured by urban millers. The Cooperative operates its own distribution fleet of river barges and wagons, employing drivers, boatmen, and warehouse workers throughout the **Eastern Kingdoms**. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** has invested in the fleet's expansion, recognizing that controlling food distribution confers significant commercial advantages.

International trade through the granaries is growing. **Crystal Bay** purchases grain for **Southern Expanse** markets that cannot be served locally, and **Stonehold** has begun importing **Halfling** agricultural technology adapted for **Dwarven** underground farming. **Poppy Greenfields** manages these contracts personally, ensuring that exports never compromise the strategic reserve's eighteen-month target. The network's reputation for reliability during the **War of Shattered Skies** makes international buyers confident that their contracts will be honored regardless of circumstances, a trust that **Greenfields** guards as the Cooperative's most valuable asset.

## Notable Features

### The Strategic Reserve Tower

The largest and most fortified granary complex, located outside **Silverhaven**'s walls, the Strategic Reserve Tower holds eighteen months of emergency rations for the capital's population. The tower is protected by military garrison, warded against magical attack, and surrounded by a cleared perimeter that makes ambush impossible. **Queen Elara** personally inspects the reserve annually, a tradition that reinforces the crown's commitment to food security.

### The Greenfields Climate Control System

**Poppy Greenfields**' signature innovation, a network of magical enchantments that maintain constant temperature and humidity within each granary tower. The system uses elemental binding techniques adapted from **Thaldor**'s **Arcane College**, modified for agricultural rather than combat applications. Each granary's system is maintained by a resident **Gnomish** engineer who monitors enchantment integrity and performs routine calibrations.

### The Redundancy Network

The granaries' most important structural feature, visible only on **Poppy Greenfields**' operational maps, is the system of backup roads, hidden caches, and alternative distribution routes that allowed the Cooperative to feed **Eastern Kingdoms** during the **War of Shattered Skies**. The network has been expanded postwar, and its details remain classified even from most Cooperative employees, known only to **Greenfields** and her senior staff.

## Rulers and Leaders

### Poppy Greenfields

The **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative**'s chief scientist and operational director, **Poppy Greenfields** is the single most important figure in the **Eastern Kingdoms**' food supply chain. A small, energetic **Halfling** woman with perpetually soil-stained hands and an encyclopedic knowledge of grain biology, she is respected rather than beloved by her workers, who find her standards demanding but her judgment infallible. She reports directly to **Queen Elara** on strategic reserve matters and operates otherwise with remarkable autonomy.

### Manager Thaddeus Barrow

The elected manager of the Strategic Reserve Tower and **Poppy Greenfields**' de facto second-in-command, **Thaddeus** is a meticulous **Halfling** who maintains the reserve with religious devotion. His quarterly inspections are legendary for their thoroughness, and he once rejected an entire shipment because a single mouse was found in the sample bin. **Queen Elara** has praised him publicly as "the reason the people of **Silverhaven** sleep soundly."

## Connected Events

- **War of Shattered Skies**: The granaries' distributed design and **Poppy Greenfields**' brilliant redistribution campaign kept the **Eastern Kingdoms** fed during the siege of **Silverhaven**, proving that food security infrastructure is as vital as military defenses
- **Queen Elara's Strategic Reserves**: The postwar expansion of the strategic reserve was **Queen Elara**'s direct response to the lessons of the siege, ensuring that no future conflict could threaten the **Eastern Kingdoms**' food supply
- **Halfling Agricultural Cooperative Founding**: The Cooperative's creation in 280 AS established the organizational framework that makes the granary network possible, transforming individual **Halfling** storehouses into a coordinated regional infrastructure
