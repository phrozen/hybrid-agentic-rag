# The Stonehold Treasury

## Overview

The Stonehold Treasury is **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s vault complex containing dwarven gold reserves, the **Mithril Vaults**, and clan heirlooms including **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s personal artifacts. Located in **Stonehold**'s deepest secure levels, the Treasury employs **Ironhand Guild** runewards, **General Ironfist**'s guards, and multiple physical barriers. The Treasury backs dwarven currency, funds **King Thorin**'s initiatives, and stores artifacts too dangerous for the **Hall of Archmages**.

## Location and Architecture

The Stonehold Treasury occupies five levels beneath **Stonehold**'s royal palace, carved into solid granite and reinforced with **Ironhand Guild** runemagic. The complex descends 800 feet below the mountain's surface, making it one of the most secure locations in Aethelgard.

### Treasury Levels

**Level 1 - Currency Vault**: Stores gold reserves backing dwarven currency. **King Thorin Stoneforge** authorizes withdrawals for kingdom expenses.

**Level 2 - Mithril Vaults**: Contains rare mithril ingots and artifacts. Only **King Thorin** and **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** possess keys.

**Level 3 - Clan Heirlooms**: Houses artifacts from major dwarven clans, including **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s personal hammer and armor.

**Level 4 - Dangerous Artifacts**: Secures cursed or unstable magical items. **Arcane College of Thaldor** researchers request access for study.

**Level 5 - Royal Vault**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s personal collection, including the **Stoneforge Hammer** ancestral weapon.

## History and Founding

The Stonehold Treasury was constructed in 298 AS during **King Durin Stoneforge**'s reign, **King Thorin**'s grandfather. The Treasury was designed to centralize dwarven wealth and protect artifacts from the **War of Shattered Skies**.

### Construction Period (298-320 AS)

**Grimjaw Ironhand's Contribution**: The legendary runemaster inscribed protective wards on vault doors. His runes still protect the Treasury 200 years later.

**Ironhand Guild Establishment**: **Grimjaw** trained the first generation of Treasury wardens, teaching them runemagic maintenance.

**Royal Decree**: **King Durin** mandated that all dwarven clans deposit heirlooms in the Treasury for safekeeping.

### Modern Era (320-450 AS)

**War of Shattered Skies**: Treasury successfully protected artifacts during orcish sieges. **General Ironfist**'s forces defended access tunnels.

**King Thorin's Expansion**: Current king added Level 5 for dangerous artifacts, consulting **Arcane College** advisors on containment.

**International Recognition**: **Queen Elara** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** acknowledge Treasury's security, storing some human and elven artifacts there.

## Security Measures

The Stonehold Treasury employs multiple layers of security:

### Physical Barriers

**Granite Doors**: Each level sealed with 10-ton granite doors, carved from single blocks.

**Guard Stations**: **General Ironfist** stations 50 elite dwarven guards at Treasury entrances.

**Trap Corridors**: Intruders face falling rocks, poison darts, and flood tunnels.

### Magical Wards

**Ironhand Runes**: **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s original wards detect unauthorized entry and alert guards.

**Alarm Systems**: Magical alarms notify **King Thorin**, **General Ironfist**, and **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** simultaneously.

**Containment Fields**: Level 5 artifacts sealed in anti-magic fields designed by **Arcane College of Thaldor** mages.

### Personnel Security

**Background Checks**: All Treasury staff undergo **General Ironfist**'s loyalty investigations.

**Rotation Schedule**: Guards rotated weekly to prevent corruption.

**Dual Control**: Two authorized personnel required to open vault doors.

## Notable Contents

The Treasury houses artifacts of immense value and power:

### Currency Reserves

**Gold Ingots**: 10,000 tons backing dwarven currency. **King Thorin Stoneforge** authorizes minting new coins from these reserves.

**Mithril Ingots**: 500 tons of rare mithril, worth more than gold. Used for royal gifts and critical enchantments.

### Clan Heirlooms

**Grimjaw Ironhand's Hammer**: The runemaster's personal tool, still used ceremonially by **Ironhand Guild** masters.

**Stoneforge Crown**: **King Thorin**'s coronation crown, worn only for major ceremonies.

**Ancestral Armor**: Armor from legendary dwarven heroes, including **King Durin**'s battle plate.

### Dangerous Artifacts

**Cursed Blades**: Weapons from the **War of Shattered Skies**, too dangerous for the **Hall of Archmages**.

**Unstable Orbs**: Magical experiments from **Arcane College of Thaldor**, stored pending safe disposal.

**Corrupted Runes**: Failed **Ironhand Guild** enchantments, studied by **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**.

## Governance and Access

### Royal Authority

**King Thorin Stoneforge** holds ultimate authority over the Treasury. His personal seal authorizes all withdrawals and deposits. The king visits the Treasury monthly to inspect contents.

### Advisory Council

**King Thorin** consults a Treasury council including:
- **General Ironfist**: Security oversight
- **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**: Spiritual and artifact authentication
- **Ironhand Guild Master**: Runemagic maintenance
- **Royal Treasurer**: Financial management

### Access Protocols

**Research Access**: **Arcane College of Thaldor** researchers request Level 4 access through **King Thorin**'s court.

**Diplomatic Storage**: **Queen Elara** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** store sensitive artifacts in Level 3, accessible only with dual authorization.

**Emergency Withdrawals**: **General Ironfist** can authorize defensive withdrawals during sieges, with **King Thorin**'s retroactive approval.

## Connected Characters

- **King Thorin Stoneforge**: Dwarf king, ultimate Treasury authority
- **General Ironfist**: Security commander, defends Treasury access
- **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge**: Spiritual advisor, authenticates artifacts
- **Grimjaw Ironhand**: Legendary runemaster, designed original wards
- **Ironhand Guild Master**: Maintains **Grimjaw**'s runemagic

## Connected Locations

- **Stonehold**: Dwarf mountain capital containing the Treasury
- **Hall of Archmages**: **Thaldor** museum storing less dangerous artifacts
- **Ironhand Guild Headquarters**: **Stonehold** runemagic workshop maintaining Treasury wards
- **Royal Palace**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s residence above the Treasury
