# The Tiefling Memorial Garden

## Overview

The Tiefling Memorial Garden is **Thaldor**'s memorial to **The Great Persecution** victims, maintained by the **Tiefling Refugee Network** and **Order of the Radiant Dawn**. The garden lists victims' names, houses survivor testimonies, and serves as a reminder of religious persecution's dangers. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** sponsors annual remembrance ceremonies, and visiting dignitaries including **Queen Elara** and **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** pay respects here.

## Geography and Climate

The Tiefling Memorial Garden occupies a quiet quadrangle in **Thaldor**'s old quarter, bounded on three sides by the **Arcane College** campus and opening on the fourth to a cobblestoned street that connects the garden to the city's main thoroughfare. The site was chosen deliberately: it sits on the former location of the **Thaldor** Inquisition's headquarters, where **Tiefling** prisoners were held and interrogated during **the Great Persecution**. The building was demolished after the persecution ended, and the garden was planted in its footprint, with each flower bed and path following the outline of a former cell or interrogation chamber.

The garden's design uses the contrast between natural beauty and historical horror to powerful effect. Walking paths wind through beds of white roses — the **Tiefling** community's symbol of remembrance — past stone walls inscribed with victims' names, and through groves of trees whose canopy filters light into patterns that shift with the seasons. In spring, the garden is carpeted with wildflowers that self-seed from year to year, a tradition that began when survivors scattered seeds from their destroyed homes. In winter, the bare branches and grey stone create a somber atmosphere that matches the garden's purpose.

The climate is typical of **Thaldor**'s inland location: cold winters, warm summers, and moderate precipitation year-round. The garden's landscape architect, a **Tiefling** designer named Kael Duskbloom who survived the persecution as a child, selected plant species that would thrive in each season and provide visual interest even during the dormant months. Evergreen hedges define the garden's perimeter, and a central reflecting pool remains unfrozen through enchantments maintained by the **Arcane College**, symbolizing the persistence of memory even through the coldest times.

Acoustic design was a critical consideration. The garden is surrounded by spell-dampening barriers that reduce the noise of **Thaldor**'s streets to a gentle murmur, ensuring that visitors can read the inscriptions and reflect in peace. The barriers are invisible from within the garden, creating an impression of boundless tranquility that belies the urban setting. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** personally approved the enchantments, overruling colleagues who argued that the garden should face the city's noise as a reminder that persecution occurred in plain sight.

## History and Founding

The Tiefling Memorial Garden was conceived in 445 AS, the year the **War of Shattered Skies** ended and **Thaldor**'s attention turned from external enemies to internal reckoning. The persecution of **Tiefling** people that had scarred the city for generations could no longer be ignored or excused, and the **Tiefling Refugee Network** — then a loose coalition of survivors and their descendants — demanded a permanent memorial that would force **Thaldor** to confront its past.

The campaign for the garden was led by **Father Tomas Windwalker**, a **Tiefling** cleric who had spent the war years documenting persecution survivors' testimonies. **Father Windwalker** argued that the memorial must be located on the site of the Inquisition's headquarters, making it impossible for future generations to visit **Thaldor** without encountering the evidence of what had been done in their city's name. The proposal faced opposition from **Thaldor** residents who preferred to forget, from property owners who valued the centrally located site, and from some **Arcane College** faculty who considered the project emotionally rather than academically motivated.

**Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** broke the deadlock by personally endorsing the project and allocating **Arcane College** funds for its construction. His decision was controversial within the **College**, but **Thornfield** argued that an institution dedicated to knowledge could not ignore evidence of historical atrocity. The garden was dedicated in 448 AS in a ceremony attended by **Queen Elara**, **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**, and over three thousand **Tiefling** survivors and their families. **Father Windwalker** gave the dedication address, speaking for forty minutes without notes, and concluded with the words inscribed on the garden's entrance: "What we remember, we cannot repeat."

The garden has since become one of **Thaldor**'s most visited sites, drawing pilgrims from across Aethelgard who come to read the names, hear the testimonies, and walk the paths that trace the outlines of suffering. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** maintains the garden in partnership with the **Tiefling Refugee Network**, ensuring that the flora remains healthy, the inscriptions legible, and the atmosphere conducive to contemplation. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** sponsors an annual remembrance ceremony on the anniversary of the persecution's official end, and his personal attendance has become a tradition that his successors will be expected to continue.

## Population and Culture

The Tiefling Memorial Garden has no permanent residential population, but it is never truly empty. The **Order of the Radiant Dawn** maintains a staff of twelve gardeners and docents who work in rotating shifts, and a **Tiefling** honor guard stands watch at the garden's entrance from dawn to dusk. Visitors arrive throughout the day, their numbers swelling on anniversaries and during **Thaldor**'s academic year, when **Arcane College** students are required to visit as part of their ethics curriculum.

The garden's culture is one of quiet reflection and mutual respect. Visitors speak in hushed tones, remove hats before entering the name-inscribed corridors, and observe a tradition of leaving small offerings — stones, flowers, or handwritten messages — at the base of the central monument. The **Tiefling Refugee Network** collects these offerings monthly, archiving the messages in the garden's underground repository and distributing the flowers to **Tiefling** elders in the community.

Monthly testimony readings draw the garden's largest regular gatherings. Survivors volunteer to share their experiences in the garden's amphitheater, and audiences of students, citizens, and visiting dignitaries listen in silence. These readings are emotionally intense and have become a rite of passage for **Arcane College** students, who report that hearing persecution described by those who survived it transforms abstract history into visceral understanding. The **Bards' College** sends students to document the readings, and selected testimonies have been published in a volume that has become required reading across the **Eastern Kingdoms**.

## Economy and Trade

The Tiefling Memorial Garden operates on a nonprofit basis, funded by the **Arcane College**, the **Order of the Radiant Dawn**, and donations from visitors and supporters. The garden charges no admission fee, a deliberate policy decision that **Father Windwalker** insisted upon, arguing that charging money to view the evidence of persecution would be obscene. Operating costs are modest — garden maintenance, docent stipends, and the enchantment upkeep for the reflecting pool and acoustic barriers — and are covered by a permanent endowment established at the garden's founding.

The garden's indirect economic impact is significant. Visitors who come to **Thaldor** specifically for the memorial stay in local inns, eat in local restaurants, and shop in local stores, generating revenue that the city has come to value. The **Arcane College** has noted that applicant numbers increase in years following high-profile testimony readings, suggesting that the garden enhances the institution's reputation for ethical seriousness. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind** has observed that **Crystal Bay** merchants who visit the garden before trade negotiations approach discussions with greater empathy, a benefit he did not anticipate but has learned to leverage.

The published testimony volume, edited by **Father Windwalker** and the **Bards' College**, generates modest royalties that flow directly to the **Tiefling Refugee Network**'s support programs for survivors and their descendants. The volume is required reading in **Silverhaven University**'s history faculty and has been translated into seven languages, making the garden's mission known far beyond **Thaldor**'s walls.

## Notable Features

### The Wall of Names

The garden's most emotionally powerful feature, a curved stone wall inscribed with the names of every known **Tiefling** victim of **the Great Persecution**, currently totaling over twelve thousand entries. New names are added as research uncovers previously unknown victims, and the wall's curved design leaves space for additions, a deliberate acknowledgment that the full extent of the persecution may never be known. The names are carved in **Tiefling** script with Common translations below, and visitors often spend hours tracing specific names with their fingers.

### The Testimony Archive

An underground repository beneath the garden that stores written, transcribed, and magically recorded survivor testimonies, accessible to researchers with **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield**'s approval and to **Tiefling** descendants seeking family records. The archive contains over three thousand individual testimonies, making it the most comprehensive documentation of **the Great Persecution** in existence. A **Silverhaven University** scholarship program funds annual research projects in the archive.

### The Reflecting Pool

A central pool of still water, enchanted to remain unfrozen year-round, where visitors toss coins that are collected monthly and donated to the **Tiefling Refugee Network**. The pool's surface reflects the sky overhead, creating a visual metaphor that the garden's designer intended: memory reflecting the present, the past visible in the contemporary world. The pool is also where **Father Windwalker** delivers his annual remembrance address, standing at the water's edge with the names carved on the wall visible behind him.

## Rulers and Leaders

### Father Tomas Windwalker

The garden's spiritual and operational director, **Father Windwalker** is a **Tiefling** survivor of **the Great Persecution** who has dedicated his life to preserving the memory of those who did not survive. His annual remembrance address is one of the **Eastern Kingdoms**' most important public rituals, attended by **Queen Elara** and broadcast by the **Bards' College** to audiences across Aethelgard. His personal authority within the **Tiefling Refugee Network** is absolute, and his insistence on confronting uncomfortable truths has shaped the garden's character.

### Curator Maren Ashworth

A **Human** archivist who manages the testimony archive and coordinates the **Silverhaven University** research program, **Maren** approaches her work with a scholar's precision and a survivor's empathy — her own grandmother was persecuted during **the Great Persecution**, though her family concealed their **Tiefling** heritage for generations. She works closely with **Dean Callista Brightwater** at **Silverhaven University** to ensure that the archive's contents are both protected and accessible to legitimate researchers.

## Connected Events

- **The Great Persecution**: The garden exists specifically because of this atrocity, and its location on the site of the **Thaldor** Inquisition's headquarters makes it a physical confrontation with the persecution's history that cannot be avoided or minimized
- **War of Shattered Skies**: The garden's construction was delayed by the war, and the postwar determination to confront both external and internal threats to peace gave the project the political momentum it needed to overcome opposition
- **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield's Endorsement**: The **Archchancellor**'s personal decision to fund and support the garden transformed it from a **Tiefling** community project into an institutional commitment by **Thaldor**'s most prestigious academic body, ensuring its permanence and credibility
