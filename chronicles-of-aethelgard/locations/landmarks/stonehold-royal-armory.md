# The Stonehold Royal Armory

## Overview

The Stonehold Royal Armory is **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s primary weapons manufacturing facility, producing the finest dwarven armor and weapons in Aethelgard. The Armory employs **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s runemagic techniques under **Ironhand Guild** supervision, creating enchanted equipment for dwarven warriors and export customers. The Armory's master smiths are legendary, and their waiting list extends years into the future for custom commissions.

## Geography and Climate

The Stonehold Royal Armory occupies a vast complex carved deep within **Stormpeak Mountains**, situated beneath **Stonehold**'s royal palace. The mountain's natural geothermal heat fuels the Armory's forges, while underground rivers provide water for quenching and cooling. The volcanic warmth keeps the forges burning year-round, and the surrounding granite provides natural insulation that maintains consistent working temperatures regardless of surface weather.

The Armory's entrance faces **Thunder Pass**, the critical trade route guarded by **General Ironfist**'s forces. This strategic positioning allows the Armory to supply both dwarven defenders and trade caravans heading toward the **Eastern Kingdoms**. The deep-mountain location also protects the Armory from aerial assault and magical attack, with multiple reinforced entrances and emergency evacuation tunnels.

## History and Founding

The Armory was established in 280 AS by **King Durin Stoneforge**, **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s grandfather, who recognized the need for centralized dwarven weapons production as border conflicts with raiding tribes intensified. The original forge complex was modest—a single great hall with twelve master forges—but grew steadily over the centuries.

When **Grimjaw Ironhand** joined the royal smiths in 320 AS, he revolutionized Armory production by introducing runemagic inscription techniques that enhanced weapon durability and cutting power without compromising structural integrity. The **Throndor-Ironhand runes**, developed in collaboration with the elven archmage **Throndor the Wise**, became the Armory's signature enchantment and remain its most sought-after product.

During the **War of Shattered Skies**, the Armory operated around the clock, producing weapons and armor for the allied coalition forces. **Runelord Dain Ironfist** coordinated production schedules with **Queen Elara**'s military commanders, ensuring that critical front-line units received priority shipments. The Armory's output during this period exceeded all previous records, with smiths sleeping in shifts to keep the forges burning.

After the **Treaty of Silverhaven**, the Armory transitioned from wartime production to peacetime commerce. **Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge** negotiated trade agreements with **King Thorin**'s allies, opening new markets for dwarven craftsmanship across **Aethelgard**. The Armory now produces luxury items alongside military equipment, with enchanted jewelry and ceremonial armor commanding premium prices.

## Population and Culture

The Armory employs approximately 3,000 dwarven smiths, apprentices, and support staff, making it one of **Stonehold**'s largest employers. Master smiths are organized into guilds by specialty—weaponry, armor, runemagic, and ceremonial—with each guild maintaining strict quality standards and apprenticeship traditions.

The culture within the Armory emphasizes precision, patience, and pride in craftsmanship. A single enchanted dwarven sword can take months to complete, and master smiths refuse to rush their work regardless of demand. This dedication to quality has earned the Armory an unparalleled reputation, though it also means that customers wait years for custom commissions.

**General Ironfist** maintains an honor guard of 200 dwarven warriors equipped with Armory products, serving as both security force and living advertisement for the Armory's craftsmanship. These guards accompany diplomatic missions and trade caravans, demonstrating dwarven weapons and armor to potential customers.

## Economy and Trade

The Armory's economic impact on **Stonehold** and the broader dwarven economy is immense. Enchanted weapons and armor command prices ten to fifty times their mundane counterparts, and the Armory's reputation for quality ensures consistent demand from across **Aethelgard**.

Primary trade partners include **Queen Elara**'s **Eastern Kingdoms** military, which purchases standardized equipment for the royal army, and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn Clans**, who commission custom weapons honoring their draconic heritage. **Admiral Hornbreaker** also sources naval weapons and ship fittings from the Armory for the **Crystal Bay** fleet.

The Armory's waiting list for custom commissions currently extends seven years, with some customers depositing gold at birth to ensure their children receive ceremonial weapons for adulthood. This demand allows **King Thorin** to set premium prices while maintaining the exclusivity that enhances the Armory's reputation.

## Notable Features

The Armory contains several remarkable features that distinguish it from ordinary smithies:

The **Great Forge Hall** houses twelve ancient forges, each blessed by **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** with **Moradin**'s sacred flames. These forges have burned continuously since the Armory's founding, their fires never extinguished. Master smiths believe that allowing a forge fire to die brings misfortune to the next weapon created upon relighting.

The **Runemagic Inscription Chamber** is where **Ironhand Guild** masters apply enchanted runes to finished weapons. The chamber is lined with **Grimjaw Ironhand**'s original runic diagrams, preserved in bronze frames as both teaching tools and historical artifacts. Only certified runemasters are permitted to work in this chamber, and each inscription is personally verified by **Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge**.

The **Gallery of Masterworks** displays the Armory's finest creations across three centuries, including weapons wielded by heroes of the **War of Shattered Skies**. The gallery is open to visiting dignitaries by appointment with **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s court.

The **Deep Vault** stores rare materials including **mithril ore**, **adamantine ingots**, and **dragon bone** awaiting transformation into legendary items. Access requires dual authorization from both **King Thorin** and **General Ironfist**, with additional runic wards designed by **Grimjaw Ironhand** himself.

## Rulers and Leaders

The Armory operates under the direct authority of **King Thorin Stoneforge**, who holds ultimate responsibility for all weapons production within dwarven territories. Day-to-day operations are managed by **Guildmaster Thrain Runeforge**, who coordinates between the **Ironhand Guild**, the royal court, and foreign customers.

**High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** blesses each batch of weapons before delivery, invoking **Moradin**'s favor upon the craftsmanship. This religious endorsement adds significant value, as warriors across **Aethelgard** believe that **Moradin**-blessed weapons strike truer and endure longer.

**General Ironfist** maintains strategic oversight of Armory production, ensuring that military requirements receive priority during times of conflict while peacetime production serves commercial interests. His relationship with **Guildmaster Runeforge** is collegial but professional, with both recognizing the importance of balancing quality against quantity.

## Connected Events

- **War of Shattered Skies** (250-450 AS): Armory operated continuously, producing weapons for the coalition
- **Treaty of Silverhaven** (450 AS): Armory transitioned to peacetime production, opening new trade markets
- **Stormpeak Earthquake** (425 AS): Forges briefly extinguished, considered a terrible omen; quickly relit
- **Grimjaw Ironhand's Innovation** (345 AS): Runemagic techniques transformed Armory production forever
- **Dragonborn Alliance** (460 AS): **Lady Zara Goldscale** established regular weapons shipments to **Drakkenheim**
