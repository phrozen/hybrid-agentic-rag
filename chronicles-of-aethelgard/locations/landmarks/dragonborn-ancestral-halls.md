# The Dragonborn Ancestral Halls

## Overview

The Dragonborn Ancestral Halls are **Drakkenheim**'s sacred repository of **Dragonborn** clan histories, genealogies, and artifacts, where **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s lineage traces back to **Lord Arkhan the Cruel**. The Halls house ceremonial armor, weapons, and eggs from ancient dragons, with **Bahamut**'s clergy maintaining the site. **Dragonborn Clans** hold induction ceremonies in the Halls, and visiting dignitaries tour the facility to understand **Dragonborn** culture.

## Geography and Climate

The Dragonborn Ancestral Halls occupy a vast underground complex beneath **Drakkenheim**'s central plateau, carved from the volcanic basalt that underlies the city. The entrance is a monumental archway of carved dragon bone framing a descending staircase wide enough for six **Dragonborn** to walk abreast. The tunnels below extend for miles, branching into clan-specific wings, ceremonial chambers, and climate-controlled vaults where the most fragile artifacts are preserved. The ambient temperature in the deeper halls is warm and dry, maintained by geothermal heat from **Drakkenheim**'s volcanic setting, which the **Dragonborn** consider a blessing from the ancestral dragons whose bones form the mountains.

The underground architecture reflects **Dragonborn** values of permanence and strength. Walls are decorated with bas-relief carvings depicting clan histories — battles won, treaties signed, dragons honored, and children hatched. The floors are polished obsidian mined from the same volcanic deposits, and the ceilings soar to heights that accommodate **Dragonborn** processional banners and the mounted skeletons of honored dragon ancestors. Natural hot springs deep within the complex provide both warmth and water for the ritual baths that precede all major ceremonies.

The Halls' deepest level, accessible only to the **High Priest of Bahamut** and **Lady Zara Goldscale** herself, contains the Vault of Eggs, where fossilized dragon eggs from the age before **the Sundering** are kept in stasis chambers maintained by **Bahamut**'s clergy. The eggs do not hatch — they have been stone for millennia — but they radiate a faint warmth that the clergy interpret as the ancestors' continuing presence. The climate in this chamber is precisely controlled by enchantments that have functioned for centuries without interruption, a testament to the **Dragonborn** commitment to preservation.

## History and Founding

The Ancestral Halls' construction began in 150 AS, when the **Dragonborn** clans first settled **Drakkenheim** and recognized the need for a central repository for their scattered histories and artifacts. Before the Halls, each clan maintained its own records in whateverConditions its territory provided — caves, volcanic vents, or hastily constructed stone vaults — and the constant threat of loss from natural disasters, raids, and simple decay meant that **Dragonborn** history was fragmentary at best. **Lord Arkhan the Cruel**, whose name survives in infamy despite his contributions to **Dragonborn** unification, ordered the construction of a permanent archive that would belong to all clans collectively.

The initial construction took forty years and required the labor of every clan, a feat of cooperation that the **Dragonborn** still cite as proof that their people can achieve greatness when united. The underground site was chosen because the volcanic basalt provided natural protection from fire, flood, and theft, and because the geothermal heat eliminated the need for artificial climate control in the artifact vaults. **Bahamut**'s clergy, who had maintained oral genealogies since before **the Sundering**, were appointed as the Halls' custodians, a role they have fulfilled continuously for three centuries.

The Halls have been expanded six times, each expansion coinciding with a significant historical event. The most recent expansion, completed in 448 AS, added a wing dedicated to the **War of Shattered Skies**, where the names of **Dragonborn** who fell in the conflict are inscribed on obsidian tablets alongside their clan crests. **Lady Zara Goldscale** personally oversaw this expansion, insisting that the war's cost be permanently recorded even as **Drakkenheim** rebuilt. The Halls' expansion history is itself recorded in the **Dragonborn** genealogical tradition, with each construction phase assigned to a specific clan that still maintains that wing today.

## Population and Culture

The Ancestral Halls employ approximately one hundred and fifty permanent staff, divided among curatorial positions held by **Bahamut**'s clergy, custodial roles filled by clan representatives, and security personnel drawn from **Drakkenheim**'s military. The Halls receive roughly five hundred visitors daily, a mix of **Dragonborn** pilgrims, foreign dignitaries, and scholars given special access to research specific artifacts or records. During clan induction ceremonies, attendance swells to several thousand as extended families gather to witness the next generation's formal inclusion in the clan rolls.

**Dragonborn** culture treats the Halls as the physical embodiment of their collective identity. A **Dragonborn** who has not visited the Halls is considered incomplete, lacking the connection to ancestry that defines full personhood in **Dragonborn** tradition. Clan induction ceremonies — held monthly for new hatchlings and annually for adopted adults — are solemn occasions where the inductee's name is carved into their clan's genealogical wall, linking them to every ancestor who preceded them back to **the Sundering** and beyond.

The Halls' atmosphere is one of reverent quiet rather than gloom. The warm stone and soft lighting create a space that feels protective rather than oppressive, and the **Dragonborn** tradition of speaking softly in the Halls ensures that visitors can contemplate the weight of history without distraction. **Bahamut**'s clergy lead daily prayers in the central chapel, and visitors of all faiths are welcome to attend, though few non-**Dragonborn** understand the liturgical language in which the prayers are conducted.

## Economy and Trade

The Ancestral Halls are not a commercial enterprise, but their impact on **Drakkenheim**'s economy is substantial. Pilgrim traffic generates revenue for inns, restaurants, and transport services throughout the city, and the requirement that visiting dignitaries present themselves at the Halls before conducting official business creates a steady stream of high-spending visitors who must be accommodated. **Lady Zara Goldscale** has leveraged this requirement to ensure that diplomatic visitors experience **Dragonborn** hospitality at its most impressive, setting a favorable tone for subsequent negotiations.

The Halls' maintenance budget is funded by a combination of clan contributions, **Bahamut**'s temple revenues, and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s personal endowment. The endowment is substantial enough to fund both routine operations and a perpetual conservation program that repairs deteriorating artifacts using traditional **Dragonborn** craftsmanship techniques. The **Gnomish Engineers Guild** has offered to install modern climate control systems in the deeper vaults, but the **High Priest of Bahamut** has declined, noting that the Halls' existing enchantments have functioned for three centuries without failure, a reliability record that no **Gnomish** device can match.

The Halls also generate indirect economic value through their role in cultural diplomacy. **Lady Zara Goldscale** uses guided tours of the facility to educate foreign visitors about **Dragonborn** history and values, creating goodwill that translates into trade agreements and military alliances. The **Dragonborn Clans** consider this cultural outreach essential to their long-term security, arguing that understanding prevents conflict more effectively than fortifications.

## Notable Features

### The Genealogical Walls

Every clan's genealogical record, inscribed floor-to-ceiling in **Dragonborn** script on obsidian panels, traces each clan's lineage from **the Sundering** to the present day. The walls are updated monthly with new births, deaths, and adoptions, and clan representatives verify each entry before it is carved. **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s personal lineage runs seventeen generations deep on these walls, connecting her directly to **Lord Arkhan the Cruel** and to the drake-riders who defended **Drakkenheim**'s founding settlement.

### The Vault of Eggs

The deepest and most sacred chamber in the Halls, containing fossilized dragon eggs preserved in stasis enchantments maintained by **Bahamut**'s clergy. The eggs range from fist-sized to barrel-sized, representing multiple dragon species that once ruled Aethelgard's skies. Visitors are permitted to view the vault from a raised gallery but may not approach the eggs, which the clergy considers still alive in a spiritual sense despite their mineralization. The eggs' faint warmth has been documented by **Arcane College of Thaldor** researchers, who theorize that residual life energy persists within the shells.

### The War of Shattered Skies Memorial Wing

The newest addition to the Halls, this wing records every **Dragonborn** who fell in the war, organized by clan and accompanied by personal effects donated by the bereaved families. A central monument of black obsidian bears a single inscription in **Dragonborn** script: "We remember. We honor. We rebuild." **Lady Zara Goldscale** visits this wing privately on the war's anniversary each year, and public ceremonies here draw **Dragonborn** from across Aethelgard.

## Rulers and Leaders

### High Priest Baelstrun Ironscale

The senior clergyman of **Bahamut** in **Drakkenheim** and the Ancestral Halls' chief curator, **High Priest Ironscale** is a massive **Dragonborn** whose quiet demeanor belies an encyclopedic knowledge of **Dragonborn** history and a fierce determination to preserve it. He reports to **Lady Zara Goldscale** on administrative matters but considers himself answerable only to **Bahamut** on questions of spiritual significance, a distinction that has created occasional tension but never open conflict.

### Curator Drakara Ashenclaw

The Halls' chief conservator, **Drakara** is responsible for the physical preservation of artifacts, records, and structural elements. A trained historian who studied at **Silverhaven University** before returning to **Drakkenheim**, she brings modern conservation techniques to traditional **Dragonborn** craftsmanship, combining the two approaches in ways that have significantly extended the lifespan of fragile items. She works closely with the **Gnomish Engineers Guild** on specific technical challenges while maintaining the Halls' overall traditional character.

## Connected Events

- **The Sundering**: The Halls preserve **Dragonborn** genealogical records that predate **the Sundering**, making them one of the few institutions in Aethelgard with continuous documentation of the world before the catastrophe, and the **Arcane College of Thaldor** has sought access to these records for decades
- **War of Shattered Skies**: The war's impact on **Dragonborn** society is documented in the Memorial Wing, and the expansion that created this wing was **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s personal response to the loss her people suffered, ensuring that future generations would understand the war's cost
- **Lord Arkhan the Cruel's Legacy**: The Halls' founding by **Lord Arkhan** represents the complex **Dragonborn** relationship with their history — a figure whose cruelty is undeniable but whose vision for collective preservation made the Halls possible
