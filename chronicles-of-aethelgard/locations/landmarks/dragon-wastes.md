# The Dragon Wastes

## Overview

The Dragon Wastes are a desolate region in the far north of Aethelgard, beyond the **Northern Wastes**, where ancient dragons fell during **the Sundering**. The land is scarred by draconic magic, littered with fossilized remains, and inhabited by dangerous creatures adapted to the harsh environment. **Dragonborn** pilgrims journey here to honor their ancestors, while **Arcane College of Thaldor** researchers study the lingering magical radiation. The Wastes remain one of Aethelgard's most dangerous and least explored regions.

## Geography and Climate

The Dragon Wastes stretch across approximately ten thousand square miles of the far northern reaches of Aethelgard, a landscape so thoroughly transformed by draconic magic that its original topography is irrecoverable. The terrain is a nightmarescape of fused glass, crystalline formations, and dragonglass obsidian spires that erupted from the earth when ancient dragons perished in catastrophic magical discharges during **the Sundering**. Between these formations stretch vast flats of sterile ash and cracked stone, devoid of vegetation and swept by winds that carry abrasive particles capable of stripping exposed skin in hours.

The climate is extreme even by the standards of the **Northern Wastes** to the south. Temperatures fluctuate wildly, from searing heat during the long summer days when draconic magic amplifies solar radiation, to bitter cold during the extended winter nights when no magical energy warms the blasted landscape. The aurora that illuminates the **Northern Wastes** takes on disturbing hues over the Dragon Wastes, shifting through greens and reds that the **Dragonborn** interpret as the spirits of fallen dragons. Rain, when it falls, is often acidic, contaminated by residual magical energy that etches stone and kills any living thing it touches.

Water sources are scarce and frequently toxic. A few rivers cross the Wastes' southern edge, their waters tinged with minerals leached from the magical contamination, and thermal vents in the deeper Wastes produce steam that condenses into pools of varying safety. **Dragonborn** pilgrims learn which water sources are potable from oral tradition passed down through generations, and the **Arcane College of Thaldor** has published a water safety map that supplements but does not replace this indigenous knowledge. Navigation is difficult because the landscape shifts subtly as residual magical energy causes rock formations to grow, shrink, or relocate over periods of months.

The Wastes' most striking feature is the Dragonfall Field, a stretch of several hundred acres where the skeletal remains of ancient dragons lie in positions suggesting they fell from the sky simultaneously during **the Sundering**. The bones have fossilized into stone over the millennia, but their scale remains awe-inspiring — a single rib can span fifty feet, and the skulls of the largest dragons could house a village. The **Dragonborn** consider this field the holiest site in Aethelgard and conduct annual pilgrimages to honor their ancestors' sacrifice.

## History and Founding

The Dragon Wastes were not founded but created, formed in a single catastrophic moment during **the Sundering** when the ancient dragons who ruled Aethelgard's skies perished in a conflagration of magical energy so intense that it permanently scarred the land. **Dragonborn** oral tradition preserves accounts of the event as a battle between the dragons and forces of cosmic destruction, while the **Arcane College of Thaldor** theorizes that the dragons' own magical energies destabilized and triggered a chain reaction. Regardless of the cause, the result was the obliteration of whatever ecosystem previously occupied the region and its replacement by the magical wasteland that exists today.

For centuries after **the Sundering**, the Dragon Wastes were considered uninhabitable and were avoided by all peoples. The first **Dragonborn** pilgrimages began around 200 AS, when **Dragonborn** clan leaders seeking to reconnect with their ancestral heritage began making dangerous journeys into the Wastes to locate the fallen dragons' remains. These early pilgrims established the routes and water sources that later expeditions would follow, and their experiences formed the basis of the oral tradition that still guides travelers through the Wastes today.

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** established its first research station in the Wastes in 380 AS, when **Throndor the Wise** personally sponsored an expedition to study the magical radiation that permeates the region. The initial station was destroyed by a magical surge that killed three researchers, and subsequent stations have been built with progressively more robust protections. The current station, erected in 440 AS and reinforced after the **War of Shattered Skies**, uses **Dwarven** construction techniques and **Gnomish** energy-shielding to protect its occupants from the Wastes' hazards.

**Lady Zara Goldscale** has taken a personal interest in the Wastes, making three pilgrimages since assuming leadership of **Drakkenheim** and funding improved trail markers and emergency shelters along the pilgrimage routes. Her visits are significant events in **Dragonborn** culture, combining religious observance with political symbolism — the leader of the **Dragonborn** people paying homage to the ancestors whose sacrifice made their existence possible.

## Population and Culture

The Dragon Wastes have no permanent population. The region is too hostile for settlement, and even the **Arcane College**'s research station is staffed on a rotating basis rather than permanently occupied. Seasonal visitors include **Dragonborn** pilgrims during the summer months, **Arcane College** researchers during spring and fall, and a handful of extreme prospectors who search the Wastes for dragonglass and other rare materials despite the considerable danger.

**Dragonborn** pilgrimage culture is the Wastes' primary cultural framework. Pilgrims travel in groups of ten to twenty, following established routes that pass the major dragonfall sites and conclude at the Dragonfall Field, where ritual observances honor the ancestors. The pilgrimage takes approximately three weeks and requires significant physical endurance, as the route crosses terrain that punishes weakness. **Dragonborn** tradition holds that only those who complete the full pilgrimage on foot, without magical assistance, earn the right to call themselves true descendants of the dragons.

The **Arcane College** researchers who study the Wastes maintain a separate but respectful culture of their own. Expeditions are planned with **Dragonborn** input, and researchers avoid disturbing sites that the **Dragonborn** consider sacred. This accommodation has not always been smooth — early expeditions were criticized for collecting bone samples without permission, and the current protocol requires **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s written approval before any material can be removed from the Wastes. The **College** considers the Wastes the most important magical research site in Aethelgard, as the residual draconic energy provides insights into pre-**Sundering** magic that cannot be obtained anywhere else.

The creatures that inhabit the Wastes — mutated scavengers, magical constructs, and entities that defy classification — are treated with caution by both pilgrims and researchers. The **Dragonborn** consider the Wastes' creatures to be corrupted echoes of the dragons' servants, deserving of mercy when possible and destruction when necessary. The **Arcane College** researchers view them as data points in the ongoing study of magical mutation, cataloging species and behaviors with academic rigor while maintaining the weaponry needed to survive encounters.

## Economy and Trade

The Dragon Wastes generate no conventional economic output, but the rare materials that prospectors occasionally extract from the Wastes' fringe command extraordinary prices. Dragonglass — the fused obsidian that forms near dragonfall sites — is valued for its magical conductivity and sells for ten times its weight in gold in **Thaldor**'s markets. Fossilized dragon bone, when legally obtained with **Dragonborn** permission, is used in the finest **Dwarven** craftwork and **Gnomish** engineering applications. The **Dragonborn Ancestral Halls** in **Drakkenheim** strictly regulate the export of these materials, and unauthorized removal is punishable by exile.

The **Arcane College of Thaldor** funds its research station through its institutional budget, considering the Wastes a long-term investment in magical understanding that will eventually yield practical applications. **Throndor the Wise** believed that the Wastes' magical radiation contained the key to understanding pre-**Sundering** energy systems, and while that understanding remains elusive, the research has produced dozens of published papers and several minor magical innovations. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** continues the funding, arguing that the only thing more expensive than studying the Wastes is failing to understand them.

The pilgrimage economy is modest but meaningful for **Drakkenheim**. Outfitters, guides, and rest stop operators along the approach routes earn their annual income during the summer pilgrimage season, and the **Dragonborn** tradition of purchasing ceremonial items in **Drakkenheim** before beginning the trek supports a small but stable craft industry. **Lady Zara Goldscale** has considered establishing a pilgrim hostel at the Wastes' southern edge, but environmental concerns and the inherent danger of encouraging more traffic into the region have so far prevented construction.

## Notable Features

### The Dragonfall Field

The holiest site in **Dragonborn** tradition, where the fossilized skeletons of ancient dragons lie where they fell during **the Sundering**. The field stretches for miles, its bones illuminated by the strange aurora that haunts the Wastes' sky. **Dragonborn** pilgrims spend three days in ritual observance here, fasting and meditating beside the bones of specific ancestors as identified by clan tradition. The field's magical energy is palpable, causing unshielded visitors to experience vivid visions and emotional intensification that the **Arcane College** attributes to residual draconic consciousness.

### The Arcane Research Station

The **Arcane College of Thaldor**'s outpost in the Wastes' southern fringe, built from reinforced **Dwarven** stone and shielded by **Gnomish** energy dampeners. The station houses up to twenty researchers during active expeditions and contains the most comprehensive collection of pre-**Sundering** magical samples in Aethelgard. Its library includes **Throndor the Wise**'s personal notes from his 380 AS expedition, which remain the baseline for all subsequent research.

### The Crystal Scars

Linear formations of crystallized magical energy that crisscross the Wastes like giant claw marks, remnants of the dragonfire discharges that created the region. The crystals pulse with residual energy and are extremely dangerous to handle without protective equipment, but they contain information about pre-**Sundering** magical systems that the **Arcane College** considers invaluable. **Dragonborn** tradition holds that the scars mark the paths of the last dragonflights, and walking a scar from end to end is considered a meditative practice.

## Rulers and Leaders

### High Priest Baelstrun Ironscale

The **Dragonborn Ancestral Halls**' chief curator also serves as the spiritual authority for the Wastes' pilgrimage routes, maintaining the trail markers and emergency shelters that keep pilgrims alive. **High Priest Ironscale** works closely with **Lady Zara Goldscale** on all Wastes-related policy and has the authority to close pilgrimage routes when conditions become too dangerous, a power he exercises without hesitation.

### Professor Valdris Ashborne

The **Arcane College**'s lead researcher in the Dragon Wastes and the director of the research station, **Professor Ashborne** has spent fifteen seasons studying the Wastes' magical radiation. A meticulous **Human** mage who lost two colleagues to a magical surge early in her career, she enforces safety protocols with near-obsessive rigor and has refused expeditions that she considered inadequately prepared, regardless of the researchers' credentials or institutional backing.

## Connected Events

- **The Sundering**: The Dragon Wastes were created by the dragons' destruction during this catastrophe, and every feature of the landscape — from the Dragonfall Field to the Crystal Scars — is a direct legacy of the event that reshaped Aethelgard
- **Throndor the Wise's Expedition**: The **Arcane College**'s first research expedition in 380 AS established the scientific framework for studying the Wastes and created the safety protocols that, updated over the centuries, still govern research in the region
- **Lady Zara Goldscale's Pilgrimages**: The **Dragonborn** leader's three pilgrimages to the Wastes have reinforced the connection between **Drakkenheim**'s political leadership and the ancestral heritage that the Wastes represent, and her funding of improved infrastructure has made the pilgrimage safer for ordinary **Dragonborn**
