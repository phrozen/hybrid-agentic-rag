# The Eastern Plains

## Overview

The Eastern Plains are a vast grassland region occupying the central **Eastern Kingdoms**, serving as the agricultural heartland that feeds **Silverhaven** and other major cities. The plains are home to **Halfling** farming communities, human ranches, and the legendary **Poppy Greenfields** whose agricultural innovations transformed the region's productivity. The annual harvest from the Eastern Plains is critical to the kingdom's stability, and **Queen Elara** maintains grain reserves in **Silverhaven** to prevent famine during poor harvest years.

## Geography and Climate

The Eastern Plains stretch across approximately three hundred thousand square miles of gently rolling grasslands, bounded by the **Stormpeak Mountains** to the north, the **River Aethel** to the west, the **Southern Expanse** highlands to the south, and the coastal hills of the eastern seaboard. The terrain is predominantly flat to gently undulating, with occasional river valleys carving shallow channels through the prairie. The richest agricultural land follows **River Aethel**'s tributaries—the Greenfield Creek, the Barleywater, and the Goldwash River—where centuries of alluvial deposition have produced soil deep enough to sustain intensive farming.

The climate is temperate continental, with four distinct seasons that govern the agricultural calendar. Spring brings warming temperatures and reliable rainfall that germinates the winter grain crops planted the previous autumn. Summer is hot and humid, with occasional drought periods that **Poppy Greenfields**'s irrigation innovations have helped mitigate. Autumn is harvest season, when the entire plains population mobilizes to bring in the grain, vegetables, and animal products that feed the **Eastern Kingdoms** through winter. Winter is cold but rarely severe, with snow cover that protects dormant crops and replenishes soil moisture for the following spring.

The plains' grassland ecology supports enormous herds of wild bison and elk that the plains' ranchers supplement with domesticated cattle, sheep, and goats. **Halfling** farmers have developed crop rotations that maintain soil fertility without magical assistance, a practical achievement that **Queen Elara**'s agricultural advisors study with considerable respect. The **Halfling** settlement pattern—clustered villages surrounded by individual farmsteads—creates a patchwork of cultivated land and wild prairie that supports both agricultural output and the hunting traditions of the plains' **human** ranchers.

## History and Founding

The Eastern Plains have been agricultural territory since the earliest **human** settlement of the **Eastern Kingdoms**. **Halfling** communities arrived during the initial migrations and established the first permanent farming villages along the **River Aethel** tributaries, bringing sophisticated crop management techniques that transformed the plains from wild prairie to productive farmland. **Human** ranchers followed, establishing cattle operations on the drier upland prairies where crops struggled but livestock thrived.

The plains' modern agricultural system owes its existence to **Poppy Greenfields**, whose innovations in the mid-400s AS revolutionized regional farming. Before Greenfields, the plains operated on a simple two-field rotation that left half the arable land fallow each year. Greenfields introduced a four-field rotation incorporating clover and root vegetables that restored nitrogen to depleted soil while producing additional food crops, effectively doubling the plains' productive capacity. **Queen Elara** recognized the strategic implications immediately and commissioned Greenfields to teach the system to every farming community in the **Eastern Kingdoms**, an effort that prevented the famine that the **War of Shattered Skies** would otherwise have caused.

The **War of Shattered Skies** devastated the Eastern Plains despite their distance from the front lines. Conscription emptied the farms of workers, military requisition consumed stored grain reserves, and several battles that spilled onto plains territory destroyed crops and irrigation infrastructure. **Poppy Greenfields** organized halfling supply networks during the **Siege of Silverhaven**, maintaining food shipments to the besieged capital while managing the agricultural crisis at home. The post-war recovery was rapid—Greenfields' four-field system enabled surplus production within three growing seasons, generating the grain reserves that **Queen Elara** stores in **Silverhaven** against future scarcity.

## Population and Culture

The Eastern Plains support approximately two million residents, predominantly **humans** (sixty percent) and **Halflings** (thirty percent), with **Catfolk** traders (five percent), **dwarven** agricultural tool manufacturers (three percent), and other species comprising the remainder. The population is dispersed across hundreds of farming villages and ranching outposts, with only a handful of settlements large enough to be called towns. The largest is Greenfield Crossing, a **Halfling** market town at the confluence of the Greenfield Creek and **River Aethel** that serves as the regional trading hub and the administrative center for **Queen Elara**'s grain reserve system.

**Halfling** farming communities on the plains maintain their traditional social organization: extended families living in interconnected burrow-homes clustered around communal green spaces, with individual families holding specific field allotments that pass through generations. **Poppy Greenfields** elevated this tradition to national prominence through her leadership in **Silverhaven**'s Littlehaven district, but the plains **Halfling** communities have practiced communal agriculture for centuries. Their crop rotation practices, seed selection techniques, and food preservation methods represent the most sophisticated non-magical agricultural system in Aethelgard.

**Human** ranchers operate on a different model: large landholdings managed by extended families, with cattle, sheep, and horse herds roaming the upland prairies under mounted oversight. The ranching culture values self-sufficiency, horsemanship, and the ability to manage livestock across vast distances without regular access to town infrastructure. Annual cattle drives converge on Greenfield Crossing's market week, when ranchers sell their surplus animals and purchase supplies for the following year. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** traders attend every market week, purchasing hides, wool, and preserved meat for distribution through the **Eastern Kingdoms** commercial network.

## Economy and Trade

The Eastern Plains' economy is overwhelmingly agricultural, producing the grain, vegetables, livestock, and animal products that feed the **Eastern Kingdoms** and supply export markets in the **Southern Expanse** and **Western Reaches**. The plains' output travels via **River Aethel** barges to **Silverhaven**, where **Queen Elara**'s grain reserve system purchases a mandated fraction of each harvest for strategic storage. The remaining surplus enters commercial distribution through **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** trading network and independent merchant houses operating from **Thaldor** and **Silverhaven**.

Primary crops include wheat, barley, oats, rye, root vegetables, and the clover that underpins **Poppy Greenfields**'s rotation system. The plains also produce significant quantities of wool from hill-sheep herds, leather from cattle ranching, and dairy products from both **Halfling** goat herds and **human** cattle operations. **Halfling** food preservation techniques—smoking, pickling, hard cheese aging, and underground cold storage—extend the marketable lifespan of perishable goods, allowing plains products to reach distant markets in acceptable condition.

Secondary industries include **dwarven**-operated agricultural implement manufacturing, watermill-powered grain processing along the **River Aethel** tributaries, and a growing horse-breeding industry that supplies cavalry mounts to **Queen Elara**'s military and riding animals to **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** caravan operators. The plains' strategic importance as the **Eastern Kingdoms** food supply makes them a priority for **General Ironfist**'s border defense planning—protecting the harvest from disruption is as important as protecting **Silverhaven** from military assault.

## Notable Features

Greenfield Crossing is the plains' primary settlement, a town of approximately eight thousand situated at the river junction that gives it its name. The town's **Halfling**-designed architecture features low, wide buildings connected by covered walkways, with underground storage vaults that maintain constant temperature for grain and food preservation. The weekly market fills the central green with traders from across the plains, and the annual Harvest Festival draws visitors from **Silverhaven** and **Thaldor**. **Poppy Greenfields** maintains a residence in Greenfield Crossing, though her duties as Mayor of Littlehaven keep her in **Silverhaven** much of the year.

The **Eastern Plains Granaries** are the most strategically important infrastructure on the plains, a network of massive underground storage facilities built to **Queen Elara**'s specifications after the **War of Shattered Skies**. Each granary holds sufficient grain to feed its surrounding district for two years, and the system as a whole maintains reserves sufficient to prevent famine across the **Eastern Kingdoms** for eighteen months. The granaries are protected by **General Ironfist**'s garrisons and managed by an agricultural ministry that reports directly to **Queen Elara**'s court in **Silverhaven**.

The **Halfling** Agricultural Academy operates from a campus near Greenfield Crossing, training farmers in **Poppy Greenfields**'s rotation methods, seed selection, water management, and food preservation. The Academy accepts students of all species, though **Halfling** methods are adapted to **human** and **dwarven** physiologies through collaborative research. **Archchancellor Marcus Thornfield** has established exchange programs between the Academy and the **Arcane College of Thaldor**, exploring magical augmentation of **Halfling** agricultural techniques without replacing the practical foundation that makes them effective.

## Rulers and Leaders

The Eastern Plains fall under **Queen Elara**'s direct governance as part of the **Eastern Kingdoms**, administered through a network of regional governors who report to the royal court in **Silverhaven**. However, day-to-day governance is intensely local: **Halfling** villages operate through communal councils, **human** ranches through family patriarchs and matriarchs, and market towns through elected mayors who coordinate with the grain reserve administration.

**Poppy Greenfields** exercises indirect but substantial influence over plains agriculture through her Academy's training programs, her advisory relationship with **Queen Elara**, and her personal connections to the **Halfling** farming communities that produce the majority of the plains' food. Though her formal title is Mayor of Littlehaven, her reputation as the plains' most effective agricultural innovator gives her authority that transcends political boundaries. When crop blight threatened the 462 harvest, **Queen Elara** dispatched Greenfields personally to coordinate the response, a measure of the trust placed in her judgment.

**General Ironfist** maintains military garrisons at strategic points across the plains, tasked with protecting the agricultural infrastructure that **Queen Elara** considers essential to national security. The garrisons are small—typically one hundred soldiers per installation—but they patrol regularly, discourage banditry, and provide emergency response during natural disasters. The soldiers supplement their rations with local purchases, creating a modest but reliable additional market for plains farmers.
