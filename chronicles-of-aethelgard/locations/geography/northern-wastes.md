# The Northern Wastes

## Overview

The Northern Wastes are a frozen tundra region in far northern Aethelgard, characterized by permafrost, brutal winters, and sparse barbarian tribes who have adapted to the extreme conditions. The Wastes serve as a natural buffer between civilized lands and the unknown dangers of the far north, with **Eastern Kingdoms** maintaining minimal military outposts along the southern border. Barbarian tribes occasionally raid southward, but the harsh climate makes large-scale invasion impractical, leaving the Wastes as a mysterious frontier.

## Geography and Climate

The Northern Wastes span 500,000 square miles of frozen tundra, ice fields, and barren rock. The region lies north of the **Stormpeak Mountains** and **Eastern Plains**, extending to the edge of mapped Aethelgard. Temperatures rarely rise above freezing, even in summer, and winter brings months of darkness.

### Geographic Features

**Permafrost Plains**: Frozen tundra covering 70% of the Wastes. Only hardy mosses and lichens grow here. **Human** barbarian tribes follow migratory routes across the plains.

**Ice Fields**: Permanent glaciers in northern regions, some miles thick. Explorers report ancient ruins buried in the ice, predating **the Sundering**.

**Mountain Ranges**: Northern extensions of the **Stormpeak Mountains**, creating natural barriers. **Dwarven** scouts from **Stonehold** map these regions occasionally.

**Frozen Coast**: Arctic shoreline where the Wastes meet the northern sea. **Minotaur** naval patrols under **Admiral Hornbreaker** monitor southern waters but avoid the frozen coast.

### Climate Patterns

**Winter (8 months)**: Temperatures reach -60°F, with constant darkness north of the Arctic Circle. Blizzards can last for weeks, burying settlements in snow.

**Summer (4 months)**: Temperatures rise to 40-50°F, with constant daylight. Brief growing season allows limited agriculture and hunting.

**Storms**: Arctic storms sweep south from the unknown north, bringing deadly cold and snow. **Queen Elara**'s meteorologists study these storms to predict southern weather.

## History and Exploration

The Northern Wastes have remained largely unexplored throughout Aethelgard's history. Barbarian tribes have inhabited the region for millennia, but civilized powers maintain minimal presence.

### Ancient Period (Pre-100 AS)

**Barbarian Settlement**: **Human** tribes migrated to the Wastes, adapting to extreme conditions. Their oral histories speak of ancient civilizations buried in ice.

**Dwarven Expeditions**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s ancestors sent mining expeditions north. Most returned with tales of monsters and frozen death.

**Elven Withdrawal**: **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**'s predecessors determined the Wastes held no value worth the cost of colonization.

### Modern Era (100-450 AS)

**Eastern Kingdoms Outposts**: **Queen Elara** maintains three military outposts along the southern border, monitoring barbarian movements.

**Arcane College Research**: **Throndor the Wise**'s successors study arctic magic and planar connections. The Wastes' extreme conditions create magical anomalies.

**Barbarian Raids**: Periodic southern raids by barbarian tribes. **General Ironfist** coordinates border defense from **Thunder Pass**.

## Population and Culture

The Northern Wastes house approximately 50,000 inhabitants, predominantly **Human** barbarian tribes (80%), with scattered **Dwarven** mining outposts (10%), **Lycanthrope** packs (5%), and other arctic dwellers (5%).

### Barbarian Tribes

**Tribal Structure**: Barbarians organize into clans led by chieftains chosen through combat or spiritual leadership. Clans war with each other but unite against southern invaders.

**Survival Culture**: Barbarians value strength, endurance, and hunting skill above all. Weak children are abandoned; strong warriors earn respect regardless of birth.

**Spiritual Traditions**: Barbarian shamans worship spirits of ice, wind, and beasts. **Order of the Silver Dawn** missionaries have made no converts in the Wastes.

### Arctic Lycanthropes

**Werebear Packs**: **Lycanthrope** clans in the Wastes predominantly take werebear forms. Their packs maintain tenuous alliances with barbarian tribes.

**Winter Wolves**: Rare **lycanthrope** variants unique to the Wastes, able to control ice and cold. **Arcane College** researchers study these creatures.

## Economy and Resources

The Northern Wastes' economy is subsistence-based, with limited trade in exotic arctic resources:

### Hunting and Gathering

**Arctic Game**: Barbarians hunt caribou, arctic foxes, and woolly mammoths. Meat provides food; hides provide clothing and shelter.

**Fishing**: Frozen lakes yield fish during summer months. Winter fishing requires shamanic ice magic.

**Gathering**: Summer berries, roots, and lichens supplement diets. **Halfling** agricultural advisors have introduced hardy crop varieties with limited success.

### Trade Goods

**Fur and Hides**: Barbarian furs traded to **Eastern Kingdoms** merchants for metal tools and weapons. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s companies profit from arctic trade.

**Ivory**: Mammoth and walrus ivory carved into art and tools. **Dragonborn** craftsmen from **Drakkenheim** prize arctic ivory for scale-mail decorations.

**Rare Minerals**: **Dwarven** outposts mine arctic iron and ice-resistant metals. **King Thorin Stoneforge** values these materials for specialized applications.

## Notable Features

### The Wall of Ice

Massive glacier at the northern edge of mapped territory. Barbarian legends speak of civilizations beyond the Wall, but no explorer has returned from crossing it. **Arcane College** expeditions have attempted magical reconnaissance with inconclusive results.

### Barbarian Strongholds

Permanent tribal settlements in southern Wastes, built from stone and ice. These strongholds serve as rallying points during raids and trading posts during summer. **Queen Elara**'s outposts monitor stronghold movements.

### Ancient Ruins

Buried ruins from pre-**Sundering** civilizations protrude from ice fields. **Arcane College of Thaldor** researchers excavate these sites during summer, recovering artifacts of unknown origin and power.

### Shamanic Sites

Sacred locations where barbarian shamans perform rituals. The largest site, the Circle of Frozen Souls, features standing stones arranged in magical patterns. **High Priest Thrain Sacredforge** studies these stones for runemagic connections.

## Governance and Politics

### Tribal Confederations

Barbarian tribes occasionally unite under powerful chieftains for raids or defense. Current confederation leader is **Chieftain Frostbeard**, who united twelve tribes in 445 AS. **Queen Elara**'s diplomats attempted negotiations with limited success.

### Eastern Kingdoms Outposts

**Queen Elara** maintains three military outposts along the southern border:
- **Northwatch Keep**: Primary fortress, 1,000 troops under **General Ironfist**'s command
- **Frostborder Station**: Intelligence gathering, monitors barbarian movements
- **Icemark Garrison**: Trade regulation, collects tariffs from barbarian merchants

### Dwarven Mining Claims

**King Thorin Stoneforge** grants mining rights to dwarven clans willing to brave arctic conditions. These claims operate independently of barbarian territories, with occasional conflicts.

## Connected Characters

- **Queen Elara**: Human ruler, maintains southern border outposts
- **King Thorin Stoneforge**: Dwarf king, grants arctic mining rights
- **General Ironfist**: Commands **Northwatch Keep** defense
- **Chieftain Frostbeard**: Barbarian confederation leader
- **Admiral Hornbreaker**: **Minotaur** naval commander, monitors arctic waters

## Connected Locations

- **Stormpeak Mountains**: Southern border of the Wastes
- **Eastern Kingdoms**: Human civilization south of the Wastes
- **Northwatch Keep**: **Queen Elara**'s primary arctic fortress
- **Stonehold**: **King Thorin Stoneforge**'s capital, source of mining expeditions
