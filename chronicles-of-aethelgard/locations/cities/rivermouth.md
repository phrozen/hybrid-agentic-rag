# Rivermouth

## Overview

Rivermouth is a multi-species trade port at the mouth of **River Aethel** in the **Southern Expanse**, where river barges from **Silverhaven** and **Eastern Kingdoms** transfer cargo to ocean-going vessels. The city's population includes **Lizardfolk**, **humans**, **Dragonborn**, and **Catfolk**, with **Trader Slickscale** among its most prominent merchants. **Mayor Ironscale** coordinates with Rivermouth's council on trade policy, and the **Crystal Bay Free Port** model influences Rivermouth's commercial regulations.

## Geography and Climate

Rivermouth sits at the precise point where **River Aethel** empties into the southern reaches of **Crystal Bay**, a geographic position that makes it the natural transfer point for all cargo moving between inland river traffic and oceanic shipping. The city occupies a broad delta where the river fans into three channels before meeting the sea, creating natural harbors on either side of the central island district. The eastern harbor handles river traffic—barges from **Silverhaven**, grain ships from the **Eastern Plains**, and passenger vessels from **Thaldor**—while the western harbor services ocean-going merchant ships carrying goods to and from **Crystal Bay**, **Drakkenheim**, and distant ports beyond the **Southern Expanse**.

The delta terrain is flat and marshy, requiring extensive engineering to support permanent construction. Rivermouth's builders—originally **Lizardfolk** swamp architects, later supplemented by **human** and **dwarven** engineers—developed a sophisticated pile-and-platform construction technique that anchors buildings to driven timber piles sunk deep into the delta silt. The central island, the only naturally solid ground in the delta, supports the council chambers and the wealthiest merchant houses. Outer districts float on anchored platforms that rise and fall with the tide, a design that **Trader Slickscale** imported from **Ssithik's Landing** and adapted for Rivermouth's saltwater conditions.

The climate is tropical and humid, moderated by constant sea breezes that make Rivermouth more comfortable than inland **Southern Expanse** cities during the wet season. Storm surges pose the primary environmental threat; **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s naval officers maintain a storm-warning system using signal towers along the coast, giving Rivermouth residents several hours' warning before major weather events. The **Lizardfolk** population navigates the delta's waterways with natural ease, while **human** and **Dragonborn** residents rely on the city's extensive canal network and pontoon bridges.

## History and Founding

Rivermouth's origins predate formal **Lizardfolk** Unification by several decades. A small **Lizardfolk** fishing village occupied the eastern delta bank long before **Chief Ssithik**'s consolidation, exploiting the river-sea junction for subsistence fishing and seasonal trade with **Lizardfolk** communities upriver. The village's strategic position attracted **human** and **Catfolk** traders as early as 400 AS, when merchants from **Silverhaven** began seeking direct access to **Southern Expanse** markets without the additional port fees charged at **Crystal Bay**.

The settlement's transformation from fishing village to trade port accelerated after the **Crystal Bay Free Port Declaration** in 440 AS. **Trader Slickscale** recognized that Rivermouth's position made it the ideal transfer point between river and ocean shipping, avoiding the overland caravan routes that added time and risk to inter-regional commerce. He invested heavily in dock construction, warehouse facilities, and guest lodgings, establishing the commercial infrastructure that **Trader Slickscale** still operates today. **Mayor Ironscale**'s predecessor, the **Lizardfolk** administrator Keth-saar, formally incorporated Rivermouth as a multi-species municipality in 442 AS, establishing the council system that governs the city.

The **Treaty of Silverhaven** in 450 AS transformed Rivermouth from a regional trading post into a hub of inter-continental commerce. **Queen Elara**'s recognition of **Lizardfolk** territorial claims included provisions guaranteeing Rivermouth's status as a free-trade zone, mirroring the **Crystal Bay** model. The city's population tripled within a decade as merchants, dockworkers, and supporting businesses rushed to capitalize on the new trade opportunities. **Trader Slickscale** expanded his operations to handle the volume increase, establishing permanent trade agreements with **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** network and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** merchants.

## Population and Culture

Rivermouth's population of approximately forty thousand makes it the third-largest city in the **Southern Expanse** after **Drakkenheim** and **Crystal Bay**. The demographic composition reflects its role as a transfer point: **Lizardfolk** (thirty percent) dominate the riverine districts and canal navigation services; **humans** (twenty-five percent) operate the administrative and financial institutions; **Dragonborn** (twenty percent) control the metalworking and shipbuilding trades; and **Catfolk** (fifteen percent) dominate the information-brokering and long-distance trade networks. The remaining ten percent includes **Halflings**, **dwarves**, and other species drawn by commercial opportunity.

This diversity creates a civic culture unlike any other in Aethelgard. Rivermouth is the only city where **Lizardfolk**, **human**, **Dragonborn**, and **Catfolk** council representatives hold equal voting power, a governance structure directly modeled on **Mayor Ironscale**'s **Crystal Bay** system. The city's official language is Common, but street conversations mix **Lizardfolk** tongue, **Dragonborn** commercial dialect, and **Catfolk** trade-argot in a creole that outsiders find bewildering but that Rivermouth natives navigate effortlessly. **Trader Slickscale** employs interpreters for every major language, making his trading house the default meeting point for cross-species negotiations.

Religious practice in Rivermouth reflects its multi-species population. **Bahamut**'s temple serves the **Dragonborn** community, **Order of the Silver Dawn** chapels cater to **human** and **Halfling** worshippers, and **Lizardfolk** spirit shrines dot the canal district. The city's most unusual religious structure is the Convergence House, founded by **Shaman Vessska** during a visit in 460 AS, where representatives of all faiths meet monthly to discuss moral and ethical questions arising from inter-species commerce. The Convergence House has no congregation and performs no rites—it exists purely as a space for dialogue, a concept that mystifies traditional believers of every faith but that Rivermouth's merchants find genuinely useful.

## Economy and Trade

Rivermouth's economy exists because of its geographic position at the **River Aethel**-**Crystal Bay** junction. Every cargo moving between inland **Eastern Kingdoms** markets and the **Southern Expanse** must pass through the city, either loading from river barge to ocean vessel or vice versa. This transfer necessarily involves warehousing, inspection, taxation, and distribution—each step generating economic activity. **Trader Slickscale**'s commercial empire processes roughly forty percent of this traffic, with independent merchants handling the remainder.

Primary imports include grain from the **Eastern Plains**, finished metalwork from **Drakkenheim** and **Stonehold**, textiles from the **Western Reaches**, and luxury goods from **Silverhaven**. Primary exports include **Southern Expanse** tropical products—spices, medicinal herbs, tropical hardwoods, and **Lizardfolk** crafted goods—plus re-exported inland cargo marked up for oceanic distribution. **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** caravans use Rivermouth as their primary **Southern Expanse** depot, connecting sea freight to overland routes reaching the **Eastern Kingdoms** interior.

The city's tax structure borrows heavily from the **Crystal Bay Free Port** model that **Mayor Ironscale** established. Rivermouth charges lower duties than **Crystal Bay** on river-traffic goods and lower duties than **Silverhaven** on oceanic goods, creating a price advantage that keeps transfer traffic flowing through the delta rather than bypassing it. **Mayor Ironscale** and the Rivermouth council maintain a friendly rivalry with **Crystal Bay**'s port authority, each city competing for market share while cooperating on anti-smuggling enforcement through **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s naval coordination.

## Notable Features

The Triple Harbor District is Rivermouth's defining architectural achievement, a three-basin arrangement that separates river traffic, ocean traffic, and military patrols into dedicated channels. The eastern basin accepts **River Aethel** barges and features adjustable quay walls that accommodate the river's seasonal level changes. The western basin handles deep-draft ocean vessels, with breakwaters constructed from **Dragonborn**-engineered volcanic stone imported from **Drakkenheim**. The central basin, smallest of the three, is reserved for **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s anti-piracy patrol vessels and features fortified dockwalls and naval barracks.

**Trader Slickscale**'s Trading House occupies the central island's waterfront, a complex of warehouses, counting houses, and diplomatic chambers that serves as the city's informal embassy to every trading nation in Aethelgard. The Trading House maintains permanent offices for **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** trade representatives, **Merchant Prince Whiskerwind**'s **Catfolk** factors, and **Queen Elara**'s commercial agents. **Trader Slickscale** himself maintains a permanent suite in the complex, though he divides his time between Rivermouth, **Ssithik's Landing**, and **Crystal Bay**.

The Canal District winds through the city's residential neighborhoods, a network of waterways that serves as Rivermouth's primary internal transportation system. **Lizardfolk** pole-boats carry passengers and small cargo through the canals at all hours, while pontoon bridges connect the residential platforms to the central island's commercial district. The canals are tidal—saltwater during incoming tide, brackish during outgoing—and the **Lizardfolk** boatmen who navigate them are famous for their ability to read current patterns that change hourly. **Mayor Ironscale**'s infrastructure program includes a multi-year project to widen the main canals and reinforce the pontoon bridges with **dwarven**-engineered steel pontoons.

The Council Spire rises from the central island's highest point, a five-story timber-and-stone tower that houses the multi-species council chambers, **Mayor Ironscale**'s administrative offices, and the city's legal archives. The Spire's top floor provides panoramic views of both harbors and the **River Aethel** approach, making it functionally useful as a watchtower as well as symbolically important as the seat of civic authority. Signal fires on the Spire communicate with **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s coastal warning network.

## Rulers and Leaders

Rivermouth is governed by a four-member council with equal representation from **Lizardfolk**, **human**, **Dragonborn**, and **Catfolk** constituencies, a structure directly inspired by **Mayor Ironscale**'s **Crystal Bay** model. The council elects a presiding mayor from its membership every four years; the current presiding mayor is the **human** merchant Aldric Vane, a former **Silverhaven** trade commissioner who relocated to Rivermouth in 458 AS. **Trader Slickscale** holds the **Lizardfolk** council seat and exercises disproportionate influence through his commercial network.

**Mayor Ironscale** of **Crystal Bay** maintains a close advisory relationship with Rivermouth's council, sharing governance expertise and trade intelligence through regular correspondence and quarterly visits. The two cities' aligned regulatory frameworks—the **Crystal Bay Free Port** model and Rivermouth's adapted version—create a unified commercial zone along the **Southern Expanse** coast that benefits both economies. **Admiral Hornbreaker** provides military coordination from the Triple Harbor's naval basin, and **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s **Dragonborn** trade representatives ensure that **Drakkenheim** commercial interests receive fair treatment.

## Connected Events

- **Crystal Bay Free Port Declaration** (440 AS): Established the commercial model that Rivermouth adapted for its own free-trade zone regulations
- **Treaty of Silverhaven** (450 AS): Granted Rivermouth official free-trade status and guaranteed **Lizardfolk** territorial rights over the delta
- **Lizardfolk Unification** (430 AS): **Chief Ssithik**'s unification created the political stability necessary for Rivermouth's commercial expansion
- **Minotaur Naval Alliance** (365 AS): **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s anti-piracy patrols made Rivermouth's oceanic trade routes safe for commercial shipping
- **Drakkenheim Trade Accord**: **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s agreement standardized trade regulations between **Dragonborn** markets and Rivermouth's transfer operations
