# Grommashar

## Overview

Grommashar is the capital city of the unified plains orc tribes, founded by **Chieftain Grommash Bloodaxe** in 385 AS as the seat of the Bloodaxe Confederation. Located in the heart of the Eastern Plains, this unique city combines traditional orcish martial culture with innovative trade-focused infrastructure, serving as a symbol of orcish transformation from raiders to respected partners in Aethelgard's multi-species civilization.

## Geography and Climate

Grommashar sits on a strategic rise in the Eastern Plains, approximately 300 miles southwest of **Silverhaven**. The location was chosen for its defensible position, access to water from the Plains River, and central position within orcish tribal territories.

The climate is continental steppe, with hot summers and cold winters. The surrounding plains provide excellent grazing for horses and livestock, central to traditional orcish culture. Seasonal winds make the area ideal for wind-powered mills and grain processing.

## History and Founding

Grommashar was founded in 385 AS by **Chieftain Grommash Bloodaxe** after unifying the twelve plains orc tribes in 380 AS. Before this, orcs lived in nomadic or semi-nomadic settlements, but Grommash envisioned a permanent capital that would demonstrate orcish commitment to peaceful cooperation.

The city's construction used both traditional orcish techniques and knowledge borrowed from **Dwarven** engineers and **Human** architects. **Master Engineer Bofur Deepdelver** consulted on defensive works, while **Human** traders advised on market design.

During the **War of Shattered Skies**, Grommashar served as the staging ground for orcish forces. After **Chieftain Grommash** signed the **Treaty of Silverhaven** (450 AS), the city transformed from military camp to trade hub.

## Population and Culture

Grommashar's population of approximately 40,000 is predominantly orcish with growing minorities of other species:

- **Orcs** (85%): Plains, Mountain, and Forest variants
- **Humans** (8%): Traders, diplomats, and teachers
- **Dwarves** (4%): Engineers and merchants
- **Halflings** (2%): Agricultural advisors and cooks
- **Other Species** (1%): Catfolk traders, Changeling diplomats

The culture is in transition, balancing traditional orcish values of strength and honor with new emphasis on trade, diplomacy, and craftsmanship. Younger orcs embrace change while elders preserve traditions.

## Architecture

Grommashar's architecture reflects orcish transformation:

- **Traditional Elements**: Circular designs, open spaces
- **Practical Construction**: Durable, functional buildings
- **Defensive Features**: Walls and guard towers
- **Market Focus**: Large commercial spaces
- **Symbolic Structures**: Unity Tower, Peace Gardens

The city blends orcish traditional aesthetics with practical trade infrastructure, creating a unique architectural style.

## Defense Systems

Grommashar's defenses combine traditional and modern:

- **City Walls**: Stone fortifications
- **Warrior Population**: Nearly all orcs trained
- **Cavalry Forces**: Renowned horse warriors
- **Allied Support**: **Silverhaven** defense treaty
- **Early Warning**: Scout network across plains

The city has not been attacked since founding, its reputation and alliances providing deterrence.

## Current Challenges

As Grommashar enters the Age of Splendor, it faces several challenges:

- **Traditionalist Resistance**: Some orcs miss raiding culture
- **Economic Transition**: Complete shift to trade ongoing
- **Youth Unrest**: Young orcs seek new identities
- **Succession Stability**: **Grommash II** proving his leadership
- **External Prejudice**: Some still view orcs as threats

---

*Grommashar stands as a monument to orcish transformation, its bustling markets and training grounds proof that a people defined by war can choose peace without losing their soul, that strength can serve creation as well as destruction.*
