# Stonehold

## Overview

Stonehold is the greatest and most ancient of dwarven holds, a sprawling underground city carved into the roots of the Stormpeak Mountains in the Western Reaches. Renowned for its unparalleled metallurgy, masterful stonework, and impenetrable defenses, Stonehold serves as the seat of the High King of the Mountain Holds and the spiritual heart of dwarven civilization across Aethelgard.

## Geography and Climate

Located deep within the Stormpeak Mountains, Stonehold extends from the surface down through multiple levels into the earth's crust. The hold is built around a massive natural cavern system, expanded and reinforced over millennia of dwarven engineering.

The climate is controlled through sophisticated ventilation systems that draw fresh air from mountain peaks and regulate temperature through geothermal vents. The upper levels experience seasonal changes, while deeper levels maintain constant temperature year-round.

Water comes from underground rivers and springs, filtered through stone and enchanted for purity. The famous "Everflow Cisterns" have provided uninterrupted water for over two thousand years.

## History and Founding

Stonehold was founded during "The Delving" in the Age of Dragons (pre-Sundering) by the first dwarven settlers seeking refuge from surface conflicts. The original founders discovered rich veins of iron, gold, and mithril—a legendary metal lighter than steel but stronger than any known alloy.

The hold grew continuously over millennia, each generation adding new levels, chambers, and fortifications. **King Thorin Stoneforge**, the current High King, is the 47th ruler of an unbroken line stretching back to the founding.

During the **War of Shattered Skies**, Stonehold served as a refuge for surface dwarves and provided weapons and armor to allied forces. **Runelord Dain Ironfist** commanded dwarven forces from Stonehold during the conflict.

## Population and Culture

Stonehold's population of approximately 100,000 is almost exclusively dwarven, with small communities of surface traders and rare long-term guests:

- **Mountain Dwarves** (70%): Ruling population, traditional craftsmen
- **Deep Dwarves** (20%): Subterranean specialists, miners
- **Hill Dwarves** (8%): Surface traders and diplomats
- **Other Species** (2%): Merchants, ambassadors, and scholars

The culture is deeply traditional, with clan loyalty and craft excellence paramount. Dwarven time is measured in generations rather than years, and projects spanning centuries are common.

## Economy and Trade

Stonehold's economy is based on mining, metallurgy, and craftsmanship:

### Mining Operations
Extraction of precious materials:
- Iron and steel production
- Gold and silver mining
- Gemstone extraction (rubies, sapphires, diamonds)
- Mithril (limited, closely guarded)
- Salt and other minerals

### Metalworking
Legendary dwarven craftsmanship:
- Weapons and armor (highest quality in Aethelgard)
- Tools and implements
- Decorative metalwork
- Enchanted items (with **Grimjaw Ironhand** runes)
- Structural components for construction

### Trade Relations
- **Silverhaven**: Primary customer for weapons and armor
- **Thronehold**: Royal commissions and military contracts
- **Arcane College of Thaldor**: Magical item collaboration
- **Human kingdoms**: Agricultural products for metalwork
- **Elven territories**: Limited trade in enchanted items

## Architecture

Stonehold's architecture is monumental and functional:

- **Massive Scale**: Accommodates dwarven size and preferences
- **Stone Construction**: Carved from living rock
- **Defensive Design**: Every level defensible
- **Functional Beauty**: Ornamentation serves purpose
- **Vertical City**: Multiple levels connected by ramps and stairs

The Throne Hall's columns are carved from single crystals, and the Great Forge's dome spans 300 feet without support.

## Defense Systems

Stonehold's defenses are legendary:

- **Mountain Position**: Natural fortress
- **Multiple Gates**: Layered entry points
- **Kill Zones**: Engineered defensive positions
- **Deep Roads Control**: Can seal tunnels
- **Dwarven Warriors**: Elite heavy infantry
- **Runewards**: **Grimjaw Ironhand's** magical inscriptions

The hold has never fallen to assault, though it has withstood several sieges throughout history.

## Current Challenges

As Stonehold enters the Age of Splendor, it faces several challenges:

- **Mithril Exhaustion**: Nearly depleted, economic impact
- **Surface Dependency**: Increasing trade reliance
- **Young Generation**: Some dwarves want more surface interaction
- **Deep Threats**: Ancient evils in deepest tunnels
- **Succession Questions**: King Thorin has no direct heir
