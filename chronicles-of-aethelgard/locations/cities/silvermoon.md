# Silvermoon

## Overview

Silvermoon is the ancient capital of the High Elves and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Aethelgard, founded in the Age of Dragons before the Sundering. Renowned for its breathtaking architecture grown from living trees and crystal, Silvermoon serves as the center of elven arcane learning and the seat of the **High Elf Council of Silvermoon**, which has maintained continuous rule for over three thousand years.

## Geography and Climate

Located in the heart of the Western Reaches, Silvermoon sits in a vast ancient forest where the trees have been magically enhanced and shaped over millennia. The city is built around the Moonwell, a sacred pool of pure magical energy that amplifies elven spellcasting and sustains the living architecture.

The climate is temperate with mild seasons, the forest canopy regulating temperature and rainfall. The magical nature of the forest means that seasons change gradually and gently, with none of the harsh extremes found in human lands.

## History and Founding

Silvermoon was founded in the Age of Dragons (pre-Sundering) by High Elf ancestors fleeing draconic conflicts. The original settlers discovered the Moonwell and built their sanctuary around it, using elven bio-magic to shape the forest into a city.

During the **Age of Expansion**, Silvermoon maintained cautious relations with emerging human kingdoms. **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper** of House Moonwhisper has served as ambassador to **Silverhaven** since 420 AS, representing elven interests in human courts.

The city remained largely isolated during the **War of Shattered Skies** (250-450 AS), following the Elven Withdrawal of 250 AS. However, individual elves like **Throndor the Wise** left to participate in broader world affairs, and Silvermoon quietly provided magical support to the peace coalition.

## Population and Culture

Silvermoon's population of approximately 30,000 is almost exclusively High Elf, with small communities of Wood Elves and occasional half-elf visitors:

- **High Elves** (90%): Ruling population, masters of arcane magic
- **Wood Elves** (8%): Serve as forest wardens and messengers
- **Half-Elves** (1%): Diplomats, scholars, and children of mixed unions
- **Other Species** (1%): Ambassadors and rare long-term guests

The culture is contemplative and scholarly, with elves pursuing knowledge and artistic perfection over centuries. Time is measured differently in Silvermoon—a decade is considered a brief period, and projects spanning centuries are common.

## Economy and Trade

Silvermoon's economy is based on magical craftsmanship and knowledge:

### Magical Items
Elven enchantments are highly prized:
- Enchanted weapons and armor
- Magical textiles and clothing
- Crystalline art objects
- Preserved foods with magical properties
- Scrolls and spell components

### Knowledge Trade
Silvermoon sells expertise and education:
- Magical consulting services
- Historical research and records
- Artistic commissions (spanning decades)
- Rare magical ingredients
- Prophecy and divination services

### Limited Commerce
Trade with other species is restricted:
- **Silverhaven** merchants granted limited access
- **Arcane College of Thaldor** exchanges
- **Dwarven** metalwork for elven enchantments
- **Gnomish** technical collaboration
- Most trade conducted through intermediaries

## Rulers and Leaders

### High Elf Council
Ancient ruling body:
- **Lady Celestia Moonwhisper**: Head of Council, Ambassador to Silverhaven
- **House Representatives**: Each noble house has seat
- **Archmage of Silvermoon**: Chief magical advisor
- **Forest Warden Chief**: Security and defense
- **Historian General**: Keeper of records and lore

### Religious Leadership
- **High Priestess of the Moonwell**: Spiritual leader
- **Circle of Elders**: Ancestral worship coordinators
- **Druidic Circle**: Nature magic overseers

The Council has maintained continuous rule for over 3,000 years, providing remarkable stability but also resistance to change.

## Architecture

Silvermoon's architecture is unique in Aethelgard:

- **Living Structures**: Buildings grown from trees and crystal
- **Organic Design**: No straight lines or sharp angles
- **Magical Integration**: Architecture enhances spellcasting
- **Natural Beauty**: Blends seamlessly with forest
- **Long-term Vision**: Structures designed to last millennia

The city appears as a forest from distance, with spires and towers revealed only when approaching closely.

## Defense Systems

Silvermoon's defenses are primarily magical and natural:

- **Forest Wardens**: Elite elven rangers
- **Living Defenses**: Animated plants and trees
- **Magical Wards**: Ancient protective enchantments
- **Moonwell Power**: Amplified defensive magic
- **Isolation**: Difficult to find or approach

The city has never fallen to assault, though its isolationist policy has been its primary defense.

## Current Challenges

As Silvermoon enters the Age of Splendor, it faces several challenges:

- **Isolation vs. Engagement**: Debate over participation in world affairs
- **Young Generation**: Some elves want more interaction
- **Magical Decline**: Moonwell's power slowly diminishing
- **Half-Elf Rights**: Status of mixed-heritage elves
- **External Threats**: Growing powers near elven borders

*Silvermoon stands as a testament to elven endurance and magical mastery, its living towers and ancient wisdom a reminder that some things cannot be rushed, and some beauties cannot be replicated.*
