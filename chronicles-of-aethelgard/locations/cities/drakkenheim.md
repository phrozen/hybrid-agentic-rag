# Drakkenheim

## Overview

Drakkenheim is the mountain capital of the **Dragonborn Clans** in the **Southern Expanse**, a fortified city built into volcanic peaks where **Lady Zara Goldscale** rules. The city's metalworking industry rivals **Stonehold**'s, with **Dragonborn** craftsmen producing distinctive weapons and armor. **Bahamut**'s primary temple stands in Drakkenheim, and the city serves as **Southern Expanse** northern anchor, coordinating with **Crystal Bay** and **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s naval forces.

## Geography and Climate

Drakkenheim occupies a series of volcanic peaks in the **Southern Expanse**'s Dragonspine mountain range, approximately two hundred miles inland from the **Crystal Bay** coastline. The city is carved directly into the igneous rock of three adjacent volcanoes—Mount Ashvein, Mount Cinderhorn, and Mount Sootspire—which last erupted during the **War of Shattered Skies** and are now classified as dormant by **Arcane College of Thaldor** geologists. The surrounding terrain rises steeply from the subtropical lowlands, creating dramatic elevation changes within the city itself: the lower districts sit at roughly sea level while the **Dragonborn Ancestral Halls** perch above the clouds.

The climate combines subtropical heat at the city's base with volcanic warmth throughout the interior chambers. Geothermal vents provide natural heating year-round, making Drakkenheim one of the few settlements in Aethelgard where winter has virtually no effect on daily life. Rainfall is heavy during the wet season, funneling through carved drainage channels into underground cisterns that supply water during dry months. The volcanic soil supports hardy mountain vegetation on the lower slopes, while the upper peaks remain bare black rock streaked with sulfur deposits.

The city's volcanic setting provides both natural fortification and industrial advantage. The forge-districts tap directly into magma chambers, producing heat that dwarves from **Stonehold** have grudgingly admitted surpasses their own coal-fueled foundries. **Lady Zara Goldscale** has invested heavily in infrastructure to harness geothermal energy for residential heating and industrial processing, reducing the city's dependence on imported fuel.

## History and Founding

Drakkenheim's founding predates the **Sundering**, though precise records were lost in the volcanic eruptions that reshaped the Dragonspine Peaks during that cataclysm. Oral tradition holds that the first **Dragonborn** clans arrived in the **Southern Expanse** fleeing draconic conflicts in the far north, and discovered the three volcanoes already venting heat and fire—conditions that felt like home to a species with fire-resistant physiology. The earliest settlements were cave networks in the mountainsides, gradually expanded into the carved stone city that exists today.

The city's modern era began with **Lord Arkhan the Cruel**'s **Dragonborn** Unification around 370 AS, when warring clan factions were consolidated under a single governance structure headquartered in Drakkenheim. Arkhan chose the city specifically because its three-peaked geography allowed each major clan—gold, red, and blue dragon-born—to claim a mountain while sharing the connecting passages below. This architectural compromise became the model for **Dragonborn** political unity: separate strength, shared purpose.

The transition from Arkhan's martial rule to **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s diplomatic leadership after the **Treaty of Silverhaven** reshaped Drakkenheim's external posture while preserving its internal traditions. Goldscale opened the city to foreign merchants, hosted the first inter-species trade delegations, and commissioned the road connecting Drakkenheim to **Rivermouth** that now carries the bulk of **Dragonborn** commercial traffic. The city's population has grown from roughly twenty thousand during Arkhan's era to nearly fifty thousand today, making it the largest **Dragonborn** settlement in Aethelgard.

## Population and Culture

Drakkenheim houses approximately fifty thousand permanent residents, predominantly **Dragonborn** (seventy-five percent), with **humans** (ten percent), **Lizardfolk** (eight percent), and other species comprising the remainder. The population swells during major religious festivals at **Bahamut**'s temple and during the annual metalworking exposition that draws craftsmen from as far as **Stonehold** and **Silverhaven**.

**Dragonborn** culture in Drakkenheim revolves around three interlocking traditions: clan loyalty, martial readiness, and craft mastery. Every adult is expected to maintain proficiency in combat regardless of their primary occupation, and the city's militia can assemble fully armed within an hour of any alarm. Clan affiliation determines residential district, social obligations, and political representation on the ruling council that advises **Lady Zara Goldscale**. The clan system creates fierce internal competition—the annual forging contests between gold, red, and blue dragon-born smiths are legendary—but also ensures that **Dragonborn** unity against external threats is absolute.

The city's spiritual life centers on **Bahamut**'s primary temple, a vast cavern converted into a cathedral of platinum and obsidian. **Dragonborn** clerics maintain eternal flame rites that have burned without interruption for over four centuries, and the temple's healing services draw pilgrims from across the **Southern Expanse**. Temple festivals coincide with volcanic activity on the peaks, which the faithful interpret as **Bahamut**'s breath, and which geologists from the **Arcane College of Thaldor** study as predictable geothermal events.

## Economy and Trade

Drakkenheim's economy rests on three pillars: metalworking, geothermal industry, and trade taxation. The city's forges produce weapons, armor, and tools that rival **Stonehold**'s output, though with a distinctively **Dragonborn** aesthetic—scale-patterned decoration, heat-resistant coatings, and designs optimized for wielders with clawed hands. **Trader Slickscale** operates a permanent warehouse in the lower merchant district, purchasing **Dragonborn** metalwork for distribution through **Rivermouth** and **Crystal Bay**.

Geothermal resources power Drakkenheim's unique industries: glass-blowing using volcanic heat, sulfur extraction for alchemical processing, and obsidian quarrying for blades and decorative work. **Lady Zara Goldscale**'s trade agreements with **King Thorin Stoneforge** exchange **Dragonborn** volcanic glass and sulfur for dwarven iron and steel, a partnership that has enriched both cities since the **Treaty of Silverhaven**.

The city also profits from its position as the **Southern Expanse**'s northern anchor on the trade route connecting **Crystal Bay** to the **Eastern Kingdoms**. Caravans passing through Drakkenheim pay transit fees and purchase provisions, and the city's merchants have developed specialized cargo-handling services for the steep mountain roads. **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s naval coordination extends to Drakkenheim's small fleet of coastal patrol vessels, which protect merchant shipping along the Dragon Coast.

## Notable Features

The **Dragonborn Ancestral Halls** occupy the uppermost chambers of Mount Ashvein, accessible only by carved stairways guarded by clan sentries. The Halls preserve **Dragonborn** history through carved stone tablets, ceremonial weapons, and the preserved eggs of ancient dragons believed to be direct ancestors of modern **Dragonborn** clans. Visiting dignitaries are routinely offered tours of the Halls as a diplomatic courtesy, though certain inner sanctums remain restricted to initiated clan members.

The Forge District spans the caldera floor between Mount Cinderhorn and Mount Sootspire, a vast open-air workspace where geothermal vents provide forge-heat without fuel. The district operates around the clock, with smiths working in shifts that follow volcanic heat cycles rather than daylight. The famous **Dragonborn** pattern-welding technique was developed here, producing blades with distinctive rippled surfaces that hold an edge longer than any other steel in Aethelgard.

**Bahamut**'s temple dominates the central cavern, its platinum doors visible from most of the city's lower districts. The temple's healing services are available to all species regardless of ability to pay, though **Dragonborn** receive priority during emergencies. The temple also maintains a library of draconic lore, a scriptorium producing illuminated manuscripts, and a vestry where young **Dragonborn** train for religious service.

The **Goldscale Council Chamber** sits at the junction of the three mountain passages, a circular room with doors facing each clan district. **Lady Zara Goldscale** holds court here three times per week, hearing petitions from clanspeople and foreign merchants alike. The chamber's design—three doors, one table, no throne—reflects the **Dragonborn** political principle that leadership serves the clans rather than ruling over them.

## Rulers and Leaders

**Lady Zara Goldscale** governs Drakkenheim as diplomatic representative of the **Dragonborn Clans** and chair of the ruling council. Her authority derives from both martial accomplishment and political skill; she has won three formal challenges from traditionalists who questioned her fitness to rule, and each victory strengthened her negotiating position with external powers. She maintains close relationships with **King Thorin Stoneforge**, whose dwarven kingdom provides essential raw materials, **Queen Elara**, whose **Eastern Kingdoms** supply agricultural products that the volcanic terrain cannot produce, and **Admiral Hornbreaker**, whose naval patrols protect **Dragonborn** shipping.

The clan council includes representatives from the gold, red, and blue dragon-born factions, each responsible for their mountain district and its associated industries. Council debates are conducted according to **Dragonborn** honor protocols: direct address, no interruption, and binding oaths on any commitment made during proceedings. The council advises Goldscale on domestic matters while deferring to her judgment on international affairs, a division of authority established during the Unification that has proven remarkably stable.

## Connected Events

- **Dragonborn Unification** (370 AS): **Lord Arkhan the Cruel** consolidated warring clans and established Drakkenheim as capital
- **Treaty of Silverhaven** (450 AS): **Lady Zara Goldscale** signed as **Dragonborn** representative, opening Drakkenheim to international trade
- **Drakkenheim Trade Accord**: Comprehensive trade agreement with **King Thorin Stoneforge** and **Queen Elara** that regulates inter-species commerce through the city
- **Minotaur Naval Alliance** (365 AS): Established naval cooperation between **Admiral Hornbreaker**'s fleet and Drakkenheim's coastal patrol
- **Southern Expanse Maritime Security Agreement**: Anti-piracy pact coordinated between **Lady Zara Goldscale**, **Admiral Hornbreaker**, and **Mayor Ironscale** of **Crystal Bay**
